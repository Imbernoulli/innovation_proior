# Slack Water: Scheduling Ships at a Tidal Anchorage

## Problem

A harbourmaster manages a rectangular anchorage `W x H` metres. Ships may only
cross the harbour mouth -- entering to anchor or leaving -- at one of `K`
scheduled **tide windows** `T_1 < T_2 < ... < T_K` (slack-water moments; the
rest of the time the current is too strong to manoeuvre). `K` is small: the
tide grid splits the day into only `K-1` **epochs**, epoch `j` running from
tide `T_j` to `T_{j+1}`.

Each candidate ship `i` has a swing radius `r_i` (while anchored it drifts
in a full circle of this radius around its anchor point and that circle must
never leave the basin), a value `v_i`, and an availability window
`[arr_i, dep_i]`: it can only enter at a tide `>= arr_i` and must leave at a
tide `<= dep_i`. You choose, for every ship you decide to service, an actual
entry tide and exit tide (both from the `K`-tide grid, entry strictly before
exit) and a fixed anchor point it holds for its whole stay. Two ships that
are both anchored during any shared epoch must keep their swing circles
disjoint; ships whose stays never share an epoch may occupy the very same
spot at different times.

## Input

```
W H K
T_1 T_2 ... T_K
N
r_1 v_1 arr_1 dep_1
...
r_N v_N arr_N dep_N
```
All values are integers. `T` is strictly increasing. `0 <= arr_i < dep_i <= T_K`.

## Output

```
M
i_1 x_1 y_1 a_1 b_1
...
i_M x_M y_M a_M b_M
```
`M` ships accepted, then for each: its 1-indexed input position `i`, its
anchor point `(x_i, y_i)`, and its entry/exit tide **indices** `a_i, b_i`
(`1 <= a_i < b_i <= K`), meaning it enters at `T_{a_i}` and leaves at
`T_{b_i}` (occupying epochs `a_i .. b_i - 1`).

## Feasibility

Each ship index appears at most once. For every accepted ship: `T_{a_i} >=
arr_i` and `T_{b_i} <= dep_i`; its circle stays in the basin
(`r_i <= x_i <= W - r_i`, `r_i <= y_i <= H - r_i`); and for every OTHER
accepted ship `j` whose epoch range `[a_j, b_j)` shares an epoch with `i`'s
(`max(a_i,a_j) < min(b_i,b_j)`), the two circles do not overlap
(`dist((x_i,y_i),(x_j,y_j)) >= r_i + r_j`). Any violation scores `0` for the
whole test.

## Objective

`F` = sum of `v_i` over accepted ships. The checker also computes an
internal reference `B`: the largest `v_i` among ships that are feasible to
anchor completely alone. Your ratio for a test is `min(1000, 100*F/B) /
1000`; per-test ratios are averaged. `B` corresponds to ratio `0.1`; genuine
extra value scores higher, capped at `1.0`.

## Constraints

* `3 <= K <= 9`, `10 <= W, H <= 400`, `1 <= N <= 500`.
* `1 <= r_i <= floor(min(W,H)/2)` is not guaranteed -- some ships may have a
  radius too large ever to fit; such ships must simply be rejected.
* `1 <= v_i <= 1000`.
* Time limit: 4 s. Memory limit: 512 MB.

## Example

`W=H=10`, `K=3`, tides `[0, 5, 10]` (epoch 1 = `[0,5]`, epoch 2 = `[5,10]`).
Ship 1: `r=2, v=30, arr=0, dep=10` (available the whole day). Ship 2:
`r=4, v=100, arr=4, dep=10` (can only use epoch 2 -- entry tide `T_2=5`,
exit `T_3=10`).

Reading ship 1's window literally and anchoring it for its whole *allowed*
span (`a=1,b=3`, both epochs) at the basin centre `(5,5)` looks safe -- but
ship 2's feasible anchor points all lie within `[4,6]x[4,6]`, at most `1.4`
from `(5,5)`, far short of the `r_1+r_2=6` clearance ship 2 would then need.
Ship 2 is shut out: `F=30`, ratio `0.03`.

Anchoring ship 1 only for the epoch it actually needs (`a=1,b=2`) instead
frees epoch 2 entirely -- the two ships' stays no longer share an epoch, so
no clearance is required between them at all, and both fit: `F=130`, ratio
`0.13`. The saving scales up sharply once many cheap, flexible ships would
otherwise squat, full-span, on the one spot a rare high-value ship needs.
