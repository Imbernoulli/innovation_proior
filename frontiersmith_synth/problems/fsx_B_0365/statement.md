# Sump Return-Line Flow Law

## Problem

You are commissioning a reef-tank **sump plumbing** rig. A return pump pushes
water up from the sump through the return line back into the display tank. The
delivered **return flow** `y` (turnover in tank-volumes/hour, normalised)
depends on four plumbing readings you can tune:

- `x1` — return-pump throttle setting
- `x2` — static head (vertical lift from sump to tank)
- `x3` — return-pipe bore (inner width)
- `x4` — fitting-restriction index (elbows / valves in the run)

A hidden physical law `y = f(x1, x2, x3, x4)` governs the rig. You are given a
noisy **training log** of trims sampled inside the safe operating core
(`x_i` in `[0, 1]`). Your job is to **discover a closed-form expression** for
`f` that will still predict well when the rig is pushed into the **over-range
frontier** (higher head, larger bore) that you never sampled.

The grader regenerates a held-out **extrapolation** split (with `x_i` in
`[1, 1.4]`) from the same hidden law and scores your expression on it, with a
penalty for expression complexity. Memorising the training points does not
help — you must recover the *shape* of the law.

## Input (stdin)

The first line is `n_train test_id`. Each of the next `n_train` lines is one
training sample:

```
x1 x2 x3 x4 y
```

All values are decimal floats. `x_i` in `[0, 1]`; `y` carries measurement noise.

## Output (stdout)

A **single line**: a closed-form Python expression in the variables
`x1, x2, x3, x4`. Allowed operators: `+ - * / ** %` and unary `-`. Allowed
function calls: `exp, log, sin, cos, sqrt, tanh, abs`. No other names, no
imports, no attributes, no keyword arguments.

Example of the required OUTPUT FORM (this is an **illustrative form only — NOT
the hidden law**, its shape is unrelated to `f`):

```
0.3 + 1.2 * sin(x1) - 0.5 * x4 / (1 + x2)
```

## Feasibility

The expression must parse under the whitelist, use only the allowed
names/operators, and evaluate to a **finite real number** at every held-out
point. Any `nan`/`inf`, disallowed name, multi-line output, or empty output
scores `0`.

## Objective

Minimise the complexity-penalised held-out mean squared error
`F = heldout_MSE * (1 + LAMBDA * complexity)`, where `LAMBDA = 0.003` and
`complexity` counts expression nodes. Lower `F` is better.

## Scoring

Let `B = baseline_MSE * (1 + LAMBDA * 1)` where `baseline_MSE` is the held-out
error of the constant equal to the training mean (the grader's internal
baseline). The reported score is

```
Ratio = min(1000, 100 * B / F) / 1000
```

A constant reproduces the baseline (`Ratio ≈ 0.1`). Recovering the hidden law
drives `heldout_MSE` down and raises the ratio, but the irreducible gauge noise
on the frontier and the coarse recovery of the hidden exponential decay rate
keep even a strong solution well below `1.0`.

## Constraints

- `n_train` up to ~400; held-out split has 400 points (regenerated by grader).
- Expression output at most 200000 bytes, a single line.
- Deterministic scoring: the hidden law, train and held-out splits are all
  seeded from `test_id`.

## Example (worked score)

Suppose on some test the held-out constant baseline has `baseline_MSE = 4.0`
and your expression achieves `heldout_MSE = 1.0` with `complexity = 20`. Then
`B = 4.0 * (1 + 0.003) = 4.012`, `F = 1.0 * (1 + 0.003*20) = 1.06`, and
`Ratio = min(1000, 100*4.012/1.06)/1000 = 0.3785`.
