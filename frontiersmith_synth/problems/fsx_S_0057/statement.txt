# Powder Peak Lift Radio Retuning

## Problem
Powder Peak ski resort runs `N` chairlifts. Every lift has a base-station radio that
must be tuned to exactly one of `K` available walkie-talkie **channels**, numbered
`1..K` (the resort's licensed channel budget).

Because lift stations sit close together across the mountain, some pairs of lifts
**interfere** on the airwaves. For an interfering pair the resort has measured an
interference weight `w` (how bad a collision would be) and a required channel
**separation** `s`: the two lifts should ideally be tuned at least `s` channels apart
on the dial. When they are closer than that, radio bleed causes garbled crackle.

Concretely, if the two lifts of an interfering pair are tuned to channels `a` and `b`,
the crackle penalty contributed by that pair is

    penalty = w * max(0, s - |a - b|)

So a pair on the *same* channel pays the full `w * s`; every extra channel of spacing
removes `w` of penalty until the pair is at least `s` apart (then it pays 0).

Tune every lift to a channel so that the **total crackle penalty over all interfering
pairs is as small as possible**. With more interference than the channel budget can
absorb you cannot always reach zero, so you are minimizing residual crackle.

## Input
```
N M K
u_1 v_1 w_1 s_1
u_2 v_2 w_2 s_2
...
u_M v_M w_M s_M
```
- Line 1: `N` lifts, `M` interfering pairs, `K` channels.
- Each of the next `M` lines: an interfering pair `(u, v)` with `1 <= u,v <= N`,
  `u != v`, interference weight `w` (`1 <= w <= 100`) and separation requirement
  `s` (`1 <= s <= K`). Pairs may repeat; each listed pair contributes independently.

## Output
Print `N` integers `c_1 c_2 ... c_N`, whitespace-separated, where `c_i` in `1..K` is
the channel assigned to lift `i`.

## Feasibility
Every `c_i` must be an integer in `[1, K]`. Exactly `N` values must be printed and
nothing else. Any missing, extra, or out-of-range value makes the output infeasible
(scored 0).

## Objective (minimize)
```
F = sum over interfering pairs of  w * max(0, s - |c_u - c_v|)
```

## Scoring
The checker builds the do-nothing baseline "tune every lift to channel 1", whose
penalty is `B = sum over pairs of w * s` (all pairs fully collided). Your score is

    ratio = min(1000, 100 * B / max(1, F)) / 1000

- Tuning everything to one channel reproduces the baseline: `F = B`, ratio `0.1`.
- Halving the crackle relative to baseline roughly doubles the ratio.
- Driving `F` to `B/10` or below caps the ratio at `1.0`.
Higher is better. Each test contributes its ratio; the reported score is the average.

## Constraints
- `3 <= N <= 2000`, `1 <= M <= 20000`, `3 <= K <= 20`.
- Time limit: 5 s. Memory limit: 512 MB.

## Example
Input:
```
3 3 2
1 2 10 2
2 3 10 2
1 3 10 2
```
Three lifts form a triangle of interference, each pair with weight 10 and desired
separation 2, but only `K = 2` channels exist (so the largest possible spacing is 1).

Baseline (all channel 1): every pair pays `10 * (2 - 0) = 20`, so `B = 60`.

A better tuning `c = (1, 2, 1)`:
- pair (1,2): `|1-2| = 1`, penalty `10 * (2 - 1) = 10`
- pair (2,3): `|2-1| = 1`, penalty `10`
- pair (1,3): `|1-1| = 0`, penalty `10 * 2 = 20`
- `F = 40`

Score `= min(1000, 100 * 60 / 40) / 1000 = 150 / 1000 = 0.15`. With only two channels
one pair must always share a channel, so `F = 40` is optimal here.
