# Silencing a War-Drum Rehearsal Hall

## Problem

A war-drum rehearsal hall is a rectangular room `W x H`. A drum sits at cell
`(sx, sy)` and pulses at a fixed period, driving the room into a set of
standing-wave resonance patterns ("channels"). You are hired to install
absorber panels on the wall ring to protect the listener seats.

The room's acoustics have already been analyzed into `NM` channels (indices
`0..NM-1`; the last one is special, see below). Channel `k` is described by
four numbers:

* `Delta_k` -- how far the drum's driving frequency is from that channel's
  natural resonance. Small `Delta_k` means a dangerously resonant channel.
* `Gamma0_k` -- the channel's baseline damping from the untreated walls.
* `S_k` -- how strongly the drum excites this channel (the channel's
  standing-wave amplitude *at the drum*).
* `L_k` -- how strongly this channel is heard at the listener seats (its
  amplitude *at the listeners*, already combined in energy over every seat).

The last channel (`k = NM-1`) is the direct sound plus its first specular wall
reflection (the mirror-image construction): no detuning, the drum always
couples fully, and only a handful of panels -- the true geometric reflection
points for the drum/listener pair -- can damp it at all.

Install absorber panels on any subset of `M` candidate wall cells (their
`(x, y)` position is given for your own geometric reasoning). Panel `i` costs
`cost_i` and has absorption strength `alpha_i` (0-1000, parts per thousand).
Panel `i`'s effect on channel `k` is given directly as `phi2[i][k]`: **the
square of channel `k`'s standing-wave amplitude at panel `i`'s position.**
That is the whole mechanism: a panel damps a channel in proportion to how
loud that channel already is *at the panel* -- not at the listener. Cost and
strength are independent of position, so a panel's distance to the listener
tells you nothing; only the `phi2` table does.

## Input

```
W H
sx sy
NL
lx_1 ly_1
...
lx_NL ly_NL
NM
Delta_0 Gamma0_0 S_0 L_0
...
Delta_{NM-1} Gamma0_{NM-1} S_{NM-1} L_{NM-1}
Budget
M
id_1 x_1 y_1 cost_1 alpha_1 phi2[1][0] ... phi2[1][NM-1]
...
id_M x_M y_M cost_M alpha_M phi2[M][0] ... phi2[M][NM-1]
```

## Output

Print `K` (number of panels installed), then `K` distinct panel ids from
`1..M` (in any order).

## Feasibility

All `K` ids must be distinct and in `[1, M]`; the sum of their `cost_i` must
not exceed `Budget`. Any violation scores 0. Installing nothing (`K = 0`) is
feasible.

## Objective

For each channel `k`, your extra damping is
`Gamma_extra(k) = sum over installed panels i of alpha_i * phi2[i][k] / 1000`
(integer division). Total damping is `Gamma(k) = Gamma0_k + Gamma_extra(k)`.
Channel `k`'s leaked energy is the Lorentzian
`E(k) = S_k^2 * 1e6 / max(1, Delta_k^2 + Gamma(k)^2)`.
**Minimize** `F = sum_k E(k) * L_k` -- the total noise reaching the listeners.

## Scoring

Let `B` be `F` computed with every `Gamma_extra(k) = 0` (installing nothing --
the trivial reference). Your score on a test is
`ratio = min(1000, 100 * B / max(1, F)) / 1000`, so doing nothing scores
`0.1`, and any real reduction of `F` scores higher (capped at `1.0`).
Per-test ratios are averaged.

## Constraints

* `8 <= W, H <= 40`; `1 <= NL <= 6`; `NM <= 200`; `1 <= M <= 200`.
* `1 <= cost_i <= 10`; `0 <= alpha_i <= 1000`; `0 <= phi2 <= 1e6`.
* Time limit: 5 s. Memory limit: 512 MB.

## Example

A small room has one dominant channel with a tiny `Delta` (near-perfect
resonance) whose `S` is huge because the drum sits at that channel's
antinode, near a corner. The listener sits at the room's center, an *exact
node* of that channel (`phi2` is 0 there for nearby wall cells). Spending the
whole budget on panels adjacent to the listener barely changes
`Gamma_extra` for the dominant channel -- `F` barely moves. Spending it on
the few panels near the drum's corner, where that channel's `phi2` is
largest, collapses the denominator and cuts `F` sharply, even though those
panels are the farthest thing from where the noise is measured.
