# Charging Depots for the Bridge City

## Problem

A city is a connected graph of `n` hubs and `m` two-way roads (each one road
segment long). Each hub `v` has a population `p_v`. The city is built from
several **dense blocks** (hubs richly interconnected, many alternate routes
between any two of them) joined together by **thin bridges** -- long single
lanes of hubs with no alternate route at all.

You must choose exactly `k` hubs as EV charging depots. A resident at hub `v`
who needs to charge performs a **simple random walk**: starting at `v`, at
every step they move to a uniformly random road out of their current hub,
and they stop the instant they first arrive at ANY depot. Their expected
number of road-crossings is `v`'s **access time** `a(v)`.

Formally, `a(v)` is the unique solution of:
```
a(v) = 0                                              if v is a depot
a(v) = 1 + (1 / deg(v)) * sum over roads (v,u) of a(u)  otherwise
```
where `deg(v)` is the number of roads touching `v`. This is governed by the
city's diffusion/resistance geometry, not by shortest-path hop count: a hub
buried inside a dense block can have a tiny access time even if its shortest
hop-path to a depot is long, because a random walker there has many
alternate routes and rarely wanders far before stumbling onto a way in.
A hub in the middle of a long thin bridge, by contrast, can have a huge
access time even at a modest hop distance, because a walker stuck on a
single lane with no alternate route tends to wander back and forth for a
long time before reaching either end.

## Input
```
n m k
p_1 p_2 ... p_n
u_1 v_1
...
u_m v_m
```
`1 <= n <= 450`, `1 <= k < n`, edges 1-indexed, undirected, no self-loops or
duplicate roads, graph connected. `1 <= p_v <= 1000000`.

## Output
`k` distinct integers in `[1, n]` -- the chosen depot hubs (any order,
whitespace-separated).

## Feasibility
Exactly `k` tokens, each an integer in `[1, n]`, all distinct. No trailing
tokens. Any violation scores `0`.

## Objective
Minimize the population-weighted average access time:
```
F = ( sum_v p_v * a(v) ) / ( sum_v p_v )
```

## Scoring
The checker solves the linear system above **exactly** for your chosen depot
set to get `F`, and also computes `B`, the same objective for a fixed
structure-oblivious reference placement (depots evenly spaced by raw hub
number). Your ratio is
```
ratio = min(1000, 100 * B / max(1e-9, F)) / 1000        (in [0, 1])
```
so matching the reference scores `~0.1`, and beating it scores higher (score
saturates only far above the reference).

## Constraints
Time limit: 4 seconds. Memory limit: 512 MB.

## Example
```
10 12 1
10 10 10 10 2 2 2 10 10 10
1 2
2 3
3 4
1 3
2 4
4 5
5 6
6 7
7 8
8 9
9 10
8 10
```
Hubs 1-4 form a dense block (population 10 each), hubs 8-10 form a second
dense block (population 10 each), and hubs 5-7 are a thin bridge of
population-2 hubs between them. With `k=1`, output `4` (a member of the
larger-population block, adjacent to the bridge):
```
4
```
The checker computes `F = 19.868421`, and the reference placement (depot at
hub `1`, evenly spaced by id for `k=1`) gives `B = 32.552632`, so
`ratio = min(1000, 100*32.552632/19.868421)/1000 = 0.163841`. Placing the
depot at hub `1` instead reproduces the reference exactly (`ratio = 0.1`).
