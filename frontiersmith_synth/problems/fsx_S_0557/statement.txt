# Ignite the Idea: Clustered Seeding in a Threshold Network

## Problem

An idea spreads through a community by **threshold contagion**: a person adopts the idea
once at least `r` of their direct contacts have already adopted it. You may hand the idea
directly to a limited number of **seed** people. A single enthusiastic contact is never
enough, so the idea only catches fire where you plant it *densely enough to form a
self-sustaining core* — scattering seeds across popular-but-loosely-connected people
fizzles out, while igniting one tight pocket can cascade through it and spill into its
neighbours. Choose your seeds to maximize how many people ultimately adopt the idea.

Formally you are given an undirected graph on `n` people, an integer threshold `r`, and a
seed budget `k`. Pick a set `S` of at most `k` distinct nodes (the initial adopters).
Then repeatedly apply the rule: any non-adopter having at least `r` adopting neighbours
becomes an adopter. This bootstrap-percolation process runs to its fixed point (the
outcome does not depend on the order of activations). Maximize the final adopter count.

Because a node needs `r` *simultaneously* active neighbours, spreading has to *nucleate*
a locally over-threshold cluster (a seeded `r`-core) before it can grow. Deciding how to
divide a fixed seed budget among the clusters worth igniting — and in what order, since
igniting one pocket can lower the cost of the next — is the heart of the problem.

## Input

```
n m r k
```

then `m` lines, each `u v`, an undirected edge between people `u` and `v`
(`1`-indexed, `u != v`, no repeated edge). The graph may be listed in any order.

## Output

First line: `s`, the number of seeds you use (`0 <= s <= k`).
Each of the next `s` lines: one node index in `1..n`. All `s` indices must be distinct.
Printing `0` alone (no further lines) is always valid.

## Feasibility

An output is feasible iff `0 <= s <= k`, every seed is in `1..n`, and all seeds are
distinct. Any infeasible or malformed output scores `0`.

## Objective

Run threshold percolation from your seed set `S` to its fixed point. Let `F` be the total
number of adopters (seeds included). You are **maximizing** `F`.

## Scoring

Let `B` be the adopter count of the reference **scatter** baseline: seed the `k` people of
smallest degree, breaking ties by smaller index, and percolate. The score for a test is

```
ratio = min(1.0, 0.1 * F / B)
```

The scatter baseline scores `0.1`; reaching ten times its reach caps the test at `1.0`.
The overall score is the mean `ratio` across the 10 tests.

## Constraints

- `2 <= n <= 3000`, `1 <= m <= 12000`, `r = 2`, `1 <= k <= n`.
- Time limit 4 s, memory 512 MB. Each input file is at most 5 MB.

## Example

```
9 7 2 2
1 2
1 3
2 3
4 5
4 8
7 6
7 9
```

Here `r = 2`, budget `k = 2`. People `1,2,3` form a triangle; person `4` links the leaves
`5,8` and person `7` links the leaves `6,9`. The scatter baseline seeds the two
smallest-degree nodes `{5,6}` (degree 1); node `5`'s only contact `4` and node `6`'s only
contact `7` each see just one adopter `(< r)`, so nothing spreads and `B = 2`. Instead
seeding the pocket `{1,2}` makes node `3` (contacts `1,2`, both adopters) adopt, giving
`F = 3` and `ratio = min(1.0, 0.1 * 3 / 2) = 0.15`.
