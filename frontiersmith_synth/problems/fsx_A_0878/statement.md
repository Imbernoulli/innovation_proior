# Loading Dock Phase Shift: Calibrated Eviction

A warehouse loading dock keeps a staging shelf that holds exactly `C` pallets.
A stream of pull requests arrives, one pallet ID at a time. If the requested
pallet is on the shelf, that's a **hit**. Otherwise it's a **miss**: the pallet
is fetched, and if the shelf is already full some resident pallet must be
**evicted** to make room. Your job is to design the eviction rule that
minimizes total misses over the shift.

A floor runner radios a **forecast** alongside every request: their guess of
how many requests until *this* pallet is needed again. The runner is sometimes
sharp (forecasts closely track the truth), sometimes just guessing (forecasts
are unrelated to the truth), and occasionally flat-out backwards (forecasts
point at exactly the wrong pallets) -- and which regime is active can flip
partway through the shift without warning, exactly when the mix of pallets
being pulled changes too. Neither ignoring the runner nor trusting them
blindly wins over a whole shift; the win is estimating the runner's current
trustworthiness from how their recent forecasts panned out, and re-estimating
it whenever the miss rate spikes (a sign the regime just changed).

## Candidate program contract

Standalone program: read ONE JSON object from **stdin**, write ONE JSON
**policy** to **stdout**. It runs once per instance, isolated, and sees only a
**calibration shift** -- a full pallet + forecast trace generated by the same
recipe as the real shift but an independent draw. You never see the real
shift; your program only submits a *policy* which the judge then replays,
one request at a time, against the real (hidden) trace.

### Public instance (stdin)

```json
{
  "name": "dock101", "capacity": 8, "n_pages": 26,
  "calib": {
    "n": 250,
    "page": [3, 11, 3, 7, ...],     // pallet ID requested at each calibration step
    "hint":  [4, 0, 9, 2, ...]      // runner's forecast at that same step
  }
}
```

### Answer (stdout) -- a policy, not a decision list

```json
{"mode": "blend", "init_trust": 0.5, "decay": 0.15,
 "window": 10, "edge_threshold": 0.5, "reset_on_edge": true}
```

- `mode`: `"lru"` (ignore the forecast), `"hint"` (always trust it), or
  `"blend"` (adaptive).
- For `"blend"`: `init_trust` in `[0,1]` (starting trust); `decay` in `[0,1]`
  (EWMA step for trust updates); `window` an integer in `[2,50]` (miss-rate
  lookback used to flag a regime change); `edge_threshold` in `[0,1]` (the
  miss rate over the window that counts as a flag); `reset_on_edge` a bool
  (re-anchor trust to `init_trust` when a flag fires).
- Any other shape, an out-of-range field, or a non-finite number is invalid.

## How the judge replays your policy

For each request `t` on the real trace: check hit/miss, then (if a miss and
the shelf is full) score every resident pallet `p` by
`trust * hint_rank(p) + (1-trust) * recency_rank(p)` (each rank in `[0,1]`,
normalized among resident pallets; higher = safer to evict) and evict the
argmax. `hint_rank` uses the forecast's *remaining* predicted gap (recorded
value minus elapsed time since); `recency_rank` is standard LRU order.
`trust` is fixed at 0 (`"lru"`) or 1 (`"hint"`), or -- for `"blend"` --
tracked online by verifying the forecast itself, independent of what your
policy chose to evict: whenever a pallet is requested again, the judge
compares the gap that actually elapsed since its previous request against
the forecast recorded at that previous request. If they're close (within
`max(3, 0.4 * actual_gap)`) the forecast was vindicated and trust grows
toward 1; otherwise it decays toward 0. (This is why trust tracks the
runner's real accuracy rather than just "did my last eviction happen to work
out", which would be true most of the time regardless of the forecast.) If
the rolling miss rate over the last `window` requests reaches
`edge_threshold` and `reset_on_edge` is set, trust snaps back to
`init_trust`.

## Scoring (deterministic)

Per instance the judge computes, itself, on the real trace:

- `m_base` = misses under always-`"lru"` (weak baseline),
- `m_opt`  = misses under the exact clairvoyant-optimal policy (true future
  lookahead -- an unreachable ideal your forecast-only policy cannot match),
- `m_cand` = misses under **your** replayed policy,

```
r = clamp( 0.1 + 0.9 * (m_base - m_cand) / max(1, m_base - m_opt), 0, 1 )
```

Matching always-LRU scores exactly `0.1`; reaching the clairvoyant ideal
scores `1.0` (essentially unreachable from a noisy forecast); doing worse
than LRU scores below `0.1`. The reported **Ratio** is the mean of `r` over
10 fixed instances (some with a reliable runner, some noisy, some backwards,
some flipping mid-shift, plus larger held-out cases); **Vector** lists the
per-instance scores.
