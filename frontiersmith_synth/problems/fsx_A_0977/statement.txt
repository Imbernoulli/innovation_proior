# Retensioning the Ground-Braced Mast Array

## Problem

A 1-D tensegrity array has `n` nodes, positioned along a line at fixed **target**
coordinates `target[0..n-1]`. Node `0` is the ground anchor, permanently at
position `0`. Every other node is one of two kinds:

* **Strut node**: rigidly welded to some earlier node by a fixed signed offset
  `d` (`position[v] - position[u] = d`, always, non-negotiable). A node
  connected to the ground through a chain of struts is **grounded**: its
  position is forever exactly `target[node]`.
* **Free node**: not welded to anything. Instead it is held by two or more
  **cables** — tension-only springs, each running to a grounded node, with a
  given stiffness `k` and a rest length **you choose**. A free node's actual
  position is wherever its incident cables balance: the unique `x` minimizing
  `e(x) = sum over its cables of 0.5 * k * max(0, |x - anchor_pos| - rest)^2`
  (a cable pulls once stretched taut past its rest length; it goes slack —
  zero force — once compressed to or below rest length).

You are given the full network (struts, cables, every cable's stiffness) and
must output a rest length for every cable. The network is then released and
settles to its physical equilibrium. You are scored on how close every free
node's equilibrium lands to its target, on how little unnecessary tension
your prestress carries, and on whether the target is a **stable** equilibrium
at all — pulling a free node from only one side lets it be shoved off target
for free the instant it's disturbed; it needs a taut cable resisting BOTH
directions.

## Input

```
n s c
target_0 target_1 ... target_{n-1}
```
then `s` lines (struts): `u v d` meaning `position[v] - position[u] = d`.
Then `c` lines (cables): `u v k` meaning a spring of stiffness `k` between `u`
and `v`; you supply its rest length.

## Output

Print `c` non-negative real numbers, the rest length of cable `i` (input
order), one per line.

## Feasibility

Every rest length must be a finite real in `[0, 100000]`. Any violation (out
of range, non-finite, wrong count, trailing tokens) scores `0`.

## Objective

Minimize
```
F = sum over free nodes (equilibrium_x - target)^2
  + 0.05 * sum over cables k * max(0, |equilibrium_gap| - rest)
  + 2.5  * (# free nodes lacking a taut cable pulling from BOTH sides)
```
The first term is shape error. The second is the actual tension every cable
carries at equilibrium (unnecessary prestress is charged). The third punishes
a free node that isn't genuinely braced from both directions — even if it
sits at its target, it isn't a *stable* fixed point. A cable counts as "taut"
once its force exceeds `0.03`.

## Scoring

Let `B` be the objective of the checker's own reference: every cable's rest
length set to exactly the target-shape distance between its two endpoints (so
every cable is unstretched — zero tension — at the target). Your score for a
test case is
```
ratio = min(1000, 100 * B / max(1e-6, F)) / 1000
```
so that reference scores `0.1`; a real improvement scores higher, capped at
`1.0`. Per-test ratios are averaged over 10 cases.

## Constraints

* `3 <= n <= 120`, node `0` is the anchor.
* Every strut's `d` is a nonzero integer; every cable's `k` is an integer in
  `[1, 20]`.
* Every free node has at least one cable to a grounded node on each side
  (smaller and larger target position).
* Time limit: 4 s. Memory limit: 512 MB.

## Example

`n=3, s=1, c=2`. Targets `0 8 20`. Strut `0 2 20` (node 2 rigidly at `20`).
Cables `1 0 10` and `1 2 10` (node 1 is free, pulled by two stiffness-10
cables to the grounded nodes 0 and 20; its target is `8`, so the gaps are `8`
and `12`).

Output `7.95` then `11.95`: both cables carry the same tension (`0.5`), so
node 1's forces cancel exactly at `x=8` — the target — and both stay taut, so
it's a stable brace. `B` (rest = `8` and `12` exactly) leaves both cables at
zero tension there: unstable, penalized, scoring only `0.1`; this construction
scores far higher.
