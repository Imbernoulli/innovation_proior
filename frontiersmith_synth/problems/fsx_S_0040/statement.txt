# Signal-Grid Courier: Prize-Collecting Pickup-and-Delivery Tour

## Problem

A single autonomous courier van serves a downtown laid out as a **traffic-signal grid**.
There are `N` signalized intersections. The van is garaged at a **depot** intersection and
must return there at the end of its shift. A dispatch board lists `M` delivery **jobs**;
job `j` has a **pickup** intersection `p_j` and a **delivery** intersection `q_j`. To
complete a job the van must visit `p_j` (collect the parcel) and *later* visit `q_j` (drop
it off). The van has unlimited cargo space, so it may hold several collected parcels at
once. For every job the van **does not** complete, the company forfeits a lost-revenue
penalty `w_j`.

Driving between intersections costs travel distance plus the red-light wait at the
intersection you arrive at. Concretely, moving from intersection `a` to intersection `b`
costs

```
dist(a, b) = |x_a - x_b| + |y_a - y_b| + delay_b
```

where `(x_i, y_i)` are the grid coordinates of intersection `i` and `delay_i` is its signal
wait. Every arrival — including the final return to the depot — pays the destination's
signal delay; the initial departure from the depot pays nothing extra.

You choose **which subset of jobs to serve** and **one depot-to-depot tour** that respects
every served job's pickup-before-delivery precedence, so as to minimize the total cost:
the driven tour length plus the penalties of all unserved jobs.

This is a prize-collecting pickup-and-delivery routing problem: it is NP-hard, the optimum
is unknown, and construction heuristics (append-greedy, cheapest-insertion, local search,
randomized restarts) all diverge.

## Input

The first line contains three integers:

```
N M depot
```

- `N` — number of intersections, numbered `1..N`.
- `M` — number of jobs, numbered `1..M`.
- `depot` — the depot intersection (`1 <= depot <= N`).

Each of the next `N` lines describes one intersection `i` (`i = 1..N`):

```
x_i y_i delay_i
```

with grid coordinates `x_i, y_i` and signal wait `delay_i >= 0`.

Each of the next `M` lines describes one job `j` (`j = 1..M`):

```
p_j q_j w_j
```

meaning pickup intersection `p_j`, delivery intersection `q_j` (`p_j != q_j`), and
lost-revenue penalty `w_j >= 1` if the job is left unserved.

## Output

Describe your tour as an ordered list of **stop events**. First print one line with `K`,
the number of events. Then print `K` lines, each

```
type j
```

where `type` is `1` for a pickup (visit `p_j`) or `2` for a delivery (visit `q_j`), and
`j` is the job index. The van drives, in the printed order, from the depot to each event's
intersection and finally back to the depot.

Printing `0` on the first line (serve nothing, van stays home) is always valid.

## Feasibility

An output is feasible iff:

- `0 <= K <= 2*M`;
- every `type` is `1` or `2` and every `j` is in `1..M`;
- no job has two pickup events and no job has two delivery events;
- for every job, its pickup event (if any) appears **before** its delivery event;
- every job that is picked up is also delivered, and vice versa (a job is either fully
  served — one pickup then one delivery — or not served at all).

Any output violating these rules scores `0`.

## Objective

Let the served jobs be those that appear (as a pickup+delivery pair). Let `T` be the total
driven cost of the tour `depot -> (event locations in order) -> depot`, where each leg is
charged by `dist(a, b)` above; if no jobs are served, `T = 0`. Let `U` be the sum of `w_j`
over all **unserved** jobs. You are **minimizing**

```
F = T + U
```

## Scoring

Let `B` be the cost of the do-nothing plan (serve no job): `B = sum of all w_j`. The score
for a test is

```
ratio = min(1.0, 0.1 * B / F)
```

The do-nothing plan (`F = B`) scores `0.1`; driving the total cost down to one-tenth of the
forfeit-everything cost (`F = B/10`) caps the score at `1.0`. The overall score is the mean
ratio across all tests.

## Constraints

- `2 <= N <= 40`, `1 <= M <= 40`.
- `0 <= x_i, y_i <= 40`, `0 <= delay_i <= 3`.
- `1 <= w_j <= 5000`, `p_j != q_j`.
- Time limit: 5 s. Memory limit: 512 MB.

## Example

Input:

```
5 3 1
5 5 0
0 5 1
10 5 1
5 0 2
5 10 2
2 3 30
4 5 25
2 4 40
```

Job 1: pickup at intersection 2 `(0,5)`, deliver at 3 `(10,5)`, penalty 30.
Job 2: pickup at 4 `(5,0)`, deliver at 5 `(5,10)`, penalty 25.
Job 3: pickup at 2 `(0,5)`, deliver at 4 `(5,0)`, penalty 40.

Do-nothing baseline: `B = 30 + 25 + 40 = 95`.

Consider serving job 1 with the tour `depot(1) -> pickup 2 -> deliver 3 -> depot`:

```
2
1 1
2 1
```

Legs: `dist(1,2)=|5-0|+|5-5|+1=6`, `dist(2,3)=|0-10|+0+1=11`,
`dist(3,1)=|10-5|+0+0=5`. So `T = 6+11+5 = 22`. Unserved penalties `U = 25+40 = 65`.
Thus `F = 22 + 65 = 87` and `ratio = min(1.0, 0.1 * 95 / 87) = 0.1092...`.

Serving more of the profitable jobs on a shared route lowers `F` further and raises the score.
