# Quay Cranes on One Rail: Zone Partition and Handoff Staggering

## Problem

`K` gantry cranes share **one straight rail** along a docked ship. On the rail they keep a
fixed left-to-right order: crane `1` is leftmost, crane `K` rightmost. They may roll along
the rail but **can never pass one another**. There are `M` container jobs; job `j` sits at
integer bay position `pos_j` and needs `work_j` units of hoisting time. Each job must be
handled by exactly one crane.

Because cranes cannot cross, a job at a smaller bay position can only go to a crane with a
smaller (or equal) index. So a feasible assignment is exactly a **monotone** one: sort the
jobs by position; the crane indices you assign must be non-decreasing. Equivalently you cut
the bay axis into contiguous zones, hand each zone to one crane in left-to-right order, and
may leave some cranes idle.

A crane that is given a non-empty zone spends time `D = work_zone + alpha * span_zone`,
where `work_zone` is the total hoisting time and `span_zone = maxpos - minpos` of its jobs
(one sweep across its zone). It is busy for exactly `D` time units in total.

**Congestion / staggering.** Look at the non-empty zones left to right. Two neighbouring
zones are *congested* if the bay gap between them (`minpos` of the right zone minus `maxpos`
of the left zone) is smaller than the safety distance `S`. A maximal block of consecutive
zones that are pairwise congested forms a **congested stretch**; inside such a stretch **no
two cranes may be busy at the same time** — their busy intervals must be disjoint, so you
must stagger their start times. Zones separated by a gap `>= S` work fully in parallel.

You output, for every crane, a start offset `s_c >= 0`; crane `c` is busy during
`[s_c, s_c + D_c)`. The plant finishes at the **makespan** `T = max over non-empty cranes of
(s_c + D_c)`.

## Input

```
K M S alpha gamma
p_1 p_2 ... p_K
pos_1 work_1
...
pos_M work_M
```

`p_c` is the standby power of crane `c`. All `pos_j` are distinct.

## Output

```
a_1 a_2 ... a_M
s_1 s_2 ... s_K
```

- `a_j` in `1..K`: the crane assigned to job `j` (input order).
- `s_c >= 0`: the start offset of crane `c` (integer).

## Feasibility

- Every `a_j` in `[1, K]`; sorting jobs by position, the sequence of `a_j` is non-decreasing
  (the non-crossing / monotone rule).
- Inside every congested stretch, the busy intervals `[s_c, s_c + D_c)` of its cranes are
  pairwise disjoint.

Any output breaking these scores 0.

## Objective (minimize)

```
idle    = sum over c=1..K of p_c * (T - D_c)     # standby energy while not hoisting
F       = T + gamma * idle
```

`T` is the makespan; every crane draws standby power `p_c` for the `T - D_c` time it is not
hoisting (an idle crane has `D_c = 0`). Smaller `F` is better.

## Scoring

Let `B` be `F` of the **even-count baseline**: split the position-sorted jobs into `K`
contiguous zones of (almost) equal job count, one per crane `1..K`, with the minimal
staggered offsets. Your score on a test is

```
ratio = min(1.0, (B / max(1, F)) / 10)
```

so matching the baseline scores `0.1` and reaching one tenth of its cost reaches the cap
`1.0`. Larger is better; per-test scores are averaged.

## Constraints

- `2 <= K <= 8`, `K <= M <= 5000`
- `0 <= pos_j <= 200000` (distinct), `1 <= work_j <= 1000`
- `1 <= S <= 100000`, `1 <= alpha <= 5`, `1 <= gamma <= 10`, `1 <= p_c <= 5`
- Time limit: 5s, Memory limit: 512 MiB

## Example

Input:
```
2 4 100 1 1
1 3
0 10
5 10
1000 10
1005 10
```

Two blobs (near `0` and near `1000`), gap `995 >= S`. Cutting at that gap gives crane 1 the
left blob (`work 20`, span 5, `D=25`) and crane 2 the right blob (`D=25`); the gap is wide,
so both start at `0`, `T=25`, `idle = 1*(25-25)+3*(25-25)=0`, `F=25`. The even-count baseline
also splits 2/2 here, so `B=25` and `ratio=0.1`. Balancing *work* would tempt a cut through
a blob, forcing the two cranes to stagger and inflating `T` — the insight is to cut at the
wide gap, not at the load midpoint.
