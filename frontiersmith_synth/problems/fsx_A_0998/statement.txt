# Ridgeline Valley: Planting the New Rootstock

## Problem

Ridgeline Valley's `n` orchard households are linked by a mutual-visits
trust network: `m` undirected ties, each with a weight `w` measuring how
much household `u`'s current rootstock choice weighs on household `v`'s
decision (and vice versa). Every household `i` farms `p_i` acres and is
either still on the OLD rootstock (`s_i=0`) or has already switched to the
NEW one (`s_i=1`). Recruiting household `i` as a permanent **seed grower**
-- someone who publicly commits to the new rootstock and never reverts, no
matter what the neighbors do -- costs `c_i` (sponsorship, grafting stock,
demo-plot money).

Every autumn, for `R` seasons, every household that is NOT a seed grower
re-weighs its trusted neighbors' **previous season's** choice: it sums the
tie weight of every trusted neighbor currently on NEW (`w1`) against the
tie weight of every trusted neighbor currently on OLD (`w0`). If `w1>w0` it
switches to NEW next season; if `w1<w0` it stays/reverts to OLD; an exact
tie (including a household with no ties) leaves it unchanged. All
non-seed households update SIMULTANEOUSLY, once per season, from the SAME
snapshot of last season's choices; seed growers stay NEW forever, from
season 0 onward.

You are given the trust network, everyone's acreage, sponsorship cost, and
starting choice, plus the season budget `R` and a target coverage `tau`
(percent). Choose a set of households to sponsor as seed growers before
season 1. Spend as little sponsorship money as you can while getting at
least `tau`% of the valley's total ACREAGE onto the new rootstock by the
end of season `R`.

## Input

```
n m R tau
```
then `n` lines, household `i=1..n`: `p_i c_i s_i` (acres, sponsorship
cost, starting choice 0/1)
then `m` lines: `u v w` (an undirected trust tie between households `u`
and `v`, weight `w`; no self-ties, no repeated pair)

## Output

```
k
z_1 z_2 ... z_k
```
`k` distinct household indices (1-indexed) to sponsor as seed growers.
`k=0` is allowed (an empty second line, or omit it).

## Feasibility

* `0 <= k <= n`; every `z_j` in `[1,n]`, all distinct.
* Simulate exactly `R` seasons as described above (seed growers fixed to
  NEW from season 0 onward). Let `A` be the total acreage on NEW after
  season `R`. You must have `100*A >= tau * (total acreage)`.
* Miss the target, or output anything malformed (out of range, duplicated,
  or with trailing tokens), and you score `0`.

## Objective

Minimize the total sponsorship cost, `sum of c_i` over your chosen seed
growers, subject to Feasibility.

## Scoring

Let `B = sum of c_i over ALL n households` -- the cost of sponsoring
literally everyone (always feasible, since then every household is NEW
from season 0 regardless of `R`). Your score on a test is
```
ratio = min(1000, 100 * B / max(1, cost)) / 1000
```
so sponsoring everyone scores `0.1`; a plan half as expensive scores
`0.2`, capped at `1.0`. Per-test ratios are averaged over 10 tests.

## Constraints

* `4 <= n <= 4000`, `0 <= m <= 5n`, `1 <= R <= 40`, `1 <= tau <= 99`.
* `1 <= p_i <= 200`, `1 <= c_i <= 100`, `s_i` in `{0,1}`, `1 <= w <= 9`.
* Time limit: 4 s. Memory limit: 512 MB.

## Example

10 households. A dense pocket `{1,2,3,4}` (all six ties among them,
weight 4), acreage 40 each, cost 6 each. A hub `5` (acreage 2, cost 2) tied
(weight 1) to all of a 5-household background ring `{6..10}` (ring ties
weight 2, acreage 10 each, cost 8 each), plus one weak bridge tie `(4,6)`
weight 1. All start OLD. `R=4`, `tau=40` (total acreage 212, need >=85
acres NEW).

* Sponsor the hub alone (cost 2): its own 5 weak ties can each move at
  most 1 of a background household's weight-6 total -- nothing ever flips
  besides the hub itself. Final NEW acreage: 2 (0.9%). Infeasible.
* Sponsor one pocket household, say `1` (cost 6): its neighbors `2,3,4`
  each see only 4 of their own >=12 weight tied to NEW -- outvoted, no one
  flips. Final NEW acreage: 40 (18.9%). Still infeasible.
* Sponsor two pocket households, `1` and `2` (cost 12): household `3`
  (neighbors `1,2,4`, weight 4 each) now sees `8` NEW vs `4` OLD -- flips.
  Household `4` (neighbors `1,2,3,6`, weight `4,4,4,1`) sees `8` NEW vs `5`
  OLD -- flips too, same season. The whole pocket is NEW after season 1
  and stays that way (self-sustaining: each member always sees the other
  three, weight >=8, outvoting anything outside). Final NEW acreage: 160
  (75.5%) -- feasible, and this is the cheapest feasible plan on this test:
  `ratio = min(1000, 100*66/12)/1000 = 0.550`.
