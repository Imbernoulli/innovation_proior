# Laser Cutter That Warps Its Own Sheet

## Problem

Your laser cuts rectangular **parts** out of a square metal sheet of `N x N` cells.
Every pierce dumps heat into the sheet, and the sheet **warps its own workpieces**:
if the accumulated heat at a part exceeds a threshold at the moment that part is
cut, the part warps and is **scrapped** (its value is lost). You choose which parts
to place, where to place them, and — crucially — the **order** you cut them, because
heat spreads and cools over time.

You are given `M` candidate parts. Part `i` is a `w_i x h_i` axis-aligned rectangle
worth `v_i`, and piercing it deposits pierce-heat `q_i`. You output a single ordered
list: the `k`-th listed part is cut at **contour-tick `k`** (`k = 1, 2, ...`).

## Heat model

When part `i` is cut at tick `t_i` and part `j` at tick `t_j` with `t_i <= t_j`, the
heat that `i` contributes to `j` is

```
floor( q_i / (1 + DA*(t_j - t_i) + DB*gap(i,j)) )   +   max(0, PW - PG*gap(i,j))
```

where `gap(i,j)` is the **Manhattan gap** between the two rectangles (the sum of the
horizontal and vertical empty-cell distances between their bounding boxes; `0` if
they touch or abut). The first term is a **transient** that cools with elapsed ticks
and with cell separation; the second is a **persistent warp** the neighbouring cut
leaves in the metal — it is **independent of time** and only spacing (a gap of at
least `PW/PG` cells) removes it. Only parts cut **earlier** heat a part, plus its own
pierce `q_j`. The total heat at part `j` is

```
H_j = q_j + sum over all i cut before j of
              [ floor( q_i / (1 + DA*(t_j - t_i) + DB*gap(i,j)) ) + max(0, PW - PG*gap(i,j)) ]
```

Part `j` is **scrapped** iff `H_j > THR`. A scrapped part still deposited its heat
(you already pierced it before it warped). `DA`, `DB`, `PW`, `PG`, `THR` are given in
the input. Because the warp term never cools, a densely packed part cannot be saved
by **any** cut order — only by leaving space; the transient term is what the schedule
controls at intermediate spacings.

## Input

```
N M DA DB PW PG THR
```
then `M` lines, part `i` (1-based, in input order):
```
w_i h_i v_i q_i
```

## Output

```
K
```
then `K` lines, in **cutting order** (line `k` is cut at tick `k`):
```
id x y
```
`id` in `[1, M]`, each used at most once; the part is placed with top-left corner
`(x, y)`, requiring `0 <= x`, `x + w_id <= N`, `0 <= y`, `y + h_id <= N`. Placed
parts **must not overlap**. Printing `K = 0` is feasible and scores `0`.

## Feasibility

Every id distinct and in range; every part fully inside the sheet; no two placed
parts overlap; exactly `K` lines and no trailing tokens. Any violation scores `0`.

## Objective

Maximize `F` = the total value of the parts you place that are **not** scrapped.

## Scoring

Let `B` be a fixed reference: drop the first `min(M, cols*rows, 25)` parts one per
cell onto a widely-spaced grid (cell size `3*maxW x 3*maxH`, where `maxW`, `maxH` are
the largest part dimensions, `cols = N / (3*maxW)`, `rows = N / (3*maxH)`), in input
order, cutting in input order. The spacing keeps every dropped part cool, so `B` is
their total value. Your score for a test is

```
ratio = min(1000, 100 * F / max(1, B)) / 1000
```

so the reference scores `0.1` and improvements score higher, capped at `1.0`
(ten times the reference). Per-test ratios are averaged.

## Constraints

* `40 <= N <= 500`; `8 <= M <= 420`.
* `1 <= w_i, h_i <= N/16` roughly; `1 <= v_i`; `1 <= q_i`.
* `DA = 1`, `DB = 2`, `PW`, `PG`, `THR` given in the input (`THR` in `[300, 500]`).
* Time limit: 4 s. Memory limit: 512 MB.

## Example

Take `DA=1`, `DB=2`, `PW=100`, `PG=25`, `THR=390`, parts with `q=250`. Two touching
neighbours (`gap=0`) cut consecutively: the second sees `q=250`, transient
`floor(250/2)=125`, warp `100`, total `475 > 390` — scrap. Spread their tick gap to
`30`: transient falls to `8`, but the warp `100` never cools, so `250+100=350`
survives here — yet a third touching neighbour adds another `100` warp and every
dense part is over `390`, unsalvageable by any order. Leave a `4`-cell gap instead:
warp `max(0,100-100)=0` vanishes and both survive regardless of schedule — but the
spacing costs you the parts that no longer fit. Layout and schedule are co-designed.
