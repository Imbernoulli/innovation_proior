# Mountain Rescue Relays: Minimum-Makespan Relay Schedule

## Problem

A mountain search-and-rescue command coordinates several **rescue missions**. Each
mission is a fixed **relay** — an ordered chain of **legs** that must be carried out
one after another (you cannot start a leg of a mission until the previous leg of the
same mission has finished). Every leg is handled by a specific **relay team**, and a
relay team can work on **only one leg at a time**.

You are given all missions and, for each mission, the ordered list of legs together
with the team assigned to each leg and how long that leg takes. You must produce a
**start time** (in minutes) for every leg so that:

- the legs of each mission run in order (precedence), and
- no team is ever handling two legs at the same time (no-overlap).

The **makespan** is the time the last leg finishes. Your goal is to make the makespan
as small as possible.

This is a job-shop scheduling instance: missions are jobs, teams are machines, legs
are operations. Finding the true minimum makespan is NP-hard, so any feasible schedule
is accepted and scored by how short its makespan is.

## Input

```
n m
job_1
job_2
...
job_n
```

- Line 1: two integers `n` (number of missions) and `m` (number of relay teams).
- Teams are numbered `0 .. m-1`.
- Each of the next `n` lines describes one mission `j` (0-indexed by line order):
  it starts with an integer `o` (the number of legs), followed by `o` pairs
  `team duration`, listing the legs in the order they must be performed.
  `team` is in `[0, m-1]` and `duration >= 1`.

## Output

Print `n` lines. Line `j` must contain exactly `o` non-negative integers: the start
times (in minutes) of the legs of mission `j`, in mission order.

## Feasibility

A schedule is feasible iff, letting `s[j][k]` be the start time of leg `k` of mission
`j` and `d[j][k]` its duration:

- `0 <= s[j][k]` and each is an integer;
- **precedence**: `s[j][k] >= s[j][k-1] + d[j][k-1]` for every `k >= 1`;
- **no-overlap**: for any two legs assigned to the same team, their intervals
  `[s, s+d)` do not overlap.

Any output that is malformed, out of range, or violates a constraint scores 0.

## Objective

Minimize the makespan `F = max over all legs of (s[j][k] + d[j][k])`.

## Scoring

Let `B` be the total work `= sum of all leg durations` (this is the makespan of the
fully-serial schedule that runs every leg one after another). Your score on a test is

```
ratio = min(1.0, 0.1 * B / F)
```

where `F` is your makespan. A fully-serial schedule scores `0.1`; a schedule ten times
shorter than serial (or better) scores the maximum `1.0`. The overall score is the
average ratio over all tests.

## Constraints

- `1 <= n <= 40`, `3 <= m <= 20`, `1 <= duration <= 99`.
- Time limit: 5 s. Memory limit: 512 MB.

## Example

Input:
```
2 2
2 0 3 1 2
2 1 2 0 4
```

Mission 0 has legs: team 0 for 3 min, then team 1 for 2 min.
Mission 1 has legs: team 1 for 2 min, then team 0 for 4 min.

A feasible output:
```
0 3
0 3
```
Mission 0: leg0 on team0 [0,3), leg1 on team1 [3,5). Mission 1: leg0 on team1 [0,2),
leg1 on team0 [3,7). Team 0 handles [0,3) and [3,7) (no overlap); team 1 handles [3,5)
and [0,2) (no overlap); precedence holds. Makespan `F = 7`. Total work `B = 3+2+2+4 = 11`,
so `ratio = min(1, 0.1 * 11 / 7) = 0.157`.
