# Mathematics candidates (vetted) for Innovation-Prior traces — THEORY ONLY

**Summary.** This list proposes **114 pure-mathematics candidates** to turn into first-person "discovery reasoning traces," split **59 RECENT (2010–2025, top-journal / laureate-level)** and **55 CLASSIC GAPS** (canonical theorems not yet in the existing inventory). Each row carries a *derivable conceptual move* (a key lemma, clever construction, or unifying reformulation) — chosen to avoid "long technical verification with no quotable idea." All recent entries were verified online for year / author / venue / arXiv id; classic entries were verified for standard attribution and the central proof idea.

Per-subarea counts (recent + classic = total):
- Number theory & arithmetic geometry: 10 + 7 = **17**
- Algebra & representation theory: 5 + 7 = **12**
- Algebraic geometry: 4 + 5 = **9**
- Analysis & PDE: 6 + 7 = **13**
- Geometry & topology: 6 + 7 = **13**
- Combinatorics & additive combinatorics: 7 + 6 = **13**
- Probability theory: 5 + 5 = **10**
- Logic, set theory & model theory: 4 + 4 = **8**
- Dynamical systems & ergodic theory: 4 + 4 = **8**
- Functional analysis & operator theory: 0 + 5 = **5**
- Mathematical physics (rigorous): 6 + 0 = **6**

Total: **114**.

**Dedup.** Cross-checked against `EXISTING_INVENTORY.md`. Notably EXCLUDED because already covered: Viazovska sphere packing (`sphere-packing-lattices`), Kantorovich optimal transport, Green-Tao, cap set, finite-field Kakeya, Roth (3-AP), Szemerédi regularity, Lovász local lemma, central limit theorem, all the covered classical analysis/topology theorems (Arzela-Ascoli, Hahn-Banach, Riesz, spectral theorem, Brouwer/Banach FP, Borsuk-Ulam, Stone-Weierstrass, Sard, Hodge, Morse, de Rham, Poincaré duality, Atiyah-Singer, Nash embedding, Calabi-Yau, Ricci flow, Galois theory, Fermat/Wiles, prime number theorem, Gödel, Cohen forcing, compactness, Löwenheim-Skolem). Within-batch duplicates were merged: DOZZ & KPZ (placed under math-physics), l²-decoupling (under analysis/PDE), Marton/PFR (under additive combinatorics), Willmore (under geometry/topology).

**Notable gaps / caveats.** A few flagship "recent" results are technically still preprints or just-accepted (flagged in their rows): Helfgott ternary Goldbach (book forthcoming), the telescope-conjecture disproof (to appear), the Cabanes-Späth McKay paper (Annals, to appear), Gaitsgory-Raskin geometric Langlands (arXiv series, refereeing ongoing). Functional analysis is intentionally all-classic (the live frontier there overlaps PDE/operator-algebras, captured under analysis & math-physics). Mathematical-physics is all-recent by design (classic rigorous-physics results like Bell, KT, quantum Hall, Yang-Mills are already in the inventory).
