# CS empirical-venue best-paper candidates (CVPR / ACL / NeurIPS / ICML / ICLR)

**Scope.** Best Paper / Outstanding Paper / Honorable Mention / Test-of-Time award winners (and a small set of extremely-high-citation, clear-conceptual-move non-award papers) from CVPR, ACL, NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR over ~2015–2025. All award lists were verified against official conference pages (thecvf.com, blog.neurips.cc, blog.iclr.cc, icml.cc, aclweb.org) and cross-checked against community trackers.

**Per-venue net-new counts:** CVPR 18 · NeurIPS 22 · ICML 20 · ICLR 17 · ACL/EMNLP 12 · other (ECCV/ICCV) 5 → **94 net-new candidates.**

**Excluded as ALREADY-COVERED (~28).** Many famous award winners are already represented in the 1261-entry inventory and were dropped: ResNet (CVPR'16 → `resnet`), DenseNet (CVPR'17 → `densenet`), StyleGAN (CVPR'19 HM → `stylegan`), SimSiam (CVPR'21 HM → `simsiam`), DreamBooth (CVPR'23 → `dreambooth`), Pixel RNN (ICML'16 → `pixelrnn`/`pixelcnn`), Neural ODE (NeurIPS'18 → `neural-ode`), Score-SDE (ICLR'21 → `score-sde`), EDM (NeurIPS'22 → `edm`/`karras`), Imagen (NeurIPS'22 → `imagen`), Visual Autoregressive/VAR (NeurIPS'24 → `var`), DPO (NeurIPS'23 → `dpo`), Chinchilla/compute-optimal (NeurIPS'22 → `chinchilla`), GPT-3/few-shot (NeurIPS'20 → `gpt3`), Scaling-data-constrained (NeurIPS'23 → `human-exact`), word2vec ToT (NeurIPS'23 → `word2vec`), AlexNet ToT (NeurIPS'22 → `alexnet`), GAN ToT / Seq2Seq ToT (NeurIPS'24 → `gan`/`seq2seq`), Faster R-CNN ToT (NeurIPS'25 → `faster-rcnn`), Batch Norm ToT (ICML'25 → `batchnorm`), DeCAF ToT (≈`alexnet`-features), R-CNN ToT (CVPR'24 → `r-cnn`), GoogLeNet/FCN ToT (CVPR'25 → `googlenet`), S4 (ICLR'22 HM → `s4`), GloVe ToT (≈`word2vec`), Native Sparse Attention (ACL'25 → `block-topk`), Lottery Ticket (ICLR'19 → `lottery-ticket`), Dueling DQN (ICML'16 → `dueling-dqn`), Lookahead/Image-GPT-adjacent items.

**Excluded as EVAL / BENCHMARK / PROBING / ANALYSIS / dataset / position / survey (~38).** No derivable method/theorem core. Examples: CheckList (ACL'20, behavioral-testing framework), "Do Androids Laugh at Electric Sheep?" (ACL'23, humor benchmark — the canonical exclusion), "Mission: Impossible Language Models" / "Why are Sensitive Functions Hard" / NL-Satisfiability (ACL'24 probing), political-bias tracking (ACL'23), "Are Emergent Abilities a Mirage?" (NeurIPS'23 analysis), "Is OOD Detection Learnable?" / data-pruning beyond-scaling-laws (NeurIPS'22 analysis), DecodingTrust/ClimSim/LAION-5B/MineDojo/PRISM/ATOM3D (datasets/benchmarks), "Stealing part of a production LM" / "Proving Test Set Contamination" / "Privacy Auditing" / watermark-detection (measurement/attack-audit), all ICML/ICLR/ACL "Position:" papers, "Reduced/Reused/Recycled" + "Measure Dataset Diversity" (meta-analysis), CVPR'24 "Rich Human Feedback" (annotation/eval), CVPR'25 Molmo/PixMo (open-data/model release), MaRVL/KiloGram/tangram (EMNLP benchmarks), "Does RL Incentivize Reasoning?" / "Artificial Hivemind" (NeurIPS'25 analysis/benchmark), CausalGym/CausalGym-style interpretability benchmarks.

**Gaps / judgement calls.** A handful of award winners are theory results (e.g. "A Universal Law of Robustness", "Uniform convergence may be unable to explain generalization", "On the Expressivity of Markov Reward") that ARE derivable conceptual moves but sit closer to learning-theory than to method+code; I included the ones with a crisp constructive/algorithmic core and skipped pure impossibility results. Older CV award winners that are pure 3D-geometry/optics (DynamicFusion, Fermat Paths, Dual-Shutter, propagating-light) introduce real algorithms with a derivable move and ARE included. Where a paper's idea overlaps a present inventory family but is a distinct mechanism (e.g. SEDD discrete diffusion vs. continuous diffusion; rectified-flow-transformer/MM-DiT vs. plain DiT), I kept it and noted the distinction.
