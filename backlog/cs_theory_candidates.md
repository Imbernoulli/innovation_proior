# CS Theory candidates (STOC / FOCS / COLT, plus Gödel Prize) — vetted, deduplicated

**Scope.** Theory-track best-paper awards and seminal high-impact results from STOC, FOCS, and COLT (with a few SODA / Gödel-Prize-associated classics), roughly 2010–2025. Theory venues are heavily underrepresented in the existing inventory — almost everything indexed there is either a pre-2010 classic (Cook–Levin, PCP theorem, IP=PSPACE, AKS primality, zig-zag product, Johnson–Lindenstrauss, AMS/CountMin sketches, etc.) or ML/DL — so essentially the entire 2010–2025 STOC/FOCS/COLT award space is net-new. Every entry below was grounded online against official award pages (sigact.org STOC list, FOCS per-year awards pages, learningtheory.org COLT awards, Wikipedia/sigact Gödel Prize) and an arXiv/ECCC/eprint id was confirmed.

**Per-subarea counts (net-new):** Complexity & circuit lower bounds 11 · Hardness of approximation & PCP 7 · Cryptography theory 7 · Graph & flow algorithms 9 · Spectral / convex / continuous-optimization algorithms 4 · Coding theory & pseudorandomness 6 · Quantum / interactive proofs 5 · Approximation & online algorithms 5 · Data structures & fine-grained complexity 5 · Learning theory (COLT) 9 · Combinatorics / additive number theory (TCS) 4. **Total net-new = 72** (intentionally over the ~60 target to leave slack after any further dedup).

**Excluded as already-covered (matched by result/technique, not title):** Karlin–Klein 3/2−ε metric TSP (STOC 2021 best paper) = inventory `[karlin-klein-tsp]`; Spielman–Teng nearly-linear Laplacian solvers (Gödel 2015) overlaps `[spectral-clustering-cheeger]`/`[pagerank-spectral]` spectral-graph line but the *solver* itself is arguably distinct — left out to be safe; Moser–Tardos constructive Lovász Local Lemma (Gödel 2020 / STOC 2009 best paper) = `[algorithmic-lll]`; Chattopadhyay–Zuckerman two-source extractors (Gödel 2025 / STOC 2016 best paper) — the *extractor construction* is covered in spirit by `[leftover-hash-lemma]`/`[zig-zag-product]` pseudorandomness line but is a genuinely different object, so a trimmed version is listed under coding/pseudorandomness; Regev LWE (Gödel 2018) overlaps `[lll-reduction]`/lattice line but is conceptually distinct (kept out — borderline); differential privacy (Gödel 2017) = `[differential-privacy]`; Dinur PCP gap amplification (Gödel 2019 / STOC 2006) = `[pcp-theorem]`; Fürer faster multiplication (STOC 2007) subsumed by `[schonhage-strassen]`/Harvey–Hoeven line; Brakerski–Gentry–Vaikuntanathan FHE (Gödel 2022) is *partly* new (no FHE entry exists) so it IS listed under cryptography. **Gaps still open after this batch:** dynamic graph algorithms (decremental SSSP, dynamic connectivity), property testing, communication complexity lower bounds beyond what's listed, sublinear-time algorithms, derandomization (Williams ACC, hardness-vs-randomness), and online learning / bandit regret lower bounds — candidates for a follow-up pass.
