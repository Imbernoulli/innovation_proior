Innovation Prior

Every paper here is rewritten as a first-person discovery trace — the method re-derived from the pain points and prior art of its time, hitting walls and self-correcting until it lands on the idea and the code.

Finished papers are written theorem-first: the answer arrives polished, with the struggle edited out. Innovation Prior runs that backwards. For each paper we reconstruct the chain of thought of how the method was actually arrived at, starting from the state of the field before it existed.

Each method comes with three artifacts:

  • Context — the pre-method ground: the research question, the prior art, and exactly what hurt at the time.
  • Reasoning — the long-form first-person trace of the derivation, walls and self-corrections included. This is the main event.
  • Answer — the distilled summary plus reference code, the place the trace lands.
