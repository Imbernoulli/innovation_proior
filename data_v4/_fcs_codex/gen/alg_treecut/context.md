We are given an unweighted tree on `n` nodes, `2 <= n <= 2*10^5`. Before cutting the tree, we may perform at most one operation: choose a current leaf, detach it from its only neighbor, and attach it to any other node, preserving a tree. Then we must remove exactly one edge, splitting the tree into two components. The cost is the absolute difference between the two component sizes. We must output the minimum possible cost over all choices of optional leaf relocation and final edge removal.
