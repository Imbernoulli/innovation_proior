We need implement `AdaptiveSpotScheduler`, an online CPU-only policy for a job that must complete `work` units by integer time `deadline`. At each timestep `t`, `decide(t, spot_available, progress)` returns either `'spot'` or `'ondemand'`. Running on either resource gives 1 unit of progress if available. On-demand is always available. Spot is cheaper when its price is lower, but choosing spot when it is unavailable wastes the step and loses `restart_overhead` progress. The policy must not assume future spot availability, must be efficient, must minimize cost on hidden availability sequences, and must guarantee completion by the deadline whenever the observed `progress` state is still recoverable.
