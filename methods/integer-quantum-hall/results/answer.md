# Integer Quantum Hall Conductance as a Topological Invariant

## Statement

For a two-dimensional electron system with an occupied subspace separated from current-carrying states by an energy gap or mobility gap, the Hall conductance is

`sigma_xy = (e^2/h) C`,

where `C` is the first Chern number of the occupied-state bundle over a two-dimensional parameter torus, such as the Brillouin zone for a band problem or boundary-twist fluxes for a disordered finite system.

For `nu` filled Landau levels,

`C = nu`, so `sigma_xy = nu e^2/h`.

## Mechanism

Landau quantization creates macroscopically degenerate levels in a two-dimensional electron gas under a strong perpendicular magnetic field. A filled Landau level contributes one unit of Chern number. The exact plateau value is therefore not a material parameter; it is the integer topological charge of the occupied states measured in units of `e^2/h`.

Disorder broadens Landau levels and localizes many bulk states. Those localized states can change occupancy as field or density varies, but they do not carry current across the sample. This lets the Fermi energy move through a range of localized states while the same occupied topological sector controls `sigma_xy`. A plateau transition requires extended states because the Chern number can change only when the gap or mobility gap protecting the occupied sector fails.

Edges provide the finite-sample transport channel. The confining potential bends Landau levels near the boundary, producing chiral edge modes. The net chirality of those edge modes equals the bulk Chern number, so edge transport is the boundary expression of the same topological invariant.

## Derivation

Linear response gives the dc Hall conductivity in Kubo form:

`sigma_xy = i hbar sum_{n != 0} [ <0|J_y|n><n|J_x|0> - <0|J_x|n><n|J_y|0> ] / (E_n - E_0)^2`.

For a band Hamiltonian `H(k)`, current is generated by parameter derivatives:

`J_i = (e/hbar) partial_i H(k)`.

Differentiating the eigenvalue equation for distinct bands gives

`<u_alpha|partial_i H|u_beta> = (E_beta - E_alpha)<u_alpha|partial_i u_beta>`.

Substitution into the Kubo expression cancels the energy denominators and leaves the Berry curvature of occupied bands:

`F_alpha = partial_x A_y - partial_y A_x`, with `A_i = i <u_alpha|partial_i u_alpha>`.

Thus

`sigma_xy = (e^2/h) sum_alpha C_alpha`,

with orientation set by the convention for `x`, `y`, and the sign of charge, and

`C_alpha = (1/2*pi) int F_alpha`.

The integral is an integer first Chern number. Since an integer cannot change continuously, small perturbations, disorder, and microscopic sample variations cannot move the Hall conductance away from its plateau value. They can only redistribute Berry curvature or localize states until a transition destroys the protecting gap or mobility gap.

## Artifact

The integer quantum Hall plateau is the equality between a transport coefficient and a topological invariant:

`measured Hall conductance = electrical unit x occupied-state Chern number`.

Landau levels make the invariant visible, disorder turns isolated integer fillings into finite plateaus, and edge states carry the robust current. The exactness belongs to the Chern number, not to a special semiconductor accident.
