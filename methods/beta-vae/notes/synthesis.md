# beta-VAE synthesis (grounding notes)

NO arXiv. Source: OpenReview PDF https://openreview.net/pdf?id=Sy2fzU9gl ("β-VAE: Learning Basic Visual Concepts with a Constrained Variational Framework", Higgins, Matthey, Pal, Burgess, Glorot, Botvinick, Mohamed, Lerchner, Google DeepMind, ICLR 2017). Verified by title search.
Canonical impl: 1Konny/Beta-VAE (model.py BetaVAE_H, solver.py loss). Loss confirmed.

## Pain point / research question
Want to learn, fully UNSUPERVISED, an interpretable, FACTORISED (disentangled) latent representation of the independent generative factors of image data — where each latent unit is sensitive to ONE generative factor and invariant to the others. Disentangled reps generalize, support transfer/zero-shot/novelty. Prior methods either:
- needed a priori knowledge of number/nature of generative factors (most pre-2016 work), OR
- unsupervised disentangling didn't scale to complex data, OR
- InfoGAN: unsupervised but built on GAN -> training instability, reduced sample diversity, sensitive to choice of prior & number of regularised noise vars, no principled inference network (encoder), needs some a priori knowledge.
- DC-IGN: semi-supervised (needs labeled/structured minibatches), so not fully unsupervised.
Also: NO general method existed to QUANTIFY degree of disentanglement -> can't compare models or tune.

## Background / setup
Data assumed generated by a true simulator from ground-truth factors: conditionally INDEPENDENT factors v in R^K (e.g. position, scale, rotation, lighting) and conditionally DEPENDENT factors w in R^H: p(x|v,w) = Sim(v,w). Goal: learn z in R^M (M>=K) with q_phi(z|x) approximating posterior, such that z disentangles v (one latent per factor), letting w stay entangled in a separate subset.
Disentangled representation (Bengio 2013): single latent units sensitive to changes in single generative factors, invariant to others.

## Baselines (load-bearing)
1. VAE (Kingma & Welling 2013; Rezende et al 2014) — THE base. Maximize ELBO = E_q[log p(x|z)] - KL(q(z|x)||p(z)). Reparameterization z=mu+sigma*eps. Already in methods/vae. Gap: with default weighting (beta=1) it achieves only LIMITED disentangling; entangles factors (e.g. azimuth with emotion on celebA, chair width with leg style).
2. InfoGAN (Chen et al 2016) — extends GAN to maximize mutual info between a subset of noise vars and a recognition net's output. Can discover some factors unsupervised. Gap: GAN instability, low diversity, sensitive to prior/num codes, no inference net, needs some a priori knowledge.
3. DC-IGN (Kulkarni et al 2015) — semi-supervised inverse-graphics net, structured minibatches where one factor varies. Gap: requires supervision/knowledge of factor count; can't discover unlabelled factors.
4. PCA/ICA — project onto independent/uncorrelated bases. Gap: independence != disentanglement; the independent components don't align with interpretable generative factors -> lack interpretability.

## THE DERIVATION (core, eqs 1-4)
Objective: maximize marginal log-likelihood over the latent factors:
  max_theta E_{p_theta(z)}[ p_theta(x|z) ].  (eq 1)
For inference, posterior q_phi(z|x). We want q_phi(z|x) to capture the independent generative factors v in a DISENTANGLED way. To encourage this, constrain q to match a prior p(z) that (a) controls capacity of the latent information channel and (b) embodies statistical independence. Achieve (b) by setting prior = ISOTROPIC UNIT GAUSSIAN p(z) = N(0, I). This gives the CONSTRAINED optimization:
  max_{phi,theta} E_{x~D}[ E_{q_phi(z|x)}[log p_theta(x|z)] ]  subject to  D_KL(q_phi(z|x) || p(z)) < epsilon.  (eq 2)
epsilon = strength of constraint (how much info bottleneck).
Re-write as a Lagrangian under KKT (Kuhn-Tucker 1951, Karush 1939):
  F(theta,phi,beta;x,z) = E_{q_phi(z|x)}[log p_theta(x|z)] - beta (D_KL(q_phi(z|x)||p(z)) - epsilon).  (eq 3)
beta = KKT multiplier = regularization coefficient constraining latent channel capacity & putting independence pressure (from the isotropic Gaussian prior). Since beta, epsilon >= 0, by complementary slackness:
  F >= L(theta,phi;x,z,beta) = E_{q_phi(z|x)}[log p_theta(x|z)] - beta * D_KL(q_phi(z|x)||p(z)).  (eq 4)
This IS the beta-VAE objective: the variational free energy / ELBO with a beta on the KL.
beta=1 -> exactly standard VAE.
beta>1 -> stronger constraint on latent bottleneck than VAE -> limits capacity of z. Combined with pressure to reconstruct (maximize log-likelihood), forces the model to learn the most EFFICIENT representation. Since data has conditionally independent factors v and the KL term (against factorised Gaussian prior) encourages conditional independence in q_phi(z|x), higher beta encourages a DISENTANGLED representation of v.
Trade-off: high beta -> better disentangling but blurrier reconstruction & may lose factors corresponding to minor pixel changes (constrained bottleneck drops high-freq detail).

## Why log-likelihood is a bad disentangle metric & beta<->epsilon
Can't learn optimal beta directly: optimal beta depends on epsilon, which is dataset/architecture-specific. When optimizing beta (eq 4) we indirectly optimize epsilon. So estimate good beta via the disentanglement metric or visual inspection.
(Appendix A.7) For given epsilon, solve eq 3 for optimal (theta*,phi*,beta*) with grad F = 0; rewrite optimal solution to original problem (eq 2) as function of epsilon: G(theta*(eps),phi*(eps)) = E_{q*}[log p_{theta*}(x|z)] (eq 11). Then beta = rate of change of optimal solution G as epsilon varies: deltaG/delta_epsilon = beta*(epsilon) (eq 12). beta is the "exchange rate" between reconstruction and KL nats.

## Normalised beta (appendix A.6, eqs 6-10)
Assume each pixel n (of N) conditionally independent given z, and each latent z_m (of M) conditionally independent given x. Then:
  recon term: E_q[log p(x|z)] = E_q[sum_n log p(x_n|z)]  (eq 7).
Divide eq6 by N: L ∝ E_q E_n[log p(x_n|z)] - (beta/N) D_KL.  (eq 8)
KL factorizes over latents: D_KL(q(z|x)||p(z)) = sum_m KL(q(z_m|x)||p(z_m)).  (eq 9)
Multiply KL by M/M, write as E_m: L ∝ E_q E_n[log p(x_n|z)] - (beta M / N) E_m[KL(q(z_m|x)||p(z_m))].  (eq 10)
=>  beta_norm = beta * M / N  (M = latent dim, N = num pixels). So a beta=4 with M=10, N=4096 is beta_norm ~ 0.0098 per-pixel-per-latent. This lets you compare beta across datasets of different pixel count / latent dim.

## Disentanglement metric (Sec 3, fig 5, eq 5)
Need to measure BOTH independence AND interpretability (alignment with true factors). Independence alone (PCA/ICA) insufficient.
Procedure: over a batch of L samples,
  1. choose a target factor y ~ Unif[1..K].
  2. for l=1..L: sample two latent reps v_{1,l}, v_{2,l} with the y-th factor FIXED EQUAL ([v_{1,l}]_y = [v_{2,l}]_y = same value), all others sampled independently. Generate images x_{1,l}=Sim(v_{1,l},w_1), x_{2,l}=Sim(v_{2,l},w_2). Infer z_{1,l}=mu(x_{1,l}), z_{2,l}=mu(x_{2,l}) via encoder.
  3. z_diff^l = |z_{1,l} - z_{2,l}| (absolute difference).
  4. z_diff^b = (1/L) sum_l z_diff^l  (average over the batch).
  5. train a LOW-VC-dimension LINEAR classifier to predict the fixed-factor index y from z_diff^b; report its accuracy = disentanglement metric score.
Why: if disentangled, the latent corresponding to the fixed factor y will have LOW variance across the pair (since that factor is shared) => the position of the low-variance entry identifies y => a linear classifier suffices. Taking pairwise differences reduces variance & conditional dependence on x. Linear + low-VC => the classifier itself can't do nonlinear disentangling, so the score reflects the representation, not the classifier.

## Architecture / hyperparameters (Table 1)
- 2D shapes / dSprites (VAE): Adagrad lr 1e-2. Input 4096 (flat 64x64x1). Encoder FC 1200,1200, ReLU. Latents 10. Decoder FC 1200,1200,1200,4096, Tanh, Bernoulli output. beta=4 used for dSprites quantitative.
- CelebA (VAE): Adam 1e-4. Input 64x64x3. Encoder Conv 32x4x4 (stride2) x4, FC256, ReLU. Latents 32. Decoder = deconv reverse, ReLU, Gaussian output. beta=250 used in fig1 (but note this is unnormalized; beta_norm small).
- Chairs / 3DFaces (VAE): Adam 1e-4, 64x64x1, Conv 32x4x4 stride2 x4 + FC256, latents 32, decoder deconv reverse, Bernoulli. beta values: chairs fig2 beta=5, faces fig3 beta=20.
- dSprites dataset: 737,280 binary 2D shapes; factors = shape{heart,oval,square}=3, posX=32, posY=32, scale=6, rotation=40 (over 2pi); 64x64.
- Prior p(z) = N(0, I).
- Metric classifier: linear, softmax, NLL loss, Adagrad lr 1e-2.

## Canonical impl (1Konny BetaVAE_H)
- reparametrize(mu, logvar): std = exp(logvar/2); z = mu + std*eps, eps~N(0,1).
- Encoder (nc->...): Conv2d(nc,32,4,2,1) ReLU x... actually: Conv(nc,32,4,2,1),Conv(32,32,4,2,1),Conv(32,64,4,2,1),Conv(64,64,4,2,1),Conv(64,256,4,1), View, Linear(256, z_dim*2). [outputs mu and logvar concatenated]
- Decoder: Linear(z_dim,256), View(256,1,1), ConvT(256,64,4),ConvT(64,64,4,2,1),ConvT(64,32,4,2,1),ConvT(32,32,4,2,1),ConvT(32,nc,4,2,1).
- forward: dist=encoder(x); mu=dist[:,:z_dim]; logvar=dist[:,z_dim:]; z=reparametrize; x_recon=decoder(z).
- reconstruction_loss: bernoulli -> binary_cross_entropy_with_logits(x_recon,x,reduction=sum)/batch; gaussian -> mse_loss(sum)/batch.
- kl_divergence(mu,logvar): klds = -0.5*(1 + logvar - mu^2 - exp(logvar)); total_kld = klds.sum(1).mean(0).  [closed form KL(N(mu,sigma)||N(0,I))]
- loss (objective 'H'): beta_vae_loss = recon_loss + beta * total_kld.
- Adam betas (beta1,beta2) e.g. 0.9,0.999.

## Design-decision -> why
- Add single scalar beta on KL (vs richer changes): minimal, one hyperparameter, directly tunable via metric search / weak labels; stable (unlike InfoGAN). beta=1 recovers VAE so it's a clean generalization.
- Prior = isotropic unit Gaussian N(0,I): factorised => KL pushes q(z|x) toward factorised/independent => independence pressure. Also caps channel capacity.
- beta>1 not beta<1: want to RESTRICT capacity to force efficient/factorised code; beta<1 would loosen the bottleneck (toward pure autoencoder), no independence pressure.
- Use mu (not a sample) as the inferred representation in the metric: deterministic, lower variance.
- Disentanglement metric uses pairwise DIFFERENCES with one factor fixed + linear low-VC classifier: isolates which latent is invariant to the shared factor (independence) AND whether a simple boundary recovers the factor (interpretability), which raw cross-correlation can't.
- Trade-off acknowledged: high beta -> blurrier recon (bottleneck drops high-freq detail), lose minor factors. So beta tuned, not maxed.
