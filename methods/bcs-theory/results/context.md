## Low-Temperature Facts

The phenomenon to be explained is not only vanishing resistance. A material below its critical temperature also expels magnetic field from its bulk, has a sharp thermodynamic transition, and shows low-temperature electronic behavior consistent with a gap in the excitation spectrum.

## Normal Metal Baseline

The ordinary quantum theory of a metal starts from Bloch states filled up to a Fermi surface. It treats low-energy excitations as individual electrons and holes that can be made arbitrarily close to the Fermi energy.

## Interaction Accounting

Most Coulomb and lattice-correlation energy is present in both phases, so it is shared between them and forms the normal-metal background. The microscopic question is about the part of the electron interaction that changes the state near the Fermi surface.

## Near-Fermi Attraction

Virtual exchange of lattice vibrations can make the effective interaction between two near-Fermi electrons attractive over an energy shell set by a phonon scale. Screened Coulomb repulsion competes with it in the same region, where many low-energy states are available.

## Macroscopic Constraints

A microscopic account also connects to the older electromagnetic clues. The magnetic response is consistent with rigidity over a finite coherence distance rather than high mobility of individual carriers. The setting links a near-Fermi microscopic description to the current response in the bulk.
