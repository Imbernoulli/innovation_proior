# Hodge theory for combinatorial geometries

## Problem

Three log-concavity conjectures from the 1970s, all open in full generality for an **arbitrary** matroid M (no realizability assumed):

- **Rota–Heron–Welsh.** The absolute values w_k(M) of the coefficients of the characteristic polynomial χ_M(λ) = Σ_{I⊆E}(−1)^{|I|}λ^{crk(I)} form a log-concave sequence: w_{k−1}w_{k+1} ≤ w_k² for 1 ≤ k ≤ r.
- **Mason–Welsh.** The numbers f_k(M) of independent k-subsets are log-concave.
- **Read–Hoggar.** The coefficients of the chromatic polynomial of any graph are log-concave (a special case, via the graphic matroid).

The structural obstruction: both sequences satisfy *additive* recursions (deletion–contraction χ_M = χ_{M∖e} − χ_{M/e}; f_k(A) = f_k(A∖v) + f_{k−1}(A/v)), yet log-concavity is *not* preserved by addition. The positivity must come from a hidden non-additive structure.

## Key idea

Realize the combinatorial numbers as **intersection numbers in a graded ring** and read log-concavity off the **signature** of a Hodge–Riemann bilinear form — but build that ring, and prove its Hodge theory, *intrinsically*, with no algebraic variety underneath, so the argument covers non-realizable matroids too.

The ring is the **Chow ring of the matroid** A*(M) (Feichtner–Yuzvinsky), defined for every M by generators and relations on the lattice of flats. The Hodge theory — Poincaré duality, the **hard Lefschetz theorem** HL, and the **Hodge–Riemann relations** HR (the "Kähler package") — is proved by a McMullen-style induction along **matroidal flips**, each flip adding one flat to an order filter and acting on the Chow ring as the combinatorial shadow of a blow-up. Poincaré duality comes first (a blow-up decomposition with a ±1 determinant); HL follows from *local* HR on the stars of rays, which are products of smaller Bergman fans (rank induction + the tensor-product stability of HR); HR is propagated across each flip by a rescale-and-limit argument that degenerates the Hodge–Riemann form to a block-diagonal sum of known-HR pieces, whose signature, being locally constant, transfers back. The degree-1 instance of HR is exactly the 2×2 Hodge-index inequality that forces log-concavity.

## The Chow ring and the main theorem

Let M be a loopless matroid of rank r+1 on E = {0,…,n}, with nonempty proper flats forming the poset P(M). The **Chow ring** is

  A*(M)_ℝ = ℝ[x_F : F a nonempty proper flat] / (I_M + J_M),

where I_M is generated by the quadratic monomials x_{F₁}x_{F₂} for **incomparable** flats F₁, F₂, and J_M by the linear forms Σ_{i₁∈F} x_F − Σ_{i₂∈F} x_F for distinct i₁, i₂ ∈ E. There is a degree isomorphism deg : A^r(M)_ℝ ≅ ℝ fixed by deg(x_{F₁}⋯x_{F_r}) = 1 on every maximal flag F₁ ⊊ ⋯ ⊊ F_r.

A real function c on nonempty proper subsets of E is **strictly submodular** if c_{I₁} + c_{I₂} > c_{I₁∩I₂} + c_{I₁∪I₂} for incomparable I₁, I₂ (with c_∅ = c_E = 0); e.g. I ↦ |I||E∖I|. Such c gives a class ℓ(c) = Σ_F c_F x_F ∈ A¹(M)_ℝ (an **ample** class). For a graded ℝ-algebra with Poincaré duality, set L^q_ℓ : A^q → A^{r−q}, a ↦ ℓ^{r−2q}a, the primitive space P^q_ℓ = ker(ℓ·L^q_ℓ), and the Hodge–Riemann form Q^q_ℓ(a₁,a₂) = (−1)^q deg(a₁ ℓ^{r−2q} a₂).

**Theorem (Kähler package for matroids).** *For ℓ = ℓ(c) with c strictly submodular and every nonnegative q ≤ r/2:*
1. *(Hard Lefschetz)* *L^q_ℓ : A^q(M)_ℝ → A^{r−q}(M)_ℝ is an isomorphism.*
2. *(Hodge–Riemann)* *Q^q_ℓ is positive definite on P^q_ℓ.*

Equivalently and more generally (the form actually proved): for every order filter P of P(M) the Bergman fan Σ_{M,P} satisfies HL and HR with respect to every strictly convex piecewise linear class; the case P = P(M) is the statement above.

## Proof outline

**Setup (the interpolation).** For an order filter P (upward-closed set of flats) form the intermediate Chow ring A*(M,P) on variables x_i (i ∈ E) and x_F (F ∈ P). At P = ∅ the linear relations collapse it to ℝ[x]/(x^{r+1}) = H*(ℙ^r); at P = P(M) it is A*(M). A **matroidal flip** P₋ ⇝ P₊ = P₋ ∪ {Z} (Z maximal in the complement, hence minimal in P₊) is one stellar subdivision. Three maps carry the algebra across it: a pullback Φ_Z (x_F ↦ x_F; x_i ↦ x_i + x_Z if i ∈ Z, else x_i), Gysin maps Ψ^{p,q}_Z : A^{q−p}(M_Z) → A^q(M,P₊), x_𝓕 ↦ x_Z^p x_𝓕, and the star pullback p_Z to A*(M_Z).

**Step 1 — Poincaré duality, via the blow-up decomposition.** Prove simultaneously, by lexicographic induction on (rk M, |P₋|):
  *(Decomposition)* A^q(M,P₊) = im Φ^q_Z ⊕ ⊕_{p=1}^{rk(Z)−1} im Ψ^{p,q}_Z,
  *(Poincaré duality)* the pairing A^q × A^{r−q} → A^r ≅ ℝ is perfect.
Surjectivity is the relation calculus (the ideal (x_Z) is exactly the sum of Gysin images; the top Gysin power x_Z^{rk(Z)} is absorbed by Φ + lower Gysins). Injectivity and trivial intersection use the inductive Poincaré dualities of the smaller pieces M_Z and (M,P₋) together with the **upper-triangularity** im Ψ^{p₁}·im Ψ^{p₂} ⊆ im Ψ^{p₁+p₂} (which kills complementary products unless the p-indices add to rk(Z)). In complementary degree the pairing matrix 𝓜₊ is then block upper triangular with diagonal blocks 𝓜₋ and 𝓜_p, so det 𝓜₊ = ± det 𝓜₋ · ∏_p det 𝓜_p = ±1. The base case ℝ[x]/(x^{r+1}) is a Poincaré duality algebra.

**Step 2 — Hard Lefschetz from local Hodge–Riemann.** For ample ℓ = Σ_e c_e x_e (c_e > 0) and f ∈ ker L^q_ℓ with r − 2q > 0: the image f_e of f in the star algebra A*(Σ)/ann(x_e) is primitive, and 0 = Q^q_ℓ(f,f) = Σ_e c_e Q^q_{ℓ_e}(f_e,f_e). If each *local* form Q^q_{ℓ_e} is positive definite on its primitive space, every term is ≥ 0, so all f_e = 0, i.e. x_e·f = 0 for all e; the x_e generate, so f = 0. After reducing to the combinatorial geometry M̄ (no parallel elements; same Chow ring and ample cone), the star of each ray is a **product** of ≤ 2 smaller Bergman fans (star(e_F) ≅ Σ_{M^F,P^F} × Σ_{M_F}, star(e_i) ≅ Σ_{M_i}). HR is closed under tensor product (reduced to the model H*(ℙ^{r₁}×ℙ^{r₂}), where the binomial-coefficient determinant has the correct sign by Lindström–Gessel–Viennot), so by rank induction every star satisfies HR — local HR holds — hence HL holds.

**Step 3 — Hodge–Riemann across a flip.** Assume HR(ℓ₋) for (M,P₋) and HR(ℓ_Z) for M_Z. The class ℓ₊(t) = Φ_Z(ℓ₋) − t·x_Z is ample for small t > 0. Decompose A^q₊ = A^q₋ ⊕ G^q_Z with the Gysin part G^q_Z ≅ (ℝ[x_Z]/(x_Z^{rk(Z)−1}) ⊗ A*(M_Z))^{q−1} =: T_Z^{q−1}; give the truncated factor the degree map (−x_Z)^{rk(Z)−2} ↦ 1 so it satisfies HR(−x_Z), whence T_Z satisfies HR(𝒯) with 𝒯 = 1⊗ℓ_Z − x_Z⊗1 (Tensor-HR). Rescale by S_t = id on A*₋ and ×t^{−rk(Z)/2 + p} on the p-th Gysin block, and set Q^q_t(a₁,a₂) = (−1)^q deg(S_t(a₁)·ℓ₊(t)^{r−2q}·S_t(a₂)); then (A^q₊, Q^q_t) ≅ (A^q₊, Q^q_{ℓ₊(t)}) for every t > 0, so they share a signature. As t → 0 the off-diagonal pairing entries carry strictly positive powers of t and die, while the Lefschetz operator degenerates to M^{ℓ₊(0)} ⊕ M^{𝒯}; the sign (−1)^{rk(Z)−1} from deg(x_Z^{rk(Z)}x_𝓩) cancels the orientation shift, giving

  lim_{t→0} Q^q_t = Q^q₋ ⊕ Q^q_Z.

By the inductive HR's, Q^q₋ ⊕ Q^q_Z is nondegenerate with the HR signature Σ_{p≤q}(−1)^{q−p}(dim A^p₊ − dim A^{p−1}₊). Signatures of nondegenerate forms are locally constant, so Q^q_{ℓ₊(t)} has that signature for small t > 0: HR(ℓ₊(t)). Since the ample cone is convex and HL already holds, "HR for one ample class ⇒ HR for all" closes the step. The lexicographic induction (base ℝ[x]/(x^{r+1})) yields HL and HR for every Σ_{M,P}, in particular for A*(M) itself.

## From Hodge–Riemann to log-concavity

Let χ̄_M(λ) = χ_M(λ)/(λ−1) = Σ_{k=0}^r (−1)^k μ^k(M) λ^{r−k}; the μ^k are positive. With α_M = Σ_{i∈F} x_F and β_M = Σ_{i∉F} x_F (each independent of i),

  **μ^k(M) = deg(α_M^{r−k} β_M^k).**

(β_M^k = Σ over descending k-flags of x_𝓕, and α_M^{r−k} selects the *initial* ones, so the degree counts initial descending flags |D_k(M)| = μ^k(M), matching Björner's shelling. On the rank-3 uniform matroid U on {0,1,2,3}: β_U² = x_2x_{12} + x_3x_{13} + x_3x_{23}, deg = 3 = μ²(U).)

For the main case k = r−1, log-concavity μ^{r−2}μ^r ≤ (μ^{r−1})² is

  deg(α² β^{r−2})·deg(β² β^{r−2}) ≤ deg(α β β^{r−2})².

Take any ample ℓ₂ and the degree-1 form Q^1_{ℓ₂}(a₁,a₂) = −deg(a₁ ℓ₂^{r−2} a₂). HR(ℓ₂) makes it negative definite on ⟨ℓ₂⟩ and positive definite on the primitive complement — Lorentzian. Restricted to ⟨α, ℓ₂⟩ it is either degenerate (α ∝ ℓ₂) or indefinite, so its 2×2 determinant is ≤ 0:

  deg(ℓ₁² ℓ₂^{r−2})·deg(ℓ₂² ℓ₂^{r−2}) ≤ deg(ℓ₁ ℓ₂ ℓ₂^{r−2})²   (ℓ₂ ample).

β_M is **nef** (a limit of ample classes), not ample, so apply this to ℓ₂ = β_M + tℓ (ℓ ample), ℓ₁ = α_M, and let t → 0; degrees are polynomial in t, so the inequality survives. That is the k = r−1 case; smaller k follow by induction on rank via truncation (μ^k(M) = μ^k(tr M) for k < r).

Finally: convolution of log-concave sequences is log-concave and χ_M = (λ−1)χ̄_M, giving log-concavity of the w_k (Rota–Heron–Welsh); the f_k are coefficients of a reduced characteristic polynomial of a related matroid (Brylawski/Lenz), so log-concave (Mason–Welsh); and χ_G = λ^{n_G}·χ_{M_G} gives the chromatic case (Read–Hoggar). All three from one signature.

## A small worked computation

For the rank-3 uniform matroid U on E = {0,1,2,3} (every ≤2-subset is independent; flats are ∅, the four singletons, the six pairs, E), the linear relations give β_{U,1} = β_{U,2} = β_{U,3}, and expanding β_U² with the incomparability relations leaves exactly three monomials,

  β_U² = x_2 x_{12} + x_3 x_{13} + x_3 x_{23},

so deg(β_U²) = μ²(U) = 3 = |D_2(U)|, the three initial descending 2-flags {2}⊂{1,2}, {3}⊂{1,3}, {3}⊂{2,3}. This is the "intersection-number" identity μ^k = deg(α^{r−k}β^k) made explicit.

```python
# Sanity-check that deg(beta_M^2) reproduces mu^2 for the rank-3 uniform matroid U
# on {0,1,2,3}, by expanding beta^2 in the Chow ring A*(U) and counting the
# surviving (incomparability-allowed) flag monomials = initial descending 2-flags.
from itertools import combinations

E = [0, 1, 2, 3]
# nonempty proper flats of U_{3,4}: all singletons and all pairs (rank<=2 closed sets)
flats = [frozenset(s) for k in (1, 2) for s in combinations(E, k)]

def comparable(A, B):           # one contains the other
    return A <= B or B <= A

# beta_{U,i} = sum of x_F over flats F not containing i; pick representative i=1,2,3
# beta^2 = beta_{U,i_F} * x_F summed so that only DESCENDING, comparable flags survive,
# with the chain's smaller flat contributing its min as the representative element.
def descending_initial_2flags():
    # flag F1 (smaller flat) subset F2 (larger flat); as flats grow the min drops
    out = []
    for F1 in flats:
        for F2 in flats:
            if F1 < F2 and len(F1) == 1 and len(F2) == 2:   # initial: rk(F_m)=m
                if min(F1) > min(F2) > 0:     # descending: min(F1) > min(F2) > 0
                    out.append((tuple(sorted(F1)), tuple(sorted(F2))))
    return out

D2 = descending_initial_2flags()
print(D2)            # [(2,(1,2)), (3,(1,3)), (3,(2,3))]
print("mu^2(U) =", len(D2))   # 3
```
