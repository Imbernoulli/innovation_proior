It may be considered that these speculations with regard to hetero-
chromatin behavior and function have been carried further than the
evidence warrants. This may be true; but it cannot be denied that one
basic kind of phenomenon appears to underlie the expression of variegation
in maize. In many cases, there can be little question about the similarities
in expression of variegation in Drosophila and maize. A heterochromatic
element has repeatedly been found to be basically associated with the
origin and expression of variegation in Drosophila. That a hetero-
chromatic element likewise is responsible for the origin and behavior of
variegation in maize has not been proved, although it is indicated, as the
analysis of Dt has shown.
