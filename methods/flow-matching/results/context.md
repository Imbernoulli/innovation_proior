# Research question

We want to learn a deep generative model that turns a simple, easy-to-sample prior (pure Gaussian noise) into an unknown data distribution `q`, from which we have only samples. A time-dependent vector field `v_t(x; θ)` (a neural network) defines a flow via the ODE `dφ_t/dt = v_t(φ_t)`, `φ_0 = id`, and the model density is the push-forward of the prior along this flow. Between the prior at `t=0` and the data at `t=1` the model traces a continuum of intermediate distributions — a "probability path."

The question is how to *fit* such a field so that the induced path carries noise to data, scaling to high-dimensional data (e.g. ImageNet-resolution images), with a training signal computable from a single network evaluation per example.

# Background

**Flows and the change of variables.** A diffeomorphism `φ` transports a base density `p_0` to a model density `p_1` by the change-of-variables formula `p_1(x) = p_0(φ⁻¹(x)) |det ∂φ⁻¹/∂x|`. Discrete normalizing flows stack invertible layers with cheap Jacobian determinants. Making the transformation continuous — a flow generated by a vector field — replaces the layer stack by an ODE. The determinant term then becomes an *instantaneous change of variables*: along a trajectory,
`d/dt log p_t(φ_t(x)) = -div(v_t(φ_t(x)))`,
where `div` is the trace of the spatial Jacobian. Integrating this over `t∈[0,1]` gives `log p_1` from `log p_0` minus the time-integral of the divergence. Exact likelihood is thus available by solving the ODE and accumulating the divergence numerically.

**The continuity (transport) equation.** The relation between a probability path `p_t` and a vector field `v_t` that "generates" it is governed by a PDE: `v_t` generates `p_t` if and only if
`∂_t p_t(x) + div(p_t(x) v_t(x)) = 0`.
This is a conservation law: probability mass is neither created nor destroyed, only advected by `v_t`. It is the necessary-and-sufficient test for "this field produces this path" and is the natural tool for reasoning about paths and fields jointly.

**Training continuous flows by maximum likelihood.** The straightforward objective is to maximize `log p_1` of the data under the instantaneous change of variables. Both the forward map and its likelihood require numerically integrating the ODE — a sequence of network evaluations per step — and backpropagating through (or adjoint-solving) that integration. Divergence is itself `O(d²)` unless estimated stochastically (an unbiased Hutchinson trace estimator `z^T (Dv) z` with `E[zz^T]=I` reduces it to vector–Jacobian products).

**Stochastic processes give a closed-form, simulation-free signal.** A stochastic differential equation `dy = f_t dt + g_t dw` defines a process whose marginal density obeys the Fokker–Planck equation `∂_t p_t = -div(f_t p_t) + (g_t²/2) Δ p_t`. Rewriting Fokker–Planck in the form of a continuity equation shows the *same marginals* are carried by a deterministic field
`w_t = f_t - (g_t²/2) ∇ log p_t`,
i.e. every such process has a deterministic ODE ("probability-flow") counterpart with identical time-marginals. For the standard noising processes — Variance Exploding and Variance Preserving — the per-example marginals are Gaussian with closed-form mean and variance at every `t`, so one can sample an intermediate point and form a training target without simulating anything. The path is whatever the SDE dictates, and one reasons about a data→noise process and then reverses it in time.

**Conditioning a regression target on a single example.** A *marginal* quantity defined by integrating over the data distribution can lack a closed form, while its *per-example conditional* version has one. For the gradient of the log-density (the "score"), regressing a network on the marginal score `∇ log p_t(x)` is intractable, yet regressing on the *conditional* score `∇ log p_t(x | x_1)` — available in closed form for a Gaussian corruption — yields the same minimizer, because the conditional target averaged over the posterior of the conditioning variable equals the marginal target. This pattern is established for score regression.

**Optimal-transport interpolation between Gaussians.** Given two distributions, the Wasserstein-2 geodesic interpolates them by *displacement* rather than by mixing densities: mass moves along straight lines at constant speed. For two Gaussians where one is the standard normal, this displacement interpolant is available in closed form and is an affine map of the sample; the means interpolate linearly and the trajectories are straight.

# Baselines

**Continuous normalizing flows trained by maximum likelihood.** Model the velocity `v_t(x; θ)` with a network; obtain likelihood through the instantaneous change of variables and train by maximizing it. *Core math:* `dφ_t/dt = v_t(φ_t)`; `log p_1 = log p_0 - ∫ div(v_t) dt`, with the divergence optionally Hutchinson-estimated. Every gradient step solves the ODE forward and backward. Auxiliary works regularize the ODE (augmentation, kinetic/Jacobian penalties, stochastic integration windows) to make trajectories easier to solve, keeping the same simulate-in-the-loop training.

**Simulation-free flow training via prescribed densities.** Prescribe a path between prior and data and learn a field consistent with it without solving the ODE. One line interpolates the *densities* linearly and expresses the target density via the divergence of a learned field; it involves an integral and is formulated in densities (not log-densities). A closely related line builds a marginal path from per-example conditional paths and matches the generating field, using a *logarithmic* form of the continuity equation that involves the log of the marginal density; both use the divergence of the learned field in the loss.

**Diffusion / score-based models.** Corrupt data toward noise with a fixed simple SDE; learn the score (or noise) of the per-time marginals by denoising-style regression against the closed-form conditional score; generate by reversing the SDE or by integrating the probability-flow ODE. *Core math:* conditional Gaussian marginals `p_t(x|x_1) = N(μ_t x_1, σ_t² I)` with fixed schedules; weighted score-matching loss `E λ(t) ‖s_t(x) - ∇ log p_t(x|x_1)‖²`; sampling field `f_t - (g_t²/2) s_t`. The path family is whatever the simple SDE produces; the loss carries a `λ(t)` weighting; the construction is phrased through a stochastic process that is time-reversed.

# Evaluation settings

- **Datasets:** CIFAR-10 (32×32) and ImageNet at 32×32, 64×64, and 128×128 for unconditional image generation; an upsampling/super-resolution setting (64×64 → 256×256) on ImageNet for conditional generation.
- **Architecture for the velocity/score network:** a U-Net (the configuration used by guided-diffusion), with channel widths, depth, attention resolutions, and head counts set per resolution; the same architecture is shared across the methods being compared.
- **Metrics:** negative log-likelihood in bits-per-dimension (with uniform dequantization and an importance-weighted estimate), Fréchet Inception Distance and Inception Score for sample quality, number-of-function-evaluations (NFE) of the adaptive ODE solver as a sampling-cost measure, and per-pixel MSE versus a high-NFE reference for low-cost sampling; PSNR/SSIM for super-resolution.
- **Protocol:** likelihood and samples computed by an adaptive ODE solver (`dopri5`) at tight tolerances (`atol = rtol = 1e-5`); Adam optimizer; identical architecture, hyperparameters, and training length across compared methods; sampling cost averaged over tens of thousands of samples.
