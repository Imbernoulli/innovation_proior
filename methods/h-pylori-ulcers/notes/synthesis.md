ABSTRACT (pubmed-6145023.html): 100 consecutive gastroscopy patients; spiral/curved bacilli in 58; cultured from 11; gram-negative, flagellate, microaerophilic, new Campylobacter-related species; present in almost all with active chronic gastritis, DU, GU → "may be an important factor in the aetiology."

- Marshall BJ, McGechie DB, Rogers PA, Glancy RJ. "Pyloric Campylobacter infection and gastroduodenal disease." Med J Aust 1985;142(8):439-44. PMID 3982346 (pubmed-3982346.html). Companion clinical paper: 11 isolates 1982 → 114/267 (Jan83-Sep84); cultured from 88% histologically positive, 0% of histologically normal; grew 3 days on BHI blood agar 37C +CO2; all sensitive to penicillin/erythromycin/tetracycline/cephalosporins/gentamicin/bismuth citrate; 80% to metronidazole/tinidazole; "antibacterial regimens... may provide a permanent cure."

## Antecedents (read, in refs/)
- **Acid/stress dogma:** Schwarz 1910 "No acid, no ulcer" (efetch-schwarz...22095019.txt). By 1982 ulcer = excess acid secondary to personality/stress/smoking/heredity. H2-receptor-antagonists (cimetidine/Tagamet 1977, ranitidine/Zantac) healed ulcers by suppressing acid — seemed to confirm acid theory. BUT ulcers recurred on stopping the drug → treatment not cure → lifelong therapy. (Lecture pp.251-252.) Tagamet ~$1B/yr by 1983; Zantac >$3B/yr.
- **"Bacteria cannot live in the acid stomach":** prevailing belief; stomach treated as essentially sterile.
- **Overlooked gastric spiral organisms (Marshall "rediscovered" them):**
  - Bizzozero 1892/1893 — spiral organisms in dog gastric glands (lecture ref 2).
  - Salomon 1896 — propagated in mice.
  - Krienitz 1906 — spirochetes in human gastric specimens.
  - Doenges 1938/39; Freedberg & Barron 1940 (Harvard) — "spirochaetes in ~40% of resected stomachs (ulcer/cancer)" (lecture p.253).
  - Steer 1975 — spiral bacteria in ~80% of gastric ulcer specimens.
  - Susumu Ito (Handbook of Physiology 1967) — EM of the organism from his OWN stomach (blind suction biopsy), assumed commensal (as in cats); didn't know almost all Japanese were infected (lecture pp.253-254).
  - Palmer 1954 (Walter Reed) — >1000 blind suction biopsies, found NO bacteria; concluded bacteria only post-mortem contaminants → killed the field for ~20 yrs (lecture p.253). KEY: he used routine H&E, not silver stain, and suction biopsies miss the organism.
- **Warren's observation (Robin Warren, pathologist, Royal Perth Hospital):** from ~1979 saw curved bacilli on gastric epithelium in active chronic gastritis biopsies, on silver (Warthin-Starry) stain; the bacteria sat where there was inflammation. The acknowledgements: "Robin Warren for showing me the spiral bacteria and explaining the meaning of gastritis."
- **Koch's postulates** (lecture p.262, Q3): 4 postulates — (1) present in every case; (2) isolated & grown in pure culture; (3) reproduces disease in healthy susceptible host; (4) recoverable from the experimentally infected host.
- **Magnus 1952** — accident victims: gastritis with 80% of gastric ulcers, 100% of duodenal ulcers; couldn't explain; ignored as paradox. Marshall's bacteria-vs-ulcer numbers matched (GU 77%/18-of-22; DU 100%/13-of-13).
- **Bismuth** as ancient (200-yr) ulcer remedy (Kussmaul); De-Nol (bismuth subcitrate) healed ulcers without lowering acid; Martin & Hollanders 1981 (Lancet) — lower DU relapse after bismuth (~60%) than cimetidine (~90%) at 2 yr.

## The discovery path (for reasoning.md, in-frame, present tense)
1. **The dogma & its crack.** Cause of ulcer "already known": acid + stress. H2RAs heal by suppressing acid → seems to confirm. But the ulcer always comes back when the drug stops. A disease that recurs the instant you stop suppressing acid behaves like an *underlying chronic process* that acid-suppression only masks. What chronic process?
2. **Warren's bacteria that "shouldn't be there."** Robin shows me curved bacilli sitting on the gastric epithelium, always where there's active chronic gastritis. Dogma says nothing lives in the acid stomach. Two possibilities: contaminant/commensal, or real and the textbook is wrong. The bacteria track the inflammation — that's not what a random contaminant does.
3. **Test the association properly.** Pilot (Warren's 27) inconclusive; design the prospective blinded 100-patient study. Result: 58/100 have bacteria; ALL 13 duodenal ulcers positive (p=0.00044 ~ 13 heads in a row); 18/22 gastric ulcers (77%); the NSAID gastric-ulcer cases are the bacteria-negative ones. Numbers match Magnus 1952's ignored paradox.
4. **The culture (Easter 1982 accident).** Couldn't grow it for 6 months because plates were discarded at 48h (throat-swab protocol); the 5-day Easter incubation let slow Campylobacter-like colonies appear. Now characterizable: gram-negative, sheathed flagella (Vibrio/Spirillum-like), microaerophilic.
5. **Association ≠ causation; the dogma won't budge on correlation.** Critics: "harmless commensal infecting people who already have ulcers." Need causation. Koch's postulates.
6. **Postulates 1 & 2 met** (present in cases; cultured in pure culture). **Postulate 3 is the wall:** reproduce disease in a healthy susceptible host. Animal model fails — piglets: instilled Helicobacter didn't take, biopsies stayed normal. Without an animal model, can't show causation.
7. **The leap: be the host.** Who is the susceptible host? Humans. The only person who can consent to the risk is me. By mid-1984 I know 43% of healthy blood donors carry it (so not necessarily fatal) and I have a cure (bismuth+metronidazole, 100% in first 4). Establish baseline (endoscopy: normal, no bacteria). Drink the culture.
8. **The self-infection.** Pre-dose cimetidine (lower acid to ease colonisation), fast, drink ~quarter beaker of cloudy brown culture in one gulp. Days 1-3 nothing; day 3 fullness; days 5-8 dawn nausea, vomiting of *clear, acid-free* (achlorhydric) liquid; halitosis. Day 10 endoscopy: spiral bacteria on Gram stain; histology teeming with Helicobacter + pus cells = gastritis. Postulate 3 met. Postulate 4: organism recovered. Then spontaneous clearance by day 14 (mystery), tinidazole after the day-14 endoscopy.
9. **Synthesis / mechanism.** Achlorhydric vomiting + halitosis = acute infection syndrome; matches Osler 1910 child illness & published "epidemic achlorhydric gastritis" outbreaks. Explains why ulcer patients recall no acute illness: infection is acquired in early childhood (~age 2-3), settles into lifelong asymptomatic chronic gastritis, occasionally erupting as ulcer in adulthood. Faecal-oral / family spread → looks hereditary & socioeconomic. Bacterium unaffected by acid drugs → ulcer relapses → "chronic, incurable." Survives acid by hiding under the mucus (neutral pH) and (Dutch 1984) producing urease → ammonia buffer. Acid-secretion abnormalities are downstream of the infection (interferes with endocrine feedback), not the primary cause; the "ulcer personality"/stress is the chronic illness's correlate, not its cause (Jung scale improves on eradication).
10. **The decisive cure argument.** If the bacterium is the cause, eradicating it should cure the ulcer permanently (move patient from red→green group). Bismuth kills it in vitro (zone of inhibition) — and bismuth's known *lower relapse* (Martin & Hollanders) is explained: ~30% were accidentally cured because bismuth eradicated the bug. The double-blind trial: 4 arms (cimetidine±tinidazole, bismuth±tinidazole); ulcer healing/no-relapse tracks *eradication of the bacterium*, not acid suppression; smoking irrelevant once eradicated. Cure of a "chronic incurable" disease with a 2-week antibiotic course.

## LAND ON (non-computational): the causal mechanism + Koch's-postulates argument.
- Mechanism: H. pylori chronically infects gastric mucosa (childhood acquisition, lifelong) → chronic active gastritis → in susceptible hosts, peptic ulcer (and gastric cancer). Survives acid via mucus refuge + urease/ammonia. Eradication = permanent cure.
- Argument form: epidemiological association (100-patient study) → Koch's postulates, with postulate 3 satisfied by the human self-experiment after the animal model failed → therapeutic proof (eradication cures; antibiotic-controlled trial). NOT code.
- Optional tiny numeric illustration: the "13 heads in a row" p-value (~1/2^13 ≈ 1/8192; reported two-tailed P=0.00044) as the back-of-envelope that the DU association isn't chance. Keep minimal.

## Design-decision → why
- Why prospective + blinded 100-patient study (not just Warren's chart review): chart review confounded (ulcer borders inflamed); need biopsies away from lesions + blinded scoring so gastritis can't be attributed to a nearby ulcer; 100 for easy percentages pre-spreadsheet.
- Why biopsy away from the ulcer: so gastritis seen is general antral gastritis, not reactive to the ulcer — kills the "commensal near damaged tissue" confound.
- Why Koch's postulates as the bar: it's the time-honoured standard for proving a microbe is a pathogen; association alone won't convince.
- Why self-experiment after pig failure: postulate 3 needs a susceptible host; no animal model; the consenting susceptible host is the investigator himself; "don't ask don't tell" because an ethics board would (rightly, given consent issues) refuse, and he'd do it anyway and then couldn't publish.
- Why pre-dose cimetidine before drinking: lower acid to make initial colonisation of a healthy stomach easier.
- Why bismuth in the trial design / why bismuth+antibiotic: bismuth is a proven ulcer-healer that does NOT lower acid → it can sit in a placebo-controlled arm ethically (every arm has a real healing agent) AND it is itself anti-Helicobacter; lets you separate "cure" (eradication) from "acid suppression."
- Why the cure (eradication→no relapse) is the clinching causal evidence beyond Koch: it's the interventional test — remove the putative cause, the disease stops recurring; explains the otherwise-mysterious lower bismuth relapse.
