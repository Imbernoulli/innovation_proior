I need the treated group's post-period outcome in the world where the treatment never arrives, but that object is missing. I can observe the treated group after treatment and I can observe an untreated group over the same dates, so I ask what each simple comparison buys me and what it leaves behind.

If I compare the two groups only after treatment, I mix the treatment effect with whatever fixed difference already separates the groups. That fixed difference can be social, geographic, industrial, epidemiological, or institutional; its exact source does not matter for the algebra. A later gap cannot by itself tell me whether the policy caused the gap or merely revealed an older level difference.

If I compare the treated group to itself before and after treatment, I remove that fixed level difference because the group is serving as its own baseline. But I now mix the treatment effect with time movement. Wages, disease, demand, measurement, or macro conditions may move between the two dates even if the policy does nothing. A before-after change is therefore not the causal effect unless the untreated time movement is zero, and that is rarely the right assumption.

The useful move is to stop treating the comparison group as a substitute level and instead use it as a substitute movement. I first measure how the treated group changes from pre to post; that change removes the treated group's fixed level but contains treatment plus time. I then measure how the comparison group changes over the same dates; that change removes the comparison group's fixed level and captures the time movement it experiences without treatment. When I subtract the comparison change from the treated change, the common time movement cancels, leaving the treated group's timing-specific departure from the path it would have followed without treatment.

This is why the design is not just a four-cell formula. Its insight is a counterfactual projection: start at the treated group's own pre-treatment level, carry that level forward using the untreated group's observed change, and compare the projected untreated post level with the treated group's observed post level. In Snow's water setting, this logic asks how mortality would have moved for improved-water households had they followed the contaminated-water path after accounting for earlier differences. In Card and Krueger's minimum-wage setting, it asks how New Jersey fast-food employment would have moved had it followed Pennsylvania's employment movement after anchoring at New Jersey's own pre-policy employment.

The required belief is therefore about an unobserved trend, not about equal levels. I can tolerate a stable gap between treated and comparison units because the first change removes it. I cannot tolerate a treatment-timing-specific shock that would have made the treated group move differently even without treatment, because then the comparison group's movement is the wrong counterfactual slope. Event-study graphs and alternative comparison groups can make the belief more or less plausible, but they do not observe the missing untreated post outcome for the treated group.

In regression form I express the same decomposition with a group indicator, a post-period indicator, and their interaction. The group indicator absorbs the fixed group level. The post indicator absorbs common time movement. The interaction coefficient is the residual movement that occurs only for the treated group after treatment begins, so it is the treatment effect only when the comparison group's untreated movement is the treated group's counterfactual movement.

If I have only two waves for each unit, I can collapse the same idea to a change score. The outcome becomes post minus pre for each unit, and the treated-group coefficient in that first-difference regression is the same treated change minus comparison change. If treatment intensity varies within the treated side, I can replace the simple treated indicator with the required exposure increase, while keeping untreated units at zero exposure. That is still the same logic: use observed untreated movement as the time adjustment, then ask whether the treated side moves by more or less than that benchmark.
