The activation lineage that matters here runs from saturating smooth units (sigmoid, $\tanh$) through hard rectification (ReLU, $\max(x,0)$) to the smooth self-gated family. ReLU cured the vanishing-gradient problem of the saturating units and is cheap and sparse, but it pays for that with the dying-ReLU failure — a negative preactivation produces zero output *and* zero gradient, so a unit can get permanently stuck — plus a non-differentiable kink and no curvature at all. The patches (Leaky ReLU, ELU, SELU) keep the piecewise-linear, kinked, monotonic character. The self-gated units changed the recipe: keep the raw input and multiply it by a smooth scalar gate of itself, $f(x)=x\cdot h(x)$, with Swish/SiLU picking $h=\sigma$ (giving $x\cdot\sigma(\beta x)$) and GELU picking $h=\Phi$ (giving $x\cdot\Phi(x)$). What these share, and what is believed to do the work, is smoothness everywhere, a shallow non-monotonic dip that *preserves a small amount of negative signal* instead of zeroing it, unboundedness above so large positives do not saturate, and boundedness below. The trouble is that Swish and GELU are each just *one* particular curve in this neighborhood. The open question is narrow but real: which precise pointwise curve in this family should be used, and is there a mechanistic reason — not merely a benchmark table — to expect it to ease optimization and stay stable as networks get deep?

I propose Mish, the parameter-free pointwise activation
$$f(x) = x\cdot\tanh(\operatorname{softplus}(x)) = x\cdot\tanh\!\big(\ln(1+e^{x})\big).$$
The design strategy is to hold the family's good properties fixed and search only over the gate $h$ in $f(x)=x\cdot h(x)$. I need a gate with the same asymptotic logic the incumbents have: $h(x)\to 0$ as $x\to-\infty$, so strongly negative inputs are suppressed gently, and $h(x)\to 1$ as $x\to+\infty$, so the activation recovers the identity on large positives. Softplus, $\operatorname{softplus}(x)=\ln(1+e^{x})$, is the natural inner primitive: it is smooth and strictly positive, close to $0$ on the far negative side and close to $x$ on the far positive side — ReLU-like asymptotics without the kink. Feeding that nonnegative value through $\tanh$ turns it into a bounded gate that starts near $0$, rises smoothly, and tends to exactly $1$ with no extra scale factor needed. So the gate is $h(x)=\tanh(\operatorname{softplus}(x))$, and the output is still input-times-smooth-gate, but not the sigmoid gate Swish uses.

Checking that it has every property I demanded: both softplus and $\tanh$ are $C^{\infty}$, so the composition is smooth and continuously differentiable everywhere, with no kink. As $x\to+\infty$, $\operatorname{softplus}(x)\to x\to\infty$ and $\tanh(\infty)\to 1$, so $f\to x$ — identity recovery, unbounded above, no positive saturation. As $x\to-\infty$, $\operatorname{softplus}(x)\to 0^{+}$ and $\tanh(0^{+})\to 0^{+}$, so $f\to 0$ — strong negatives suppressed. In between, for slightly negative $x$ the gate is small but the product $x\cdot h(x)$ dips *below* zero into a shallow negative bump whose minimum sits around $-0.31$, giving range $[\approx\!-0.31,\infty)$: non-monotonic, bounded below, and preserving a little negative signal, which removes the dying-ReLU precondition by construction. The silhouette is the same as Swish, with slightly different curvature in the negative region.

The mechanistic justification lives in the first derivative, because backpropagation multiplies the upstream gradient by this scalar at every unit. Differentiating $f=x\cdot\tanh(s)$ with $s=\operatorname{softplus}(x)$ and using the fact that the derivative of softplus is the sigmoid, $s'=\sigma(x)$, gives
$$f'(x) = \tanh(s) + x\cdot\operatorname{sech}^{2}(s)\cdot\sigma(x).$$
The point of this form is what it exposes once it is rewritten. The factor $x\cdot\sigma(x)$ is exactly Swish with $\beta=1$, and $\tanh(s)=f(x)/x$ whenever $x\neq 0$, so for $x\neq 0$,
$$f'(x) = \Delta(x)\cdot\operatorname{swish}(x) + \frac{f(x)}{x},\qquad \Delta(x)=\operatorname{sech}^{2}(\operatorname{softplus}(x)),\quad \operatorname{swish}(x)=x\cdot\sigma(x).$$
At $x=0$ the term $f(x)/x$ is a removable singularity read by continuous extension: $f(x)/x\to\tanh(\operatorname{softplus}(0))=\tanh(\ln 2)=3/5$, while $\operatorname{swish}(0)=0$, so the rewritten form returns $3/5$, matching the direct derivative exactly. The identity is therefore literal away from zero and exact everywhere once the $x=0$ value is filled in. Now $\Delta(x)$ has a precise role. Since $\operatorname{softplus}(x)$ is nonnegative and increasing, $\operatorname{sech}^{2}(\operatorname{softplus}(x))$ is positive, near $1$ on the far negative side, equal to $0.64$ at zero, and decaying toward $0$ on the far positive side. It is not a literal inverse Hessian, but in backpropagation it acts like an input-dependent scaling sitting inside the activation derivative: the Swish-shaped component is continuously modulated rather than passed at one fixed scale, while the gate term $f(x)/x$ carries the activation toward derivative $1$ on large positives. That is the preconditioning analogy I can defend — the derivative supplies a smooth local rescaling of gradient flow, not a hard kink and not a constant leak. It also lines up with the smoothness argument: a smoother per-unit map produces a smoother output landscape, whereas ReLU builds piecewise-linear facets because every unit kinks.

This is why stopping at Swish is not enough. Swish's gate $\sigma(x)$ gives only one smooth self-gating shape, while $\tanh(\operatorname{softplus}(x))$ keeps the same endpoints and changes the derivative structure in a way I can inspect: Swish does not reappear as "the same function renamed" — it appears inside *one term* of the derivative, multiplied by $\Delta(x)$, with the gate term $f(x)/x$ added separately. That yields a concrete hypothesis to test with depth, noise, initializer, and landscape probes — if the rescaling story is right, the payoff shows up as stability and smoother optimization rather than a new architectural dependency. Practically Mish is a pure drop-in, $x\cdot\tanh(\operatorname{softplus}(x))$, with no parameters; swap it for ReLU or Swish and change nothing else. The only cost is that it is a touch more expensive than Swish — an extra softplus and $\tanh$ versus a single sigmoid — which is the price paid for the smoother gradient.

```python
import torch
import torch.nn as nn
import torch.nn.functional as F


class Mish(nn.Module):
    """Mish: x * tanh(softplus(x))."""

    def forward(self, x):
        return x * torch.tanh(F.softplus(x))
```
