## Design-decision -> why table
- Finite field instead of C: DFT only needs an element of exact order n with Σ r^{iK}=0 for K≠0 mod d (Pollard lemma 8); C uses e^{2πi/n} but a finite field Z_p supplies a *primitive n-th root of unity* exactly when n|p-1 — no floats, no round-off, exact integer convolution. The convolution theorem (which is what we actually want) needs ONLY that algebraic property, not analysis.
- q ≡ 1 mod n (cyclic) / q ≡ 1 mod 2n (negacyclic): (Z/q)^* is cyclic of order q-1; an element of order n exists iff n | q-1; order 2n iff 2n | q-1. For x^n+1 (RLWE) we need ψ of order 2n with ψ^n=-1.
- ω=g^{(p-1)/n}: g generator => g^{(p-1)/n} has order exactly n. ψ=g^{(p-1)/2n}, ψ^2=ω.
- Cooley-Tukey butterfly over Z_p: the radix-2 split is purely algebraic (uses ω^{n/2}=-1), so it transplants verbatim from C to Z_p. N log N.
- negacyclic via ψ trick (not zero-padding to 2n + reduce): direct length-n NTT computes cyclic conv (mod x^n-1). For x^n+1 either (a) zero-pad to length 2n and reduce — doubles work + explicit reduction — or (b) pre-weight a_i->ψ^i a_i, b_i->ψ^i b_i; then the length-n NTT at the SAME ω yields, after pointwise mult and INTT, c_i = Σ_{j+k=i} a_j b_k - Σ_{j+k=i+n} a_j b_k post-weighted by ψ^{-i}. Derivation: substituting the ψ-weights makes the cyclic wrap-around term pick up ψ^n=-1, exactly the sign flip of x^n+1. Half the length, no explicit reduction.
- merge ψ into twiddles + bit-reversed table: ψ^i a_i prescale can be folded into the first stage of CT if twiddles are powers of ψ (odd powers) instead of ω; the CT DIT naturally consumes them in bit-reversed order; GS DIF on the inverse consumes ψ^{-1} powers and emerges in normal order. Combining CT-forward(std->bitrev) with GS-inverse(bitrev->std) cancels the bit-reversal permutation entirely (Pöppelmann/Chu-George).
- n^{-1} scaling: INTT = (1/n) M^* ; from lemma 8, M M^* = n I.
- NTT-friendly / Proth primes q=k2^m+1: make q≡1 mod 2n cheap AND k2^m≡-1 mod q gives K-RED fast reduction. Montgomery/Barrett are the general modular-reduction fallbacks; K-RED specializes to this q shape.
