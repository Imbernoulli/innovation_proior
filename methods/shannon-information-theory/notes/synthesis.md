## How the self-account shapes the trace
From the ETHW interview (Price), confirmed in Shannon's own words:
1. **Hartley's paper was the real launch point.** "The real thing for me was Hartley's paper which I thought was a good paper, but I thought had not yet taken account of things like noise and best encoding... They hadn't really done a maximum-minimum, perhaps min-max." So the trace STARTS from Hartley (log of number of messages) and the explicit gap: no noise, no best encoding, no probabilistic source. The narrator's job is to close exactly those gaps.
2. **Information modeled as a stochastic/probabilistic process is HIS conceptual move**, predating and independent of Wiener's "Yellow Peril." Price repeatedly: "To model the signal as probabilistic, that to me is the conceptual breakthrough." Shannon places it ~1939-41, before cryptography. So the trace's pivotal early move = treat the SOURCE as a random (Markov) process.
3. **The von Neumann entropy-naming story: Shannon does NOT confirm it happened.** Price relays Tribus's version (vN: "call it entropy... nobody knows what entropy really is, so you'll win every debate"). Shannon laughs and says "I'm quite sure it didn't happen between von Neumann and me." He already knew −Σp log p = entropy "from thermodynamics... that's an old thing," and the word "entropy" + "p log p" was already in his 1945 classified cryptography report. So IN-FRAME: the narrator recognizes the form as Boltzmann/Gibbs entropy himself, from his own reading of statistical mechanics — NOT handed the name by von Neumann. (The popular anecdote is documented in notes only; not used as the in-frame path.)
4. **"bit" was suggested by J.W. Tukey** (primary text confirms; in 1945 report Shannon used "alternative"). In-frame the narrator coins/adopts "bit" as the base-2 unit.
5. **Crypto and info theory "intimately intertwined," a duality** ("information, at one time trying to conceal it, and at the other time trying to transmit it"). Used lightly as flavor; the main spine is communication.
6. **Sphere-hardening / N-dimensional geometry** (Somerville's *Geometry of N Dimensions*, Heisenberg/Weyl on dimensionality) underlies the continuous-channel capacity. The discrete random-coding argument is the in-frame heart; sphere-packing is the geometric mirror for the continuous case.

## Antecedents (the lineage)
- **Nyquist 1924**: W = K log m. Speed of transmitting *intelligence* ∝ log(number of current values m). First log measure, but tied to telegraph signaling rate, deterministic, no source statistics, no noise.
- **Hartley 1928**: explicitly sets out to define a quantitative measure of *information* "based on physical as contrasted with psychological considerations" — i.e. STRIP MEANING. Information = successive selections from an alphabet; each selection eliminates alternatives. With s symbols, n selections → s^n possible sequences. Demands H = Kn additive in n, and that equal numbers of possible sequences (s1^n1 = s2^n2) carry equal info ⇒ K = K0 log s ⇒ **H = n log s = log(s^n)**. This is the additivity functional-equation argument Shannon GENERALIZES. Hartley's gap: symbols equiprobable, independent; no source statistics, no noise, no notion of optimal coding.
- **Boltzmann/Gibbs entropy** S = −k Σ p log p (Boltzmann H-theorem). Shannon recognizes his H has exactly this form (primary text footnote cites Tolman, *Principles of Statistical Mechanics*).
- **Wiener** (contemporaneous): signal+noise as stochastic processes, optimal linear filtering/prediction; the continuum, MMSE. Shannon: Wiener gets credit for "information as random process" but "never carried it very far... just a philosophical notion." Distinct line; Shannon's is discrete + coding-theoretic.

## The derivation spine (what reasoning.md re-derives, in order)
1. **Pain/goal**: a *general* theory of communication that accounts for noise and for the savings from source statistics + the receiver only needing the message, not its meaning. Separate information from meaning ("these semantic aspects are irrelevant to the engineering problem"; "the actual message is one selected from a set of possible messages").
2. **Hartley's log measure**: equally-likely messages, info = log(number). Why log: additivity (two cards twice the capacity), engineering parameters (relays, time, bandwidth) scale with log, math convenience. Base 2 ⇒ unit = **bit** (Tukey). One relay doubles states, adds 1 bit.
3. **Generalize: the source is a stochastic process (Markov chain).** Real sources (English) have unequal, correlated symbols. Need a measure of the *rate* a source produces information / our uncertainty per symbol. Set up H(p1..pn).
4. **Axioms → uniqueness (Appendix 2).** Require (i) H continuous in pi; (ii) for equal pi=1/n, H = A(n) monotone increasing in n; (iii) grouping/decomposition: a choice broken into successive choices = weighted sum. Derive A(n)=K log n (the s^m = t^n squeeze argument, monotonicity), then for commensurable pi = ni/Σni, decompose ⇒ K log Σni = H + K Σ pi log ni ⇒ **H = −K Σ pi log pi**. Continuity extends to all pi. RECOGNIZE: this is Boltzmann's H / Gibbs entropy. Call it entropy.
5. **Properties**: H=0 iff certain; H ≤ log n with equality iff uniform (max uncertainty); H(x,y) ≤ H(x)+H(y), equality iff independent; conditioning reduces entropy.
6. **Source coding (AEP, Thm 3,4; Appendix 3).** Long sequences of length N split into a tiny-probability junk set and a "typical set" of ≈ 2^{HN} sequences each of probability ≈ 2^{−HN}. So a source of entropy H needs ≈ HN bits to name N symbols. **Noiseless coding theorem (Thm 9)**: can encode at rate → C/H symbols/sec over a channel of capacity C, and no faster. Compression limit = entropy. (Fano/Shannon-Fano code as the constructive illustration; OPTIONAL tiny code.)
7. **Noise.** Output no longer determines input. Naive "count correct symbols" fails (independent-noise example: 0 info but looks like half-rate). Right correction = **equivocation** H_y(x), the conditional entropy of input given output. Rate R = H(x) − H_y(x) = mutual information. Theorem 10 (correction-channel) justifies equivocation as the unique right measure.
8. **Capacity** C = max over input sources of [H(x) − H_y(x)].
9. **Noisy-channel coding theorem (Thm 11) by RANDOM CODING.** The "surprising" claim: for H < C reliable communication is possible with error → 0 at FIXED positive rate (not rate→0). Proof = average over all random codebooks, not by construction. Typical-set counting: 2^{TH(x)} typical inputs, each output has a "fan" of ≈ 2^{TH_y(x)} reasonable input causes. Put 2^{TR} random messages into 2^{TH(x)} inputs. Prob a given point is a message = 2^{T(R−H(x))}; prob no OTHER message lands in the fan = (1 − 2^{T(R−H(x))})^{2^{TH_y(x)}}. With R < H(x)−H_y(x), exponent R−H(x) = −H_y(x) − η (η>0) ⇒ (1 − 2^{−TH_y(x)−Tη})^{2^{TH_y(x)}} → 1. Error → 0. Converse: can't beat C (excess R1 above C forces equivocation ≥ R1). Land: **C = max(H(x)−H_y(x)); reliable communication iff rate < C.**

## Design-decision → why table
- **Strip meaning / "selected from a set"**: engineering must work for every possible message; semantics vary by receiver and are unmeasurable (Hartley's "psychological factors"). Without stripping it, no universal measure exists.
- **Logarithmic measure**: additivity (independent sources add; two channels twice capacity), linear in engineering resources (relays, time, bandwidth ~ log of #states), and analytic convenience. Alternatives (raw count N, or any nonlinear f(N)) break additivity / don't match intuition.
- **Base 2 → bit**: natural for two-state devices (relay/flip-flop = 1 bit); Tukey's word. Base e (nats) or 10 (Hartleys) are just unit changes.
- **Source as Markov process**: captures real correlations (English digram/trigram statistics) the equiprobable-independent model misses; ergodicity lets time-average = ensemble-average so a single long sample is representative.
- **The three H-axioms**: continuity (small prob change → small H change), monotonicity for uniform (more equally-likely options = more uncertainty), grouping (info is invariant to how you stage the choices). These are exactly the minimal properties any "amount of choice" must have; together they FORCE −Σp log p uniquely.
- **Why −Σ p log p and not e.g. variance or 1−Σp²**: only −Σp log p satisfies grouping/additivity exactly (Gini 1−Σp² fails additivity over independent sources). Recognized as Boltzmann/Gibbs entropy.
- **Equivocation H_y(x) as the noise penalty**: must subtract the receiver's *remaining uncertainty about the input*; the independent-noise example shows raw throughput overcounts. Theorem 10 pins it as the unique correct amount via a correction channel.
- **Capacity = MAX over input sources**: capacity is a property of the channel, so optimize over how you drive it.
- **Random coding (not explicit construction)**: an existence proof — average error over all codebooks < ε ⇒ some codebook beats ε. Explicit good codes are hard (≈ as hard as explicitly constructing a random-looking sequence); the probabilistic method sidesteps this. This is the central counterintuitive move: positive rate AND vanishing error simultaneously.
