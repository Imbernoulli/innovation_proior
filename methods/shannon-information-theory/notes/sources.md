## von Neumann entropy-naming anecdote (documented, NOT used as the in-frame path)
Tribus's account: von Neumann told Shannon to call it "entropy" because (1) the uncertainty function already
goes by that name in statistical mechanics and (2) "no one knows what entropy really is, so in a debate you
will always have the advantage." Source: M. Tribus & E.C. McIrvine, and Tribus 1971 interview.
**In the ETHW oral history Shannon DENIES this happened between him and von Neumann** — he laughs and says
"I'm quite sure it didn't happen between von Neumann and me," that he knew entropy "from thermodynamics...
that's an old thing," and the word entropy + the p log p identification were already in his 1945 classified
report. So the trace has the narrator recognize the entropy form himself from statistical mechanics.
