Notes
The first 2-approximation for Steiner Forest is in the paper [1] by Agrawal, Klein, and Ravi. The exposition
above is from the influential paper [2] by Goemans and Williamson which also gives primal-dual algorithms
for many other ‚Äúconstrained forest‚ÄĚ problems. The integrality gap of the LP is also 2, even for the case when
all pairs are terminals (the spanning tree problem), and the above algorithm remains the best approximation
algorithm known for the Steiner forest problem to date. A strengthening of this relaxation is proposed in
the paper [3] by KoŐąnemann, Leonardi, SchaŐąfer, and van Zwam; unfortunately, that relaxation also has a gap
arbitrarily close to 2.
