# Context

## Problem

A straight shoreline runs along a line; it separates a beach (region 1) from the water (region 2). A lifeguard runs across the sand at speed $v_1 = 5\ \mathrm{m/s}$ and swims through the water at speed $v_2 = 2\ \mathrm{m/s}$.

The lifeguard starts at point $A$ on the beach, at perpendicular distance $30\ \mathrm{m}$ from the shoreline, and must reach a swimmer at point $B$ in the water, at perpendicular distance $40\ \mathrm{m}$ from the shoreline on the far side. Measured along the shoreline, the foot of $A$ (its nearest point on the shoreline) and the foot of $B$ are $100\ \mathrm{m}$ apart.

The lifeguard may choose any point on the shoreline at which to enter the water, running in a straight line to that point and then swimming in a straight line to $B$. Find the path from $A$ to $B$ that takes the least total time, and determine that least time.
