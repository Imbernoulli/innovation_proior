# Context

## Problem

Consider a $(2m - 1) \times (2n - 1)$ rectangular region, where $m$ and $n$ are integers such that $m,n \ge 4$. This region is to be tiled using the following two types of tiles made from $1 \times 1$ squares:

1. a three-square L-tromino, a $2 \times 2$ block with one corner square removed, congruent to the cell set $\{(0,0),(0,1),(1,0)\}$;
2. a four-square zig-zag tetromino, a $2 \times 3$ block with the upper-left and lower-right squares removed, congruent to the cell set $\{(0,1),(0,2),(1,0),(1,1)\}$.

The tiles may be rotated and reflected, as long as their sides are parallel to the sides of the rectangular region. They must all fit within the region, and they must cover it completely without overlapping.

What is the minimum number of tiles required to tile the region?
