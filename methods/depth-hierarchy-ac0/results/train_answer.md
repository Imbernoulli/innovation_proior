We have known for a long time that depth is a genuine resource for constant-depth Boolean circuits over $\{\text{AND}, \text{OR}, \text{NOT}\}$: for each $d$ there is an explicit linear-size depth-$d$ formula that any depth-$(d-1)$ circuit needs size $\exp(n^{\Omega(1/d)})$ to compute. But that statement is about computing the formula *exactly* â€” agreeing on all $2^n$ inputs. It leaves the door wide open for a modest depth-$(d-1)$ circuit to agree with the depth-$d$ formula on $99.9\%$ of inputs, or on $(1-o(1))\cdot 2^n$ of them, and the worst-case theorem would never notice. I want to slam that door: a depth-$d$ formula $f$ such that *every* subexponential depth-$(d-1)$ circuit agrees with $f$ on at most $(\tfrac12 + o(1))\cdot 2^n$ inputs â€” correlation essentially zero, the way parity has zero correlation with all of AC$^0$. The strong $\tfrac12$ bound is not cosmetic. A worst-case $d$-vs-$(d-1)$ separation yields an oracle making the polynomial hierarchy infinite; an *average-case* one â€” a correlation bound â€” yields the same separation relative to a *random* oracle with probability one, settling whether the polynomial hierarchy is infinite relative to a random oracle. And if $f$ has low total influence, a $\tfrac12$-inapproximability statement kills any approximate converse to the Linialâ€“Mansourâ€“Nisan / Boppana total-influence bound. I want this for all $d$ at once.

Every correlation bound against small-depth circuits goes through random restrictions, run as a sequence $\rho \in \{0,1,\ast\}^n$ until three things happen. Property 1, the approximator simplifies: the circuit $C$, restricted by the composition, collapses to a shallow decision tree (the switching lemma â€” a width-$w$ DNF hit by $R(p)$ fails to be a depth-$t$ decision tree with probability $\le (5pw)^t$). Property 2, the target retains structure: $f$ restricted by the same composition stays well-structured, still uncorrelated with any shallow tree. Property 3, the composition completes to uniform: evaluating $f$ on a uniform input equals first applying the restrictions and then evaluating on a fresh uniform point â€” this is the one that turns "$f$ and $C$ disagree somewhere" into "they disagree on half the inputs." For parity all three are easy because parity is *much more complex than the circuit to begin with*: $R(p)$ carves out a uniform subcube (Property 3), parity restricts to parity on the survivors (Property 2), and the switching lemma gives Property 1. The depth hierarchy destroys that asymmetry â€” now the target is *itself* a depth-$d$ formula, only one layer deeper than $C$, while $C$ gets to be exponentially larger. The natural target is a Sipser function: read-once, monotone, depth-$d$, alternating AND/OR layers of roughly equal fan-in, tuned to be balanced, and self-similar â€” peel its bottom layer and you essentially get a depth-$(d-1)$ Sipser. But $R(p)$ wrecks it: under $R(p)$ a constant fraction of bottom AND-gates die, the fan-ins scramble, and Property 2 fails. HĂĄstad's fix â€” blockwise restrictions matched to the Sipser blocks â€” restores Property 2 and keeps Property 1, but to mirror the blocks they must be *correlated across coordinates*, and once correlated their composition does not complete to uniform (in fact not to any product distribution), so Property 3 fails and he gets only worst-case. The tension is structural: Property 2 *forces* correlations among the restricted coordinates; Property 3 *forbids* them. As long as the only operation is "fix a variable or keep it alive," one is being asked to be correlated and independent in the very same coordinates, and the method stalls.

So I question the toolbox. A restriction can only send a surviving variable to itself ($x_i \mapsto x_i$). I propose to let a surviving variable map to *something else* â€” a fresh variable in a smaller space â€” and I call the resulting operation a **random projection**. Group the formal variables into blocks $\mathcal{X} = \{x_{a,i} : a \in A, i \in [\ell]\}$ and introduce a smaller space $\mathcal{Y} = \{y_a : a \in A\}$, one new variable per block. For $\rho \in \{0,1,\ast\}^{A\times[\ell]}$, the $\rho$-projection of $f$ is $(\mathrm{proj}_\rho f)(y) = f(x)$ with $x_{a,i} = y_a$ when $\rho_{a,i} = \ast$ and $x_{a,i} = \rho_{a,i}$ otherwise. All alive variables of a block **collide** onto the single fresh variable $y_a$. Restrictions are exactly the case $\ell = 1$, $\mathcal{Y} \equiv \mathcal{X}$, so projections strictly generalize them; this re-mapping of survivors is in the spirit of the generalized restrictions Impagliazzo and Segerlind used for constant-depth Frege lower bounds, now turned on the average-case depth hierarchy. The collision does three jobs no restriction can do simultaneously, and they are exactly the three properties. First, the target peels cleanly: a bottom AND-gate on block $a$, after projection, becomes $\text{AND}(y_a, \dots, y_a) = y_a$, and an OR-gate becomes $\text{OR}(y_a,\dots,y_a)=y_a$, so if $\text{Sipser}^{(k)}$ is Sipser truncated to depth $k$ (each depth-$k$ gate a fresh variable), then $\mathrm{proj}\,\text{Sipser}^{(k)} \equiv \text{Sipser}^{(k-1)}$ â€” projection walks down the Sipser hierarchy by exactly one layer, giving Property 2 *by construction* rather than by luck. Second, the approximator gets extra collapse: a gate of $C$ containing both $x_{a,i}$ and $\bar x_{a,j}$ from the same block becomes $y_a \wedge \bar y_a = 0$, dead for free with no variable fixed â€” simplification a restriction cannot produce, which is exactly what lets the switching lemma cut depth faster (Property 1). Third, the extra freedom of mapping survivors to a fresh smaller space leaves room for a clean product completion (Property 3), once the biases are tuned correctly.

I instantiate the target as $\text{BalancedSipser}_d$: depth-$d$, read-once, monotone, alternating, depth-regular, all $n$ leaves at depth $d$, with depth-$(d-1)$ gates AND (so the root is OR for even $d$, AND for odd $d$). The fan-in sequence is $w_{d-1} := m$ at the bottom, with $p := 2^{-m}$ the probability a bottom AND is satisfied by a uniform input; the middle fan-ins $w_k := w := \lfloor m\cdot 2^m / \log e\rfloor$ for $1 \le k \le d-2$, chosen so each layer self-balances â€” a depth-$(d-2)$ OR sits above $w$ bottom ANDs each satisfied w.p. $p$, so it is unsatisfied w.p. $\approx e^{-pw}$, and $pw = 2^{-m}\cdot(m2^m/\log e) = m\ln 2$ gives $e^{-pw} = 2^{-m} = p$, the same scale layer to layer; and the top fan-in $w_0 :=$ the smallest integer with $(1-t_1)^{q w_0} \le \tfrac12$, so the projected top gate has bias $\approx \tfrac12$. Then $n = \prod_k w_k \approx (m 2^m/\log e)^{d-1}/\log e$, i.e. $m \approx (\log n)/d$, and the formula is balanced, $\Pr_X[\text{BalancedSipser}_d(X)=1] = \tfrac12 \pm \tilde O(w^{-1/12})$.

Two base probabilities run the whole construction: a keep-alive scale $q := \sqrt p = 2^{-m/2}$ and a tiny all-$\circ$-forcing probability $\lambda := (\log w)^{3/2}/w^{5/4}$. The keep-alive $q$ gives a block of $w$ variables an alive set of expected size $qw = \tilde\Theta(\sqrt w)$ â€” small enough that the approximator's bottom two layers switch, large enough that a Sipser gate keeps a huge surviving fan-in; and $q = \sqrt p$ specifically because the bottom layer needs $p$ but every layer after wants the survivor count to shrink only by a $\sqrt{\cdot}$ so the formula survives all $d-1$ peels. The choice of $\lambda$ is forced by two coincidences it has to create at once: in the switching-lemma weight count I need a per-level multiplicative gap of $w^{1/4}$, and $\lambda/q^3 = (\log w)^{3/2} w^{-5/4} / ((\log w)^{3/2} w^{-3/2}) = w^{1/4}$ exactly; and in the completion algebra that little all-$\circ$ event is what lets each block's probability mass close up. The per-stage biases are then *solved for*, not chosen: $t_{d-1} := (p-\lambda)/q$ and $t_{k-1} := ((1-t_k)^{qw} - \lambda)/q$ for $k = d-1, \dots, 2$, and one checks by downward induction ($|t_k q - p| \le (2m)^{d-1-k}\lambda$) that $t_k = q \pm q^{1.1}$ for every $k$, so all biases stay $\approx q$ through the whole range $2 \le d \le c\sqrt{\log n}/\log\log n$.

The projection sequence is built so that completion to uniform holds despite each stage being correlated. The **initial projection** $R_{\text{init}}$, over the $n$ bottom variables, draws each bottom block independently: all-$1$'s with probability $\lambda$; a uniform alive/$1$ string excluding all-$1$ (kept alive) with probability $q$; a uniform $0/1$ string excluding all-$1$ (determined, has a $0$) with probability $1-\lambda-q$. The choice $t_{d-1}=(p-\lambda)/q$ is exactly what forces $\lambda + q\,t_{d-1} = p$, which makes both the all-$1$ mass and the everything-else mass equal $2^{-m}$ when survivors are re-randomized with the $t_{d-1}$-biased coin â€” that is the uniform distribution, block by block. The **subsequent projection** $R(\tau)$ is *adaptive*, depending on the previous outcome $\tau$, because after a stage the alive-set sizes $|S_a|$ vary and the per-block keep-alive must react to keep completion exact. Per block with alive set $S_a = \tau_a^{-1}(\ast)$: if the gate is already determined toward $\circ$ or $S_a$ is not $k$-acceptable, simply re-randomize the alive coords with the $t_k$-biased coin; otherwise draw all-$\circ$ w.p. $\lambda$, kept-alive w.p. $q_a$, determined w.p. $1-\lambda-q_a$, where $q_a := ((1-t_k)^{|S_a|} - \lambda)/t_{k-1}$, i.e. $(1-t_k)^{|S_a|} = \lambda + q_a t_{k-1}$ â€” the exact per-block analogue of the $t_{d-1}$ computation, again solved (not chosen) by demanding completion. Here $S$ is **$k$-acceptable** if $|S| = qw \pm w^{\beta(k,d)}$ with $\beta(k,d) := \tfrac13 + (d-k-1)/(12d) \in [\tfrac13, \tfrac{5}{12})$ â€” a deviation window that *widens toward the root* precisely so typicality can outrun the per-stage error accumulation, yet stays below $\tfrac12$ so Chernoff control survives. Writing $\Psi(f) := \mathrm{proj}_{\rho^{(2)}} \cdots \mathrm{proj}_{\rho^{(d)}} f$ with $\rho^{(d)} \leftarrow R_{\text{init}}$ and $\rho^{(k)} \leftarrow R(\hat\rho^{(k+1)})$, the whole sequence maps $2^n \to 2^{w_0}$ and satisfies, for all $f,g$, $\Pr_{X\sim\text{unif}}[f(X)\ne g(X)] = \Pr_Y[(\Psi f)(Y) \ne (\Psi g)(Y)]$ with $Y$ being $t_1$-biased (even $d$) or $(1-t_1)$-biased (odd $d$). The correlations each projection introduces are real, but they are absorbed: the biases $t_k$ and the all-$\circ$ event $\lambda$ are tuned so each block's mass telescopes back to a product, which is the maneuver HĂĄstad's blockwise restriction could not perform.

Property 1 is a switching lemma *for projections*. Following Razborov's encoding argument (in Beame's and Thapen's streamlined form), one analyzes $\mathrm{proj}_\rho F = \mathrm{proj}(F{\restriction}\rho)$ via a canonical projection decision tree $\text{ProjDT}(F{\restriction}\rho)$ that queries the *merged* variables $y_a$; a bad $\rho$ (deep path) is encoded injectively as $\theta(\rho) = (\rho\sigma, \pi', \mathrm{encode}(\eta), \mathrm{encode}(\gamma))$, where $\sigma$ satisfies the terms hit along the path and the convention "set the non-occurring alive coords to $0$" makes $\sigma$ undoable by the decoder without spending extra bits. The per-block weight ratio is $\zeta((\rho\sigma)(S_a))/\zeta(\rho(S_a)) = \Omega(w^{1/4})\cdot((1-t_k)/t_k)^{\Delta_a}$ â€” here the $w^{1/4}$ is exactly $\lambda/q^3$ â€” and stratifying the union bound over the Hamming weight of the new-$1$'s encoding gives $\sum_i \binom{rs}{i}(t_k/(1-t_k))^i = (1 + t_k/(1-t_k))^{rs} = (e^{r t_k/(1-t_k)})^s$. The result is $\Pr_{\rho\leftarrow R_{\text{init}}}[\mathrm{proj}_\rho F \text{ not a depth-}s\text{ DT}] = (O(r\cdot 2^r \cdot w^{-1/4}))^s$ and $\Pr_{\rho\leftarrow R(\tau)}[\cdots] = (O(r\cdot e^{r t_k/(1-t_k)} \cdot w^{-1/4}))^s$. Run bottom-up (trimming too-wide bottom gates to a constant in the opposite-alternation case), $\Psi(C)$ becomes a depth-$n^{1/4(d-1)}$ decision tree (or $1/S$-close to a width-$n^{1/4(d-1)}$ CNF/DNF of matching top gate) with probability $1 - \exp(-\Omega(n^{1/6(d-1)}))$.

Property 2 is the lift fact $\mathrm{proj}_\rho \text{Sipser}^{(k)} \equiv \text{Sipser}^{(k-1)}{\restriction}\hat\rho$, so chaining gives $\Psi(\text{BalancedSipser}_d) \equiv \text{Sipser}_d^{(1)}{\restriction}\hat\rho^{(2)}$, a single OR/AND of fan-in $w_0$. To control all $d-1$ stages at once I use *typicality*: $\tau$ at level $k$ is typical if every block's alive set is $k$-acceptable and every lifted block keeps all but $\le w^{4/5}$ of its entries alive. Typicality holds initially and bootstraps from one level to the next, each w.p. $1 - e^{-\tilde\Omega(w^{1/6})}$ by Chernoff (this is where $\beta$ widening toward the root and $d \le c\log w/\log\log w$ are used), so all $d-1$ lifts are typical w.p. $1 - d\cdot e^{-\tilde\Omega(w^{1/6})}$. On that event the surviving top OR has $\approx q w_0$ live coords, and by the choice of $w_0$, $\mathbb{E}_\Psi[\mathrm{bias}(\text{Sipser}_d^{(1)}{\restriction}\hat\rho^{(2)}, Y)] \ge \tfrac12 - \tilde O(w^{-1/12})$. Finally, bottoming out: a base-case lemma (extending O'Donnellâ€“Wimmer) shows a width-$r$ CNF $F$ and a restricted top $\text{OR}_S$ satisfy $\Pr_Y[\text{OR}_S(Y)\ne F(Y)] \ge \mathrm{bias}(\text{OR}_S, Y) - r t_1$ â€” either every clause has a negated $S$-literal so $F(0^S)=1$, or some clause is monotone so $\Pr[F=1] \le r t_1$. Assembling with $r = n^{1/4(d-1)}$ and $r t_1 = n^{1/4(d-1)}\cdot\tilde\Theta(w^{-1/2}) = n^{-\Omega(1/d)}$, the pieces multiply to $\Pr_X[\text{BalancedSipser}_d \ne C] \ge \tfrac12 - n^{-\Omega(1/d)}$. The opposite-alternation case is identical up to a negligible $1/S$. The worst-case depth hierarchy becomes an average-case one, the polynomial hierarchy is infinite relative to a random oracle, and no approximate converse to the total-influence bound can hold.

The finished result:

**Theorem (average-case depth hierarchy).** Let $2 \le d \le c\sqrt{\log n}/\log\log n$ for an absolute constant $c > 0$, and let $\text{BalancedSipser}_d$ be the explicit $n$-variable read-once monotone depth-$d$ formula defined above. Then any circuit $C$ of depth $\le d-1$ and size $\le S = 2^{n^{1/(6(d-1))}}$ agrees with $\text{BalancedSipser}_d$ on at most $(\tfrac12 + n^{-\Omega(1/d)})\cdot 2^n$ inputs.

This follows from two incomparable lower bounds, each of which already implies it (a depth-$(d-1)$ circuit is a depth-$d$ circuit with bottom fan-in $1$, and is also a depth-$d$ circuit; if its top alternation matches $\text{BalancedSipser}_d$ it falls under the first theorem, otherwise the second):

**Theorem (first main bound â€” small bottom fan-in).** For $2 \le d \le c\sqrt{\log n}/\log\log n$, any depth-$d$ circuit $C$ of size $\le S = 2^{n^{1/(6(d-1))}}$ and bottom fan-in $\le (\log n)/(10(d-1))$ satisfies $\Pr_X[\text{BalancedSipser}_d(X) \ne C(X)] \ge \tfrac12 - n^{-\Omega(1/d)}$ for $X$ uniform on $\{0,1\}^n$.

**Theorem (second main bound â€” opposite alternation).** For $2 \le d \le c\sqrt{\log n}/\log\log n$, any depth-$d$ circuit $C$ of size $\le S = 2^{n^{1/(6(d-1))}}$ whose top gate is opposite to $\text{BalancedSipser}_d$'s (OR vs AND) satisfies $\Pr_X[\text{BalancedSipser}_d(X) \ne C(X)] \ge \tfrac12 - n^{-\Omega(1/d)}$.

**Corollaries.** (1) With probability $1$, a random oracle $A$ satisfies $\Sigma_d^{P,A} \subsetneq \Sigma_{d+1}^{P,A}$ for all $d$ â€” the polynomial hierarchy is infinite relative to a random oracle. (2) There is a monotone $f$ with $\mathrm{Inf}(f) = O(\log n)$ that no depth-$d(n)$ circuit of size $\le \exp((\log n)^{\omega(1)})$ (with $d(n) = \omega(1)$) approximates to $\tfrac12 + o(1)$ â€” no approximate converse to the Linialâ€“Mansourâ€“Nisan / Boppana total-influence bound holds.

**Near-optimality.** For monotone $f$, Bshoutyâ€“Tamon forces correlation $\ge \tfrac12 + \Omega(1/n)$ with a single variable; and the Hajnal et al. discriminator lemma rules out a $d$-vs-$(d-1)$ hierarchy at correlation $\tfrac12 + n^{-\omega(1)}$. So $\tfrac12 + n^{-\Theta(1)}$ is best possible.

The proof, in four pieces:

**Property 3 â€” $\Psi$ completes to uniform.** For all $f, g$: $\Pr_{X\sim\text{unif}}[f(X) \ne g(X)] = \Pr_Y[(\Psi f)(Y) \ne (\Psi g)(Y)]$, where $Y$ is $t_1$-biased ($d$ even) or $(1-t_1)$-biased ($d$ odd). Proof: block by block, the choice of $t_{d-1}$ forces $\lambda + q\,t_{d-1} = p$, making both $\Pr[X_a = 1^m]$ and $\Pr[X_a = Z]$ equal $2^{-m}$ (uniform); the choice of $q_a$ forces $(1-t_k)^{|S_a|} = \lambda + q_a t_{k-1}$, making the stage regenerate the $t_k$-biased product from the $t_{k-1}$-biased one; induct from $k = 2$ up.

**Property 1 â€” approximator simplifies (projection switching lemma).** For a width-$r$ DNF/CNF $F$: $\Pr_{\rho\leftarrow R_{\text{init}}}[\mathrm{proj}_\rho F \text{ not a depth-}s\text{ DT}] = (O(r\cdot 2^r\cdot w^{-1/4}))^s$; and $\Pr_{\rho\leftarrow R(\tau)}[\mathrm{proj}_\rho F \text{ not a depth-}s\text{ DT}] = (O(r\cdot e^{r t_k/(1-t_k)}\cdot w^{-1/4}))^s$. Proof (Razborov/Beame/Thapen encoding, generalized to projections): the canonical projection decision tree $\text{ProjDT}(F{\restriction}\rho)$ queries the merged variables $y_a$; a bad $\rho$ (deep path) is encoded as $\theta(\rho) = (\rho\sigma, \pi', \mathrm{encode}(\eta), \mathrm{encode}(\gamma))$ where $\sigma$ satisfies the hit terms, the "set non-occurring alive coords to $0$" convention makes $\sigma$ undoable without extra bits, and $\theta$ is an injection. The weight ratio per differing block is $\zeta((\rho\sigma))/\zeta(\rho) = \Omega(w^{1/4})\cdot((1-t_k)/t_k)^{\Delta_a}$ (here $\lambda/q^3 = w^{1/4}$); summing over Hamming weight of the new-$1$'s encoding $\vartheta_4$ gives $\sum_i \binom{rs}{i}(t_k/(1-t_k))^i = (1+t_k/(1-t_k))^{rs} = (e^{r t_k/(1-t_k)})^s$; union over the small $\vartheta_2, \vartheta_3$ finishes. Applied bottom-up (with trimming of too-wide bottom gates in the opposite-alternation case): $\Psi(C)$ is a depth-$n^{1/4(d-1)}$ decision tree (or $(1/S)$-close to a width-$n^{1/4(d-1)}$ CNF/DNF of matching top gate) w.p. $1 - \exp(-\Omega(n^{1/6(d-1)}))$.

**Property 2 â€” target retains structure.** $\mathrm{proj}_\rho \text{Sipser}^{(k)} \equiv \text{Sipser}^{(k-1)}{\restriction}\hat\rho$ (lift fact), so chaining gives $\Psi(\text{BalancedSipser}_d) \equiv \text{Sipser}_d^{(1)}{\restriction}\hat\rho^{(2)}$ â€” a single OR/AND of fan-in $w_0$. With typicality (each block $k$-acceptable, $\ge w_{k-2} - w^{4/5}$ lifted survivors) which (i) holds initially and (ii) bootstraps (typical $\Rightarrow$ next typical) each w.p. $1 - e^{-\tilde\Omega(w^{1/6})}$ via Chernoff ($\beta(k,d)$ widens toward the root precisely to outrun the per-stage error, using $d \le c\log w/\log\log w$), all $d-1$ lifts are typical w.p. $1 - d\cdot e^{-\tilde\Omega(w^{1/6})}$. On that event the surviving top OR has $|S(\hat\rho)| \approx q w_0$ survivors, so by the choice of $w_0$, $\mathbb{E}_\Psi[\mathrm{bias}(\text{Sipser}_d^{(1)}{\restriction}\hat\rho^{(2)}, Y)] \ge \tfrac12 - \tilde O(w^{-1/12})$.

**Bottoming out.** Base-case correlation lemma (extending O'Donnellâ€“Wimmer): a width-$r$ CNF $F$ and a restricted top $\text{OR}_S$ satisfy $\Pr_Y[\text{OR}_S(Y) \ne F(Y)] \ge \mathrm{bias}(\text{OR}_S, Y) - r t_1$ (two cases: every clause has a negated $S$-literal $\Rightarrow F(0^S)=1$; else a monotone clause $\Rightarrow \Pr[F=1] \le r t_1$). Assembling, with $r = n^{1/4(d-1)}$:
$$\Pr_X[\text{BalancedSipser}_d \ne C] = \mathbb{E}_\Psi\big[\Pr_Y[(\text{OR}{\restriction}\hat\rho^{(2)})(Y) \ne (\Psi C)(Y)]\big]$$
$$\ge \mathbb{E}_\Psi[\mathrm{bias}(\text{OR}{\restriction}\hat\rho^{(2)}, Y)] - r t_1 - \Pr_\Psi[\Psi(C)\text{ not a depth-}r\text{ DT}]$$
$$\ge (\tfrac12 - \tilde O(w^{-1/12})) - r t_1 - \exp(-\Omega(n^{1/6(d-1)})) \ge \tfrac12 - n^{-\Omega(1/d)},$$
since $r t_1 = n^{1/4(d-1)}\cdot\tilde\Theta(w^{-1/2}) = n^{-\Omega(1/d)}$. The opposite-alternation case is identical up to an extra negligible $1/S$ term. $\blacksquare$
