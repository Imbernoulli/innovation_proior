## The pain point (research question)
Find the best material LAYOUT (topology) — where to put solid vs void in a fixed domain Ω — to minimize compliance (maximize stiffness) of a linearly elastic structure under given loads/supports, subject to a volume budget. Unlike sizing/shape optimization, topology is free: holes can appear/merge. The honest design variable is a 0/1 (solid/void) indicator per point → integer program with ~N variables (N = #elements, thousands). Two problems: (1) combinatorial intractability of 0/1; (2) the continuum 0/1 problem is mathematically ill-posed — no optimal solution exists; minimizing sequences develop ever-finer microstructure (chattering), so finer meshes give different, finer designs (mesh dependence) and checkerboards.

## Discovery chain (the spine of reasoning.md)
1. **0/1 integer is intractable** → relax to continuous density ρ_e∈[0,1] per element. Now gradients exist, can use FE sensitivities + continuous optimizer.
2. **But relaxation gives grey.** If stiffness is LINEAR in density, E(ρ)=ρE0, intermediate ρ is fully "paid for" by the volume it uses — grey is as economical as black, optimizer happily fills the domain with mush. Linear interpolation is exactly the homogenization-limit composite (rank-laminates realize it), so grey is physical and there's no incentive to resolve to 0/1. (This is the Bendsøe-Kikuchi 1988 homogenization route's signature: optimal designs are full of grey microstructure.)
3. **Penalize grey via a power law.** Make stiffness grow SLOWER than volume at low density: E(ρ)=ρ^p E0, p>1. Then volume cost ∝ ρ but stiffness benefit ∝ ρ^p < ρ. A half-dense element costs half the budget but delivers (1/2)^p << 1/2 of the stiffness → uneconomical → optimizer drives ρ→0 or 1. Penalty is implicit (no explicit penalty term), just an interpolation choice. SIMP.
4. **Which p?** Not arbitrary. Want intermediate ρ to be genuinely unphysical so penalizing it isn't cheating. Compare SIMP stiffness to the Hashin–Shtrikman upper bound on the stiffness of ANY isotropic two-phase (solid+void) composite of density ρ. If SIMP stiffness ≤ H-S upper bound for all ρ, then every intermediate-density point corresponds to a realizable composite (a microstructure exists with at least that stiffness), and the optimizer is correctly told grey is dominated. Condition (Bendsøe): p ≥ max{4/(1+ν), 2/(1−ν)} in 2-D; for ν=1/3, =3. So p=3 with ν=1/3 is the smallest p making SIMP physically consistent with composites. (p too small → SIMP above H-S, penalization is unphysical / weak; p large → strong penalization but very nonconvex, more local minima → continuation in p.)
5. **Modified SIMP.** Replace E=ρ^p E0 by E=Emin+ρ^p(E0−Emin), Emin~1e-9. Keeps K non-singular at ρ=0 (void), keeps sensitivity nonzero at ρ=0, and decouples penalization from the filter (lets density become exactly 0).
6. **The objective + FE.** Discretize Ω with square bilinear Q4 elements, 2 dof/node. KU=F, c=U^TKU=F^TU. With SIMP, K(ρ)=Σ E(ρ_e) k0 (element). lk() gives k0 for unit-E plane-stress Q4 (ν=0.3).
7. **Sensitivity (self-adjoint).** Need dc/dρ_e. Augment: c=F^TU − λ^T(KU−F). dc/dρ = (∂F/∂ρ − λ^T ∂K/∂ρ)U + (F^T − λ^T K)∂U/∂ρ. Kill the ∂U/∂ρ term (unknown, expensive) by choosing adjoint λ: K λ = F. But compliance is self-adjoint: F=KU ⇒ λ=U. With F independent of ρ: dc/dρ_e = −U^T (∂K/∂ρ_e) U = −U_e^T (dE/dρ_e) k0 U_e = −p ρ_e^{p-1}(E0−Emin) u_e^T k0 u_e ≤ 0. Always negative: more material always lowers compliance — so the volume constraint is always active.
8. **The optimizer: OC.** KKT for min c s.t. Σρ_e v_e=V*, 0≤ρ≤1. Lagrangian stationarity (interior): −∂c/∂ρ_e = λ ∂V/∂ρ_e ⇒ B_e := (−∂c/∂ρ_e)/(λ ∂V/∂ρ_e) = 1 at optimum. Heuristic fixed-point multiplicative update: ρ_e^new = ρ_e B_e^η (η=1/2 damping), clipped to box and a move limit m (=0.2). B_e>1 ⇒ stiffness payoff exceeds the "price" λ of material ⇒ add material; B_e<1 ⇒ remove. λ chosen so volume constraint holds → monotone in λ ⇒ bisection. η<1 damps the multiplicative step (η=1 oscillates); move limit caps per-step change. (MMA = Svanberg 1987 is the general-purpose convex-separable-approximation alternative; OC is the cheap special-purpose update for this single-constraint self-adjoint problem.)
9. **Checkerboard / mesh dependence wall.** Raw OC on the relaxed+penalized problem reproduces the ill-posedness: alternating solid/void "checkerboard" patches appear (they're an FE artifact — Q4 checkerboards are artificially stiff), and refining the mesh gives finer, different structures (no length scale). Penalization made it worse (drives to 0/1 at the finest scale).
10. **Fix: filtering = impose a length scale r_min.** Either (a) sensitivity filter: replace dc/dρ_e by a weighted neighborhood average (Sigmund 1994/1997) — heuristic, kills checkerboards, mesh-independent. Or (b) density filter (Bourdin 2001; Bruns-Tortorelli 2001): physical density ρ̃_e = (Σ H_ei ρ_i)/(Σ H_ei), H_ei=max(0,r_min−dist(e,i)); analyze ρ̃, chain-rule the sensitivities. Filter radius r_min sets the minimum feature size, restores well-posedness/existence.
11. **Land on code.** 99-line (sensitivity filter, classical SIMP) → 88-line (vectorized, +density filter, modified SIMP) → 165-line Python port. Continuation: optionally ramp p (1→3) to avoid poor local minima.

## Design-decision → why table
- relax {0,1}→[0,1]: integer intractable + need gradients. (alt: branch&bound — hopeless at N~10^4.)
- power law ρ^p not linear ρ: linear leaves grey economical (=composite); power makes grey uneconomical (stiffness<<volume). (alt: explicit penalty term — extra weight to tune, nonconvex; SIMP folds penalty into interpolation.)
- p=3 (with ν=1/3): smallest p with SIMP ≤ H-S upper bound (Eq 15: p≥max{4/(1+ν),2/(1−ν)}=3), so grey ↔ realizable composite. (alt p<3: unphysical penalization; p>>3: stronger 0/1 but more local minima → continuation.)
- Emin (modified SIMP): nonsingular K and nonzero sensitivity at ρ=0; allows ρ=0 exactly; clean filter coupling. (alt classical: lower-bound ρ≥ρmin>0 — messier.)
- self-adjoint adjoint λ=U: compliance F^TU, adjoint RHS=F=KU ⇒ λ=U; one free FE sensitivity, no extra solve. 
- OC multiplicative B_e^η: KKT optimality B_e=1; multiplicative fixed point converges fast for this monotone problem; cheaper than MMA for 1 constraint. (alt: MMA Svanberg 1987 — general but heavier.)
- η=1/2 damping: η=1 multiplicative update over/undershoots & oscillates; η<1 stabilizes.
- move limit m=0.2: caps per-iteration change so linearized step stays valid.
- λ by bisection: Σρ̃ monotone decreasing in λ ⇒ 1-D root find; l1=0,l2=1e9.
- filter r_min: relaxed+penalized problem is ill-posed → checkerboards + mesh dependence; filter imposes length scale, restores existence & mesh-independence. sensitivity filter (heuristic, robust) vs density filter (consistent, physical density, paves way for projection/Heaviside).
- Q4 plane-stress element, ν=0.3, KE from lk(): standard bilinear unit-E stiffness; regular mesh ⇒ one KE for all elements ⇒ cheap assembly.
- MBB half-beam BCs: symmetry (roller on left edge x-dofs) + bottom-right vertical roller, unit downward load at top-left — the classic test (settings only).
