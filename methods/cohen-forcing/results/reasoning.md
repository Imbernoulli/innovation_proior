I start from the side that is already understood. If I pass to the constructible sets, I get a model in which choice and the generalized continuum hypothesis hold. That proves that the continuum hypothesis is not refutable from the remaining axioms, provided those axioms are consistent. But it also shows the shape of the missing half: I need a model that keeps the axioms and choice while making the continuum larger.

I cannot get that by going inward again. An inner model removes sets; it does not manufacture the many new subsets of omega that would make `2^aleph_0` large. So I need an outer construction. The naive move is to attach a new subset of omega to a ground model, but that is too uncontrolled. A completed external subset may encode facts about the ground model that the ground model cannot see. If I add such a set and close under definability, I have no reason to believe Replacement, Power Set, or even the intended cardinal structure will survive.

So I stop trying to choose the new real directly. I choose only finite information. A condition is a finite, contradiction-free set of decisions of the form `n in a_delta` or `not n in a_delta`. Stronger conditions add more decisions. This is small enough that the ground model can reason about it, and flexible enough that no single finite condition smuggles in a completed outside code.

Now I need to say what a finite condition already guarantees. I define a relation `P forces phi` inside the ground model, by recursion on formulas and on the generated names. For a universal statement, `P` forces it when no stronger condition can force a counterexample. For an existential statement, `P` forces it when some named witness is already forced to work. For atomic membership statements between generated objects, I reduce the question to the recursive definitions of those objects. This is the point where the future extension becomes a syntactic object the old model can audit.

I check that this relation behaves like truth should behave before I build the final universe. A condition cannot force both a statement and its negation. If a condition forces something, every stronger condition also forces it. Most importantly, every condition can be strengthened to decide any given statement. This decision property is the engine: it turns the countability of the ground model into a way to meet every requirement.

Because the ground model is countable from the outside, I can enumerate its statements and relevant ordinals. I build a sequence of stronger finite conditions. At one step I decide the next statement; at another I add the least possible membership witness when one is needed. The union of this sequence determines the new subsets `a_delta` of omega. They are not arbitrary; they are exactly what survives all the old model's decision requirements.

The generated universe is built from names rather than from a vague phrase like "the old model plus the new reals." Each object is one of the recursively generated `F_alpha` objects interpreted through the chosen membership decisions. I then prove the truth lemma: a statement is true in the generated universe exactly when some condition in the construction forces it. This is the bridge I need. It lets every semantic assertion about the extension be pulled back to a finite condition and a recursive definition in the ground model.

The construction is still not finished, because changing the universe is easy compared with preserving the axioms. Replacement could fail if the image of a set under a definable function wanders through unboundedly many new stages. I handle this by showing that any single-valued definition can be bounded: for arguments from a fixed generated set, suitable witnesses live below a fixed generated rank. Power Set is similar but more delicate. I control incompatible families of conditions and close index sets under the operations used in the construction, so subsets of a generated set appear by a bounded stage.

Choice also has to be earned. Since the construction starts over a constructible ground model and carries a definable ordering of the generated objects, I can define a well-order of the extension. So the extension satisfies choice. I also need the old cardinals used to measure the continuum not to collapse. The ordinal comparison lemmas make sure that the relevant cardinals retain their roles in the extension.

Now the continuum calculation becomes honest. I arrange distinct added subsets `a_delta` of omega for `delta < aleph_tau` in the ground model. No finite condition can force two of them to be equal, and the construction meets the requirements that make them distinct. Thus the extension has at least `aleph_tau` many reals. The power-set analysis gives the matching upper bound in the cases Cohen isolates, so with the right parameter the extension satisfies choice and `2^aleph_0 = aleph_2`, hence not-CH.

The method I arrive at is therefore not just adding sets. It is a controlled extension procedure: finite conditions, generic completion by meeting all old requirements, names for all new objects, and a forcing relation that converts finite evidence into truth in the extension. Once this is in place, independence is no longer a search for a hidden proof in one universe. It is a construction of alternative universes that preserve the same axioms while changing the target statement.
