14.1       Online Bipartite Matching
Input: Unweighted Bipartite Graph G = (L ∪ R; E); E ⊆ L × R


   • L is known ahead of time
   • R is revealed one vertex at a time
   • When v ∈ R arrives, we see all edges adjacent to v
   • Algorithm needs to commit to a match for v before observing any future arrivals

Goal: Pick a matching of maximum size.

14.4     The RANKING algorithm
In [1], the authors give an algorithm with an expected competitive ration of 1 − 1/e ≈ 0.63 for
solving the online bipartite matching.

RANKING(Karp, Vazirani, Vazirani ’90):

  1. Pick a uniformly random permutation for nodes

  2. When v ∈ R, match it to highest ranked unmatched neighbor.

Theorem 1: The Competitive Ration of RANKING is 1 − 1/e ≈ 0.63.
Theorem 2: No randomized algorithm can do better.
