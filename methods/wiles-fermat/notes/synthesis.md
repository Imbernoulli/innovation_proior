# Synthesis — Wiles, modularity of semistable elliptic curves / FLT

## The discovery path AS WILES TELLS IT (backbone of reasoning.md)
Source: Wiles's own Introduction to the 1995 Annals paper (pp. 449-454, the "account of the
origins of this work") + NOVA interview + Singh/BBC Sept-19-1994 quote. The Annals introduction
is itself a first-person retrospective ("I began working on these problems in the late summer of
1986...") — an unusually explicit self-account inside the primary source.

1. MOTIVE. Childhood: FLT found in a library book at age 10. Sets it aside as a researcher because
   "the only techniques we had to tackle it had been around for 130 years" (Kummer regular primes,
   cyclotomic fields) — not getting to the root.
2. THE ROUTE OPENS (1986). Frey (1985): a counterexample a^p+b^p=c^p gives the elliptic curve
   y^2 = x(x-a^p)(x+b^p). Serre's epsilon conjecture + Ribet's theorem (summer 1986): this Frey
   curve, if it were modular, would force a weight-2 newform of level 2; but S_2(Gamma_0(2))=0
   (X_0(2) has genus 0). So a Fermat counterexample would be NON-modular. Hence: prove every
   semistable elliptic curve over Q is modular => no such curve => FLT. "I was electrified."
3. THE REFRAME. Don't study the elliptic curve E directly; study its Galois representations
   rho_{E,p}: Gal(Qbar/Q) -> GL_2(Z_p) on p-power torsion. E modular <=> rho_{E,p} modular.
4. FIRST BREAKTHROUGH (the choice of p=3). Tried 2-adic first (would directly fit Frey curves);
   stuck. Switched to 3-adic: Langlands-Tunnell theorem => rho_{E,3} mod 3 (image in PGL_2(F_3)=S_4)
   is ALWAYS modular. So the residual rho_0 = rhobar_{E,3} is modular for free. Now the task is a
   LIFTING problem: show every lift of the modular residual rho_0 is itself modular. Prove it
   inductively for GL_2(Z/3^n) for all n.
5. THE NUMERICAL CRITERION / R=T. Mazur's deformation theory: there is a universal deformation
   ring R_D parametrizing all lifts of rho_0 with prescribed ramification type D. The modular lifts
   are parametrized by a Hecke algebra T_D. There is a canonical surjection phi: R_D ->> T_D (every
   modular lift is a lift, so T_D is a quotient of the universal R_D). "All lifts are modular" <=>
   phi is an ISOMORPHISM, R_D = T_D. Mazur's conjecture (R=T) refines this.
   THE CRITERION (appendix, Props 1 & 2, grounded from the paper):
   - Prop 1: a surjection phi: R ->> T of complete local O-algebras with T/p_T = O is an iso if
     it induces an iso on reduced cotangent spaces p_R/p_R^2 = p_T/p_T^2 AND T is generated by r
     elements with r relations (r = min generators of p_T/p_T^2). I.e. count generators vs relations.
   - Prop 2 (the eta / numerical criterion): a Gorenstein O-algebra T with T/p_T=O is a complete
     intersection IFF l(p_T/p_T^2) = l(O/eta_T), where eta_T = beta(beta-hat(1)) is the congruence
     ideal measuring congruences between the modular form f and other forms. The two invariants
     "p/p^2" (counts deformations / the Selmer group) and "eta" (counts congruences / special L-value)
     coincide exactly when T is a complete intersection. This is the bridge between the two great
     traditions: deformations of Galois reps (cotangent side) <-> special values of L-functions
     (congruence/eta side).
   So: prove R=T by proving (a) T is a complete intersection and (b) the cotangent space (a Selmer
   group H^1_*) has order <= |O/eta| (the upper bound), matched by the lower bound |O/eta| <= Selmer
   from the congruence side. Equality => complete intersection => R=T => modularity of all lifts.
6. THE WALL (the engine to prove the bound). Need: |Sel| <= |O/eta| = bound on a generalized class
   number / Selmer group. Wiles first (1988-91) tried IWASAWA THEORY: in his proof of the main
   conjecture for totally real fields he had a technique of replacing the cyclotomic Z_p-tower by a
   sequence of auxiliary primes q_i = 1 mod p^{n_i}, n_i -> infinity. He wanted the projective limit
   of Hecke rings up the tower to be a POWER SERIES RING (then descend the bound). But translated
   into ring-theoretic language this "seemed to require unknown principles of base change" — exact
   relations between Hecke rings for different fields in the cyclotomic tower, not just up to torsion.
   He searched "without success for the key." WALL #1.
7. THE PIVOT (Aug 1991). Learns of Flach's construction (a first step toward an EULER SYSTEM,
   Kolyvagin-style). An Euler system would give the precise upper bound on the Selmer group directly.
   Looked "tailor made." He pivots, abandons the Iwasawa approach, spends 1991-93 extending Flach.
   Fall 1992: believes the Euler system is complete. May 1993: the 3-5 switch (families of elliptic
   curves sharing a common rho_{E,5}) handles the case rho_{E,3} reducible. June 21-23 1993:
   announces at Cambridge.
8. THE GAP (fall 1993). Katz (refereeing) pins it down: the Euler system construction extending
   Flach is INCOMPLETE / possibly flawed — it does not actually establish the upper bound on the
   Selmer group in the generality needed (the system isn't long enough; the bound fails for the
   needed cases). WALL #2 — the central one. A year of despair; Taylor joins Jan 1994; the p=2
   attempt reaches an impasse end of August 1994.
9. THE FIX (Sept 19, 1994). "I was sitting at my desk examining the Kolyvagin-Flach method... it
   was all I needed to make my original Iwasawa theory work from three years earlier." Concretely:
   the SPECIAL auxiliary primes he had found while extending Flach (the q = 1 mod p^n primes of
   Chapter 3, not the old q = 1 mod p primes) + de Shalit's generalization of Hida's theory + Galois
   duality could be used to GLUE the Hecke rings T_{D_n} at the auxiliary levels Q_n into a POWER
   SERIES RING O[[x_1,...,x_r]] in the limit. That power series ring is exactly the object the old
   Iwasawa approach needed and couldn't build. The patching (n generators, n relations at each
   finite level; pass to the limit) forces R = T to be a complete intersection. The Euler system's
   failure WAS the missing key: the special primes it produced are what make the patching work.
   This is the Taylor-Wiles patching argument, written up in [TW].
10. ASSEMBLY. R=T (minimal level) + level-raising/lowering induction (Chapter 2.3) propagates
    modularity from the minimal level to all levels => every lift of the modular rho_0 is modular =>
    every semistable E with rho_{E,3} irreducible is modular (Thm 0.3); the 3-5 trick removes the
    irreducibility hypothesis (Thm 0.4: all semistable E are modular) => with Frey/Ribet, FLT (Thm
    0.5: u^p+v^p+w^p=0 => uvw=0).

## Antecedents (load-bearing), grounded:
- Frey curve, discriminant 2^-8 (abc)^{2p}, conductor = rad(abc), semistable, rho on p-torsion
  unramified outside 2p, flat at p. (Ribet's theorem WebFetch + Wiles intro.)
- Serre epsilon conjecture (level-lowering) + Ribet 1986: modular Frey curve => weight-2 level-2
  newform; S_2(Gamma_0(2))=0 (X_0(2) genus 0); contradiction. Reduces FLT to semistable modularity.
- Taniyama-Shimura-Weil conjecture (1950s-60s, Weil 1967): every elliptic curve over Q is modular.
- Eichler-Shimura / Deligne: Galois reps attached to modular eigenforms (Theorem 0.1).
- Langlands-Tunnell: rho_0 with solvable (S_4/A_4/dihedral) projective image is modular -> gives the
  modular residual rep at p=3 for free.
- Mazur: deformation theory of Galois reps; universal deformation ring; Gorenstein Hecke rings;
  conjecture R=T in special cases. Hida, Ribet: congruences between modular forms of different levels
  (level-raising/lowering).
- Iwasawa theory (main conjecture, Mazur-Wiles), Wiles's own totally-real-fields main conjecture
  (the auxiliary-primes technique). Poitou-Tate duality in Galois cohomology (Selmer group relations).
- Flach / Kolyvagin Euler systems (Kolyvagin's bounding of Selmer/Sha; Flach's first step).
- Kunz, Tate (Grothendieck duality) -> the eta-invariant complete-intersection criterion (appendix).

## Final artifact to land on (no code): the PROOF STRATEGY stated precisely.
- The reduction chain Frey -> Ribet -> "semistable modularity => FLT".
- The reformulation E modular <=> rho_{E,p} modular; p=3 + Langlands-Tunnell to seed the residual.
- The modularity lifting theorem via R=T, with the appendix numerical criterion
  l(p_T/p_T^2) = l(O/eta_T) <=> complete intersection, and the Selmer = O/eta bound.
- The Taylor-Wiles patching (auxiliary primes Q_n, glue to power series ring) as the engine.
- The 3-5 trick to remove the irreducibility hypothesis.
