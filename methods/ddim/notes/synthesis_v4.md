# Synthesis (DDIM) — composing the deliverables FROM this file

Pre-Phase-2 understanding dump. The three deliverables transcribe from here. Method = a non-Markovian generalization of the DDPM forward process that keeps the SAME marginals q(x_t|x_0) (hence the SAME ε-MSE training objective and a pretrained DDPM net), but admits a family of reverse/generative processes indexed by σ; the σ=0 member is fully deterministic (an implicit model), and because training is decoupled from the forward chain length, the same net can be sampled on a short sub-sequence τ for a 10–50× speedup. Name (DDIM) reserved for answer.md; context.md must NOT name it.

NOTATION WARNING (load-bearing): this work uses **α_t for the CUMULATIVE product** that DDPM calls ᾱ_t. So here α_t = ∏_{s≤t}(1−β_s), and q(x_t|x_0)=N(√α_t x_0,(1−α_t)I), x_t=√α_t x_0+√(1−α_t)ε. There is no separate per-step ᾱ vs α; one schedule α_{1:T} decreasing in (0,1], α_0:=1. Keep this convention throughout reasoning/answer for internal consistency; flag it once.

## The pain point (research question, in-frame, late 2020)
DDPM/NCSN match GAN sample quality without adversarial training, but sampling is brutally slow: the generative chain approximates the reverse of a forward chain with T≈1000 steps, and every step is one sequential forward pass of a big U-Net. Concretely ~20 hours to draw 50k 32×32 images vs <1 min for a GAN on the same GPU; ~1000 hours at 256×256. GANs sample in ONE pass. So: can we keep DDPM's training (likelihood-based, non-adversarial, stable, GAN-quality samples) but sample in 20–100 steps instead of 1000, WITHOUT retraining — ideally reusing already-trained DDPM checkpoints? Sub-questions that fall out: can the generative process be made deterministic (an implicit model like a GAN/flow) so x_T is a meaningful latent code admitting interpolation and encoding?

## Load-bearing ancestors (with the precise gap each leaves)

### Ho, Jain, Abbeel 2020 — DDPM (THE direct ancestor; reused wholesale)
Latent-variable model p_θ(x_0)=∫p_θ(x_{0:T})dx_{1:T}, x_1..x_T same dim as x_0, with a FIXED Gaussian forward Markov chain q(x_t|x_{t-1})=N(√(α_t/α_{t-1}) x_{t-1},(1−α_t/α_{t-1})I) [in this work's cumulative-α notation], closed-form marginal q(x_t|x_0)=N(√α_t x_0,(1−α_t)I). Trained by a variational bound that, after Rao-Blackwellization (conditioning the forward posterior on x_0) and the ε-prediction reparameterization, reduces to a weighted denoising MSE L_γ = Σ_t γ_t E[‖ε_θ(√α_t x_0+√(1−α_t)ε)−ε‖²], and Ho sets γ=1 (L_1) for best samples. KEY structural facts DDIM exploits: (a) the loss L_γ is a SUM OF PER-t TERMS, and if ε_θ does NOT share parameters across t, each term is optimized independently so the optimum is INDEPENDENT of the weights γ; (b) every term depends on q only through the MARGINAL q(x_t|x_0), never the joint q(x_{1:T}|x_0). Gap: the generative process is rigidly tied to reversing the full T-step Markov chain → T sequential steps to sample, and it is stochastic (each reverse step injects fresh noise σ_t z), so x_T is not a deterministic latent code.

### Sohl-Dickstein et al. 2015 — diffusion via nonequilibrium thermodynamics
Origin of the fixed-forward / learned-reverse skeleton and the variational bound; also the "large T keeps each reverse conditional near-Gaussian" justification that makes T large (≈ the slowness root cause). Gap inherited by DDPM: nothing forces the GENERATIVE chain to have the same length as the forward chain — but the Markovian framing makes that coupling feel mandatory.

### Song & Ermon 2019 — NCSN + annealed Langevin
The other slow sampler: learn the score at many noise scales, sample by annealed Langevin dynamics (many small steps). Same denoising-autoencoder-over-noise-levels objective as DDPM; same Langevin-style sampler. Gap that frames the contribution: BOTH DDPM and NCSN sample via something Langevin-like, and Langevin is a discretization of a gradient flow → many steps are intrinsic to that view. DDIM's escape: derive a sampler from a PURELY VARIATIONAL angle where Langevin's step-size restrictions are irrelevant.

### Vincent 2011 — denoising score matching
For Gaussian corruption, predicting the added noise ε equals (up to a positive scale) predicting the score ∇ log q_σ. Used in the appendix to show the deterministic DDIM ODE is the probability-flow ODE of a VE-SDE: ε_θ is a scaled score, so the DDIM ODE coincides with Song et al.'s probability-flow ODE.

### Mohamed & Lakshminarayanan 2016 — implicit generative models
"Implicit model" = samples are a deterministic pushforward of latent noise through a fixed procedure, no explicit per-step density needed (GANs are the canonical example). The σ=0 member is exactly this: x_0 is a deterministic function of x_T. Gives the name "implicit" and the expectation of GAN/flow-like latent-space interpolation.

### Chen et al. 2018 (Neural ODE) / Song et al. 2020 (Score SDE, concurrent) — ODE framing
Continuous-depth / probability-flow-ODE viewpoint. The accelerated deterministic update, rewritten in (x̄=x/√α, σ=√((1−α)/α)) coordinates, is exactly an Euler step of an ODE dx̄ = ε_θ(x̄/√(σ²+1)) dσ. Hence: fewer steps = coarser ODE discretization; "consistency" (same x_T → same high-level image regardless of #steps) is the ODE solution being approximately discretization-invariant; encoding x_0→x_T = running the ODE backward (like a flow). Song et al.'s probability-flow ODE is concurrent; DDIM's ODE is shown equivalent to the VE special case but takes Euler steps in dσ rather than dt.

## The full derivation chain (every step lived in reasoning.md)

1. **The only thing training pins down.** L_γ is a sum of per-t terms; with t-specific (non-shared) ε_θ each term is minimized independently → the minimizer is the SAME for every positive weight γ. And each term's only contact with q is through the marginal q(x_t|x_0)=N(√α_t x_0,(1−α_t)I). So a TRAINED ε_θ is committed ONLY to these marginals — NOT to the Markov joint, NOT to any particular generative chain. The Markovian forward process is one arbitrary joint with those marginals; any other joint sharing them is equally compatible with the same trained net. This is the whole opening.

2. **Build a non-Markovian forward family with the right marginals.** Want a family q_σ (indexed by σ∈R_{≥0}^T) of joints all having q_σ(x_t|x_0)=N(√α_t x_0,(1−α_t)I). Factor it "backwards" conditioned on x_0:
   q_σ(x_{1:T}|x_0) = q_σ(x_T|x_0) ∏_{t=2}^T q_σ(x_{t-1}|x_t,x_0),
   with q_σ(x_T|x_0)=N(√α_T x_0,(1−α_T)I) and the reverse-conditional Gaussian ansatz
   q_σ(x_{t-1}|x_t,x_0)=N(√α_{t-1} x_0 + √(1−α_{t-1}−σ_t²)·(x_t−√α_t x_0)/√(1−α_t), σ_t² I).   (★)
   WHY this exact mean form: it is forced by demanding the marginal stay N(√α_{t-1} x_0,(1−α_{t-1})I) — derive by undetermined coefficients (next step). σ_t² is a FREE per-step variance: it is the one degree of freedom left after pinning the marginals.

3. **Lemma (marginal consistency) — must derive by induction.** Claim q_σ(x_t|x_0)=N(√α_t x_0,(1−α_t)I) for all t. Induct downward from t=T (base holds by construction). Given q_σ(x_t|x_0)=N(√α_t x_0,(1−α_t)I) and (★), marginalize x_t: q_σ(x_{t-1}|x_0)=∫q_σ(x_t|x_0)q_σ(x_{t-1}|x_t,x_0)dx_t. This is a linear-Gaussian marginalization (Bishop 2.115). Mean: substitute E[x_t]=√α_t x_0 into the mean of (★) → √α_{t-1}x_0 + √(1−α_{t-1}−σ_t²)·(√α_t x_0−√α_t x_0)/√(1−α_t) = √α_{t-1}x_0 (the correction term vanishes because the chosen mean is AFFINE in x_t with the x_0-anchored offset cancelling). Variance: σ_t² + [(1−α_{t-1}−σ_t²)/(1−α_t)]·(1−α_t) = σ_t² + (1−α_{t-1}−σ_t²) = 1−α_{t-1}. ✓ So every q_σ in the family has the DDPM marginals, hence the SAME L_γ, hence a trained DDPM net solves ALL of them. WHY the coefficient √(1−α_{t-1}−σ_t²)/√(1−α_t): it is precisely what makes the variance bookkeeping close to 1−α_{t-1}; the (x_t−√α_t x_0) term is a unit-ish "direction" (it's √(1−α_t)ε in distribution) so dividing by √(1−α_t) normalizes it, then re-scaling by √(1−α_{t-1}−σ_t²) supplies exactly the leftover variance not accounted for by σ_t².

4. **Generative process from the family (the "predict x_0, then step back" move).** We don't know x_0 at sample time, but the net gives a prediction. From x_t=√α_t x_0+√(1−α_t)ε invert: predicted-x_0 f_θ(x_t)=(x_t−√(1−α_t)ε_θ(x_t))/√α_t. Define p_θ(x_{t-1}|x_t)=q_σ(x_{t-1}|x_t, f_θ(x_t)) for t>1 (and N(f_θ(x_1),σ_1²I) for t=1, to keep support everywhere). Sampling step = plug f_θ into (★):
   x_{t-1}=√α_{t-1}·f_θ(x_t) + √(1−α_{t-1}−σ_t²)·ε_θ(x_t) + σ_t ε,  with (x_t−√α_t f_θ)/√(1−α_t)=ε_θ(x_t) by definition of f_θ.   (12)
   Three named pieces: "predicted x_0" (√α_{t-1}f_θ), "direction pointing to x_t" (√(1−α_{t-1}−σ_t²)ε_θ), "random noise" (σ_t ε).

5. **Unification theorem J_σ = L_γ + C — must derive (appendix).** The variational objective for this generative process, J_σ = E_{q_σ}[log q_σ(x_{1:T}|x_0) − log p_θ(x_{0:T})], reduces (drop θ-independent entropies, ≡) to Σ_{t>1} E[KL(q_σ(x_{t-1}|x_t,x_0)‖p_θ(x_{t-1}|x_t))] − E[log p_θ(x_0|x_1)]. Both arguments of each KL are Gaussians with the SAME variance σ_t²I (p_θ just substitutes f_θ for x_0 in (★)), so KL = ‖mean diff‖²/(2σ_t²). The means differ only in the x_0-slot: difference = √α_{t-1}(x_0−f_θ) + √(1−α_{t-1}−σ_t²)/√(1−α_t)·(−√α_t)(x_0−f_θ)... — but the cleaner route the appendix takes: the mean (★) is affine in x_0, and since BOTH the x_0-coefficient parts share the SAME structure, KL ≡ ‖x_0−f_θ(x_t)‖²/(2σ_t²) (the √(1−α_{t-1}−σ_t²) cross-term cancels because it multiplies (x_t−√α_t·)/√(1−α_t) identically in both means and the x_t part is shared). Then x_0−f_θ = [(x_t−√(1−α_t)ε)−(x_t−√(1−α_t)ε_θ)]/√α_t = √(1−α_t)/√α_t·(ε−ε_θ)... actually = (√(1−α_t)/√α_t)(ε_θ−ε) up to sign; norm² = (1−α_t)/α_t·‖ε−ε_θ‖². Per-dimension d: J_σ term ≡ ‖ε−ε_θ‖²/(2 d σ_t² α_t)·(1−α_t)... — match to L_γ gives γ_t = 1/(2 d σ_t² α_t) and the (1−α_t) gets absorbed; the appendix's stated result is γ_t=1/(2dσ_t²α_t). Net: J_σ = L_γ + C with γ_t=1/(2dσ_t²α_t), C θ-independent. (Re-derive carefully in reasoning; the exact factor matters less than: (i) Gaussian KL → mean-MSE because variances are equal-by-construction, (ii) mean-MSE in x_0 → ε-MSE via the reparam invert, (iii) result is L_γ for SOME γ.) Combined with fact 1 (optimum independent of γ when params not shared across t): the optimum of J_σ = optimum of L_1 for EVERY σ. So L_1 (the plain DDPM loss) is a valid surrogate for every member of the family → one trained net, infinitely many samplers.

6. **DDIM as σ→0 (the deterministic member).** σ_t=0 ∀t kills the random-noise term in (12): x_{t-1}=√α_{t-1}f_θ(x_t)+√(1−α_{t-1})ε_θ(x_t). Now x_0 is a DETERMINISTIC function of x_T (except the supporting noise at t=1, which →0 as σ_1→0). This is an implicit probabilistic model. Consequences: x_T is a fixed latent code → semantic interpolation by slerp in x_T; deterministic so a sample is reproducible; "consistency" = same x_T gives the same high-level image at any chain length. WHY deterministic helps few-step sampling: with no per-step noise injection there is no stochastic error to average down with many steps; the map is an ODE flow and fewer steps just coarsen the discretization rather than leaving residual noise.

7. **Recover DDPM as a special σ.** Setting σ_t=√((1−α_{t-1})/(1−α_t))·√(1−α_t/α_{t-1}) makes the forward process Markovian again and (12) becomes the DDPM ancestral step. So the family is a true generalization: DDPM and DDIM are the σ=σ_DDPM and σ=0 endpoints. Introduce η to slide between: σ_t(η)=η·σ_DDPM,t, η=1→DDPM, η=0→DDIM. (Also σ̂ = √(1−α_t/α_{t-1}), the "fixedlarge" DDPM variance, even more stochastic — note it degrades fast on short trajectories, motivating low η.)

8. **Accelerated sampling on a sub-sequence τ — must derive (appendix).** Because L_1 depends only on the marginals {q(x_t|x_0)}, we can DEFINE the forward/generative process on only a sub-sequence τ=(τ_1<...<τ_S) of [1..T] (plus the leftover indices in a star graph used only in the objective, not for sampling). The reverse conditional (★) is reused with (t,t−1)→(τ_i,τ_{i-1}); the same marginal-consistency lemma and J=L_γ argument go through (appendix factorization q_{σ,τ}=q_{σ,τ}(x_{τ_S}|x_0)∏q_{σ,τ}(x_{τ_{i-1}}|x_{τ_i},x_0)∏_{t∈τ̄}q(x_t|x_0)). So with NO retraining we sample only S≪T steps. The per-step update (closed form, appendix):
   x_{τ_{i-1}} = √α_{τ_{i-1}}·(x_{τ_i}−√(1−α_{τ_i})ε_θ)/√α_{τ_i} + √(1−α_{τ_{i-1}}−σ_{τ_i}²)·ε_θ + σ_{τ_i}ε,
   σ_{τ_i}(η)=η√((1−α_{τ_{i-1}})/(1−α_{τ_i}))√(1−α_{τ_i}/α_{τ_{i-1}}). τ chosen linear (τ_i=⌊ci⌋) or quadratic (τ_i=⌊ci²⌋); quadratic for CIFAR10, linear elsewhere.

9. **The ODE / Euler view — must derive (main text + appendix).** Take η=0, write (12) for a small step and divide by √α_{t-1}:
   x_{t-Δt}/√α_{t-Δt} = x_t/√α_t + (√((1−α_{t-Δt})/α_{t-Δt}) − √((1−α_t)/α_t))·ε_θ(x_t).
   Reparameterize x̄=x/√α, σ=√((1−α)/α) (so α=1/(1+σ²), x=x̄/√(σ²+1)). Then this is x̄(t−Δt)=x̄(t)+(σ(t−Δt)−σ(t))ε_θ(x̄/√(σ²+1)); divide by −Δt, Δt→0:
   dx̄ = ε_θ(x̄/√(σ²+1)) dσ.   (ODE)
   So DDIM with η=0 is Euler integration of this ODE in σ; init x̄(T)~N(0,σ(T)²I) with σ(T) large (α≈0). Run backward (t:0→T) to ENCODE x_0→x_T. Proposition: with the optimal ε_θ, this ODE = the probability-flow ODE of the VE-SDE (Song et al. 2020). Proof sketch (appendix, derive): VE probability flow dx̄=−½g(t)²∇log p_t dt, g²=dσ²/dt; DSM gives ∇log p_t = −ε_θ(x̄/√(σ²+1))/σ; substitute → dx̄=½(dσ²/dt)(ε_θ/σ)dt = (dσ/dt)ε_θ dt = ε_θ dσ. Same ODE. But DDIM Euler-steps in dσ, Song et al. in dt → differ at few steps (the Euler-in-dσ update has coefficient (√((1−α_{t-Δt})/α_{t-Δt})−√((1−α_t)/α_t)); the dt one has ½((1−α_{t-Δt})/α_{t-Δt}−(1−α_t)/α_t)√(α_t/(1−α_t))), agree as α_t,α_{t-Δt}→ close.

10. **Discrete (categorical) generalization — appendix, mention.** The non-Markovian-with-shared-marginals trick is not Gaussian-specific. For one-hot x_0: q(x_t|x_0)=Cat(α_t x_0+(1−α_t)1_K), q(x_{t-1}|x_t,x_0)=Cat(σ_t x_t+(α_{t-1}−σ_t α_t)x_0+((1−α_{t-1})−(1−α_t)σ_t)1_K), reverse p_θ replaces x_0 by f_θ. KL between categoricals, convexity bound → a multiclass-classification loss whose optimum is again σ-independent up to reweighting. Shows the contribution is the MARGINAL-PRESERVING non-Markovian construction, not the Gaussian instantiation.

## Design-decision → WHY table (rejected alternatives + failure modes)
- **Generalize the FORWARD process (not invent a new sampler ad hoc).** WHY: training only constrains the marginals; rebuilding the inference joint while preserving marginals is the principled way to get a new sampler that the SAME trained net already optimizes. Ad-hoc sampler surgery (e.g. just take bigger Langevin steps) breaks the objective↔sampler link and degrades fast.
- **Condition the reverse-conditional on x_0, factor backwards.** WHY: gives a closed-form Gaussian whose mean we can tune by undetermined coefficients to hold the marginal fixed; a plain non-Markovian forward q(x_t|x_{t-1},x_0) would not obviously preserve marginals.
- **Leave σ_t free, set the rest by marginal-matching.** WHY: σ_t is the single residual DOF after pinning marginals; it is exactly the knob that trades stochasticity (η) without touching training.
- **ε-prediction parameterization of f_θ (reuse DDPM's).** WHY: makes J_σ collapse to ε-MSE = L_1, so a pretrained DDPM checkpoint is literally a solution; predicting x_0 or the mean directly would still work in principle but loses the exact reuse of existing nets and the clean γ-independence argument.
- **No parameter sharing assumption for the γ-independence claim.** WHY: only when each per-t term is independently minimizable does the optimum become weight-independent; in practice one shared net is used, and the claim is the theoretical justification that L_1 is a sound surrogate.
- **σ=0 (DDIM) for the headline sampler.** WHY: deterministic ⇒ no per-step noise to average down ⇒ few steps suffice; ⇒ x_T is a usable latent (interpolation, encoding); ⇒ consistency. η>0 adds stochasticity that, on SHORT trajectories, injects noise the few remaining steps cannot clean up → worse FID (σ̂ worst).
- **Sub-sequence τ, length S≪T.** WHY: the objective is forward-length-agnostic (marginals only), so sampling length is a free choice decoupled from training length — the direct cure for the slowness.
- **Quadratic vs linear τ spacing.** WHY: quadratic puts more steps at low t (fine denoising) where the ODE curvature is higher; empirically best on CIFAR10, linear elsewhere. (stated as a choice, small effect.)
- **Euler steps in dσ, not dt.** WHY: dσ depends less on the arbitrary "time" parameterization; gives a more stable few-step discretization than the dt Euler step of the probability-flow ODE.

## Evaluation settings (pre-method facts only; NO outcomes in context)
Datasets: CIFAR10 32×32, CelebA 64×64, LSUN Bedroom/Church 256×256. Metric: FID (Heusel 2017). ## Code grounding (final code mirrors ermongroup/ddim functions/denoising.py generalized_steps + runner sample_image)
generalized_steps(x, seq, model, b, eta): seq_next=[-1]+seq[:-1]; loop i,j over reversed(seq),reversed(seq_next): at=ᾱ at i, at_next=ᾱ at j (j=-1→ᾱ=1); et=model(xt,t); x0_t=(xt−et√(1−at))/√at; c1=eta·√((1−at/at_next)(1−at_next)/(1−at)) [=σ]; c2=√((1−at_next)−c1²); xt_next=√at_next·x0_t + c1·z + c2·et. Exactly (12) on τ. seq built: uniform skip=T//S, range(0,T,skip); or quad linspace(0,√(0.8T),S)². ddpm_steps is the η=1/fixedlarge variant. compute_alpha builds ᾱ from betas via cumprod with a leading 1 (so index −1→ᾱ=1=α_0). Training is unchanged DDPM ε-MSE.
