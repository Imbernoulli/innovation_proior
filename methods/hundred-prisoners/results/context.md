# Context

## Problem

One hundred prisoners, numbered $1$ through $100$, are given one chance at freedom. A room contains $100$ boxes labeled $1$ through $100$. The warden places the numbers $1$ through $100$ into the boxes, one distinct number per box, in a uniformly random order unknown to the prisoners.

The prisoners enter the room one at a time. Each prisoner may open at most $50$ boxes and inspect the numbers inside. After a prisoner leaves, the boxes are closed again exactly as they were before the next prisoner enters. The prisoners may agree on a strategy before anyone enters the room, but once the search begins there is no communication of any kind, and no prisoner learns what any other prisoner saw or did.

A prisoner succeeds if one of the boxes they open contains their own number. The whole group is freed if and only if all $100$ prisoners succeed; if even one prisoner fails, the whole group loses. What strategy should the prisoners agree on to maximize the probability that everyone is freed?
