## Key facts (grounded)
- Christofides: MST T (c(T) ≤ OPT), min matching M on odd-degree set O of T (c(M) ≤ OPT/2 via O-join LP with y=x/2), shortcut. 3/2 tight (Fig 2 dissertation: unit-weight gadget, MST forces all vertices odd, matching ≈ OPT/2; ratio → 3/2 - 2/n).
- Hardness 123/122 (KLS15). Best prior general = 3/2 for ~45 yrs.
- Held-Karp / subtour LP: min Σ c_e x_e ; x(δ(v))=2 ; x(δ(S))≥2 ∀S ; x≥0. Optimal LP cost ≤ OPT.
- Splitting trick: pick node u, split into u0,v0, add edge e0 with x=1, c=0 ⇒ WLOG x^0 has an edge e0 with x=1,c=0. Restrict x^0 to E = x, which lies in spanning tree polytope (Fact: sptreepolytope, via Edmonds spanning-tree polytope z(E)=|V|-1, z(E(S))≤|S|-1).
- λ-uniform / max-entropy distribution: P[T] ∝ ∏_{e∈T} λ_e ; maximizes entropy subject to marginals = x. Computable to (1±2^-n) marginals via MWU / interior point / ellipsoid (AGMOS10 = Asadpour et al; SV19). E[c(T)] = c(x) ≤ OPT.
- SR (Borcea–Brändén–Liggett 2009): generating polynomial real stable. λ-uniform ST distributions are SR. Closed under conditioning/projection/truncation/product. Negative association, stochastic dominance. Rank sequence = sum of independent Bernoullis ⇒ log-concave/unimodal.
- Even-vertex fact: P[δ(v)_T even] ≥ ½(1+e^-2) ≈ 0.43 (BS(q) with q=2: Fact:evensum gives ½(1+(1-2p)^n); q=2 ⇒ ≥ ½(1+e^-4)... thesis states 0.43 via ½(1-e^-2)? careful: actually P even for BS(2) ≥ ½(1+e^{-4})≈0.49? The thesis says ½(1-e^-2)≈0.43 — that's the "many even" intuition. Use the qualitative "constant prob even", and cite Corollary cor:bernoullisumeven: P[BS(q) even] ≤ ½(1+e^{-2q}) for q≤1.2.) -> I will state min(even,odd)=Ω(1) for q bounded in (0,2), grounded in Fact:evensum + cor.
- O-join LP (Edmonds–Johnson 1973): min c(y), y(δ(S))≥1 ∀S with |S∩O| odd, y≥0. Min O-join = min matching on O.
- Key reframing: only need to satisfy cut S when δ(S)_T is ODD. If δ(S)_T even, constraint auto-satisfied (Def: satisfied cuts).
- Slack vector plan: y_e = x_e/2 + s_e (in the proof they actually use z=(x+OPT)/2 and y=x/4+s, y*=1/4+s*; for the conceptual trace I use the cleaner x/2 + s framing then note the OPT-averaging refinement). Want E[s_e] < -ε β x_e.
- β choice: β = η/4.1 ensures far-from-min cuts (x(δ(S))>2(1+η)) stay satisfied even if every edge reduced.
- Simple-case computation (Example simple): top edge bundle e=(u,v) in degree cut S; reduce s_e = -η x_e when both δ(u)_T, δ(v)_T even ("happy"); else increase to fix odd cut. Using x(δ(S))=2 ⇒ T splits into independent trees on S and V∖S (Fact treeIndep / lem:treeconditioning). Then P[δ(u)_T odd | g reduced] ≤ 1 - O(ε) when x_u,x_v ∈ [ε,1-ε], because δ^→(u)_T = BS with mean in [1+ε,2-ε] ⇒ min(P even, P odd)=Ω(ε). With m_{e,u}x_u + m_{e,v}x_v = x_e and uniform reduction prob p: E[s_e] ≤ -pη x_e + (1-ε)pη x_e = -ε p η x_e < 0. ✓
- Walls / four anomalies (overview): (a) not all top edges are good — BAD edges exist, must be half-edges (x_e ≈ 1/2 ± ε_{1/2}); of two half-edge bundles sharing endpoint at least one is good; ≥3/4 of mass in any cut δ(u) is good ⇒ never reduce bad edges, still works. (b) bottom edges (inside triangles, x_f ≈ 1) — naive compensation at 2× rate ⇒ E[s_f]>0; fix: reduce bottom by β, top by τ=0.571β with τ<β; AND reduce bottom edge only when A_T=B_T=1 with marginals preserved (max-flow theorem, prob Ω(α^2)) ⇒ ∞-ideal, bottom-bottom example gives E[s_f] = pβ(-1 + 8/27 + 0.571) = -0.13 pβ < 0. (c) x_u close to 1 — pseudo-triangle / 2-1-1 reduction rule. (d) x_u close to 0 — modify matching, Z_u doubles match.
- Polygon/hierarchy reduction: start from z=(x+OPT)/2 not x ⇒ every η-near-min cut of z is a contiguous interval of the OPT cycle (since in OPT min cuts=2, non-min ≥4, averaging forces intervals). Cuts crossed on both sides: odd with prob O(η) ⇒ charge 2 OPT edges, O(η^2) each ⇒ reduces relevant cuts to O(n). Cuts crossed on ≤1 side: connected components = polygons; structural theorem "polygons look like cycles" (x(E(a_i,a_{i+1}))≈1, x(δ(a_i))≈2) ⇒ each polygon ≈ a triangle; hierarchy H = laminar family of degree cuts + triangle cuts. Triangle happy when A_T,B_T both odd ⇒ all 3 cuts even.
- Generalized Gurvits lemma (Thm gurvits): SR, m disjoint sets each expected 1 ⇒ P[∀ A_i=1] ≥ e^{-m}. Generalization: Σ|n_i-1|<1-ε ⇒ P[∀ A_i=n_i] ≥ f(ε,m), no dependence on n.
- Final constants: η ≤ 10^-12, 218η = ε_P/6, ε_P = 3.12×10^-16, β = η/4.1 = ε_P/5362.8 ⇒ ratio 3/2 - 3×10^-36.
- Caveat: uses OPT edges ⇒ NOT an integrality-gap bound (only algorithmic). ε absurdly small because 218η ≪ ε_P forces η tiny.
- Not aware of any instance where the analyzed algorithm exceeds 4/3 in expectation.

## Design-decision → why
- Random tree instead of MST: deterministic MST can be the worst tree (all-odd gadget); averaging over a large-support distribution dodges adversarial worst case. WHY this distribution: marginals = x ⇒ E[tree]=c(x)≤OPT (tree side free); among all such, MAX ENTROPY because (i) spreads mass widely so O varies, (ii) = λ-uniform = SR ⇒ negative dependence + the independent-trees-across-a-2-cut fact, which is what makes "even with constant prob" provable and tractable.
- Why marginals matter not just any convex combo: gives the per-edge handle to lower the O-join below x/2.
- Why x/2 baseline & only odd cuts: O-join feasibility only binds on odd cuts; even cuts free ⇒ all savings come from cuts that are even in the sample.
- Why near-min cuts only: β<η/4.1 ⇒ slack reduction can't break far-from-min cuts; only η-near-min cuts can become binding.
- Why z=(x+OPT)/2: turns arbitrary near-min cuts into OPT-cycle intervals ⇒ laminar/polygon structure; cost: must use OPT edges (kills integrality-gap).
- Why triangles/happy: a polygon behaves like a cycle; happy (both sides odd) ⇒ all internal cuts even, the clean reduction trigger.
- Why τ=0.571β (top<bottom reduction): balance so neither top nor bottom compensation flips the sign; with marginal preservation giving the 8/27 bound, -1+8/27+0.571<0.
- Why never reduce bad (half) edges: their two top cuts aren't simultaneously even w/ const prob; but rare (must be half-edges, ≥3/4 mass good) so good edges carry compensation.
