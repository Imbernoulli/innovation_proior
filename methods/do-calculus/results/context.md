## Research Situation

I observe a population generated by stable mechanisms, but the target question asks what would happen under an intervention. The data show how variables vary together when left alone. The causal question asks for the distribution of an outcome after one mechanism is externally fixed while the rest of the system continues to operate.

## Graphical Commitments

A directed graph can record which variables are direct parents of which other variables. Missing directed arrows stand for exclusion assumptions, and hidden common causes can be represented by explicit latent nodes or special confounding marks. Earlier graphical-model work supplies a way to read conditional independence from such a graph through path blocking.

## Existing Estimation Moves

Randomized experiments make intervention effects easy to estimate because treatment assignment is severed from its usual causes. In observational studies, analysts instead use regression, stratification, or matching, choosing covariates so as to block paths between treatment and outcome.

## The Two Notions

Conditioning on a variable selects units whose value already arose from the system. Setting a value replaces part of the system: the mechanism that ordinarily produces the variable is overridden while the rest continues. The graph encodes hidden confounding, mediation, and selection by colliders.

## Question

How can passive observational data together with explicit causal assumptions, recorded in a graph, determine an interventional quantity?
