# MAE — Synthesis Notes (Phase 1.5)

## The pain point at the time (2021)
- NLP solved data hunger via self-supervised pretraining: GPT (autoregressive) and BERT (masked autoencoding / masked LM). "Remove part of the data, predict it." Scales to 100B+ params.
- Vision pretraining in practice still dominantly **supervised**. ViT (Dosovitskiy 2021) showed Transformers work on images but are **data-hungry** — need JFT-300M (303M labeled images, mostly inaccessible) to beat CNNs; on ImageNet-1K alone ViT-L overfits and underperforms.
- The masked-autoencoding idea is *natural* in vision (denoising autoencoders predate BERT), and vision works (Vincent 2010, Context Encoder 2016) preceded BERT. Yet after BERT, masked autoencoding in vision **lagged** NLP. Central question: **what makes masked autoencoding different between vision and language?**

## Three diagnostic differences (the heart of the motivation — all pre-method facts)
1. **Architecture gap (now closed).** Convnets operate on regular grids; injecting "indicators" like mask tokens or positional embeddings into convs is awkward. ViT closed this gap — Transformers ingest tokens, mask tokens and positional embeddings are native. So architecture is no longer the obstacle.
2. **Information density.** Language is human-generated, highly semantic, information-dense. Masking ~15% of words → predicting them needs real language understanding. Images are natural signals with **heavy spatial redundancy** — a missing patch is recoverable from neighbors by interpolation/texture-extension, with little high-level understanding. So a BERT-style low masking ratio is a *trivial* task in vision (low-level extrapolation). Implication: must remove redundancy → mask a **very high** fraction of random patches so the hole can't be filled locally.
3. **Decoder role.** In language the decoder predicts **words** (rich semantics), and BERT's decoder can be a trivial MLP. In vision the decoder reconstructs **pixels** (low semantic level), so the decoder design matters: it sets the semantic level of the learned representation. A trivial decoder forces the encoder to carry pixel-level reconstruction info; a deeper decoder absorbs reconstruction specialization, freeing the encoder latent to be abstract.

## Load-bearing ancestors (each: contribution + limitation)
- **Denoising autoencoder (Vincent et al. 2008).** Corrupt input, reconstruct clean original; representation robust to corruption. MAE is a form of DAE. Limitation: classical DAE computes loss on **all** pixels and uses small/symmetric nets on convnets; doesn't exploit sparsity, low masking, no scalable Transformer.
- **Context Encoder (Pathak et al. 2016).** Convnet inpaints a large *contiguous* missing region, reconstruction + adversarial loss. Pioneered masked-image feature learning. Limitations: convnet (grid, can't drop tokens), single large block region, GAN training, representations lag supervised.
- **BERT (Devlin et al. 2019).** Masked LM: mask ~15% tokens, predict from bidirectional context; loss only on masked tokens; mask token [M] fed into the encoder. Trivial MLP decoder. The template MAE adapts — but the 15% ratio and encoder-side [M] are exactly what *don't* transfer to vision.
- **ViT (Dosovitskiy et al. 2021).** Image → 16×16 patches → linear projection + positional embedding → Transformer; class token for classification. Provides the backbone and the patch tokenization. Also ran a preliminary masked-patch-prediction self-supervised experiment (~50% masking, predict mean color), got ~79.9% ViT-B, still 4% behind supervised — masked modeling "didn't work as well" in vision. Data-hungry.
- **iGPT (Chen et al. 2020).** Autoregressive/BERT pretraining on **pixel sequences** (k-means 9-bit color palette). Heavy compute, low-res, predicts pixel tokens. Shows pixel prediction can learn features but expensive.
- **BEiT (Bao et al. 2021).** BERT-for-images: mask patches, predict **discrete dVAE visual tokens** (DALL-E tokenizer, 8192 vocab). Mask tokens fed into encoder; block-wise masking ~40%. Limitations: needs a separate dVAE pretraining stage (extra 250M-image data), tokenizer is a big conv net (overhead), encoder sees mask tokens (train/deploy gap + wasted compute), pixel target reported worse for BEiT.
- **Contrastive learning (SimCLR/MoCo/BYOL/DINO/MoCo v3).** Learn invariance between augmented views; heavy reliance on data augmentation; strong linear probing. Different direction — not reconstruction; saturates earlier with epochs; encoder sees ≥200% of patches per epoch.

## Design-decision → why table (with rejected alternatives)
1. **Random patch masking, uniform, without replacement.** Why random not block: random with high ratio removes redundancy everywhere, can't be solved by local extrapolation. Block-wise (BEiT) at 75% is too hard (blurry, higher loss, worse). Grid (keep 1 of 4) is too easy (sharp recon, lower loss, worse features). Uniform avoids center bias. Random sampling also enables the highest ratio → biggest speedup.
2. **Very high masking ratio (75%).** Why high: images redundant; low ratio = trivial interpolation. Optimal ratio surprisingly high (vs BERT 15%, vs prior vision 20–50%). Linear probing rises steadily with ratio to ~75% (gap up to ~20%); fine-tuning flat over 40–80%. Too high (>~85%) starts to lose. 75% also means encoder sees only 25% → big compute win. Forces holistic inference of object gestalt.
3. **Asymmetric encoder: encoder on visible patches only, no mask token.** Why drop [M] from encoder: (a) **compute** — encoder cost is quadratic in sequence length; processing 25% of tokens cuts FLOPs ~3.3×, wall-clock 2.8–4.1× and memory; (b) **accuracy / train-deploy gap** — if encoder ingests [M], it spends capacity on artificial mask tokens absent at deployment (uncorrupted images), dropping linear probing ~14%. Constrain encoder to always see real patches.
4. **Lightweight, separate decoder (8 blocks, 512-d, <10% per-token FLOPs vs ViT-L).** Why a real but small decoder: decoder only used in pretraining (discarded for recognition), so its design is independent of encoder. A single block is the *minimum* to propagate info from visible to mask tokens; works for fine-tuning. But linear probing needs a **deep** decoder: the last layers specialize to pixel reconstruction; a deep decoder absorbs that specialization so the encoder latent stays abstract (up to ~8% linear-probe gain). Narrow OK because pixel reconstruction is low-level. Keep it small so it adds little compute even though it processes all tokens.
5. **Mask token introduced after the encoder, in the decoder; shared learned vector + positional embedding.** Why: it's the placeholder telling the decoder "predict here"; needs positional embedding or it has no location info.
6. **Reconstruction target = pixels (MSE), loss on masked patches only.** Why pixels not tokens (BEiT): pixels are far simpler — no separate dVAE stage, no extra data, no big conv tokenizer overhead; pixel target is competitive (token gain statistically insignificant vs normalized pixels). Loss on masked only (like BERT): computing on all pixels slightly hurts (~0.5%). MSE in pixel space.
7. **Per-patch normalized pixel target (optional, used for best results).** Why: normalize each patch by its own mean/std → enhances local contrast, emphasizes high-frequency structure → improves representation. (PCA target — keep only low-freq coeffs — *hurts*, confirming high-freq components matter.)
8. **Sine-cosine (fixed, 2D) positional embeddings, added to encoder and decoder inputs.** No relative position, no layer scaling (unlike BEiT's code). Simplicity.
9. **Simple implementation via shuffle/gather, no sparse ops.** Tokenize all patches (+pos embed), random-shuffle, drop the last (mask_ratio) fraction → exactly sampling-without-replacement; encode the kept subset; append shared mask tokens; **unshuffle** (argsort of argsort gives the restore index) to realign tokens with targets; add decoder pos embed; decode. Shuffle/unshuffle are cheap; negligible overhead, no custom kernels.
10. **Minimal augmentation (random resized crop + flip).** Why: random masking *is* the regularizer/augmentation — masks differ every iteration, generating fresh samples. Contrastive methods collapse without heavy aug (BYOL −13%, SimCLR −28% with crop-only); MAE works even with center-crop-only. Color jitter hurts.
11. **Class token: an auxiliary dummy token appended to encoder input** (treated as cls for linear probe / fine-tune). Works equally well with average pooling instead.
12. **Hyperparams:** AdamW, base_lr 1.5e-4, linear lr scaling (lr = base_lr × batch/256), batch 4096, β2=0.95 (following iGPT, for stability with large models), wd 0.05, cosine decay, 40-epoch warmup, no drop path / no grad clip / no color jitter in pretrain. Longer schedules keep improving (no linear-probe saturation even at 1600 epochs) — because encoder sees only 25% of patches per epoch.

## Evaluation settings (pre-method, no outcomes)
- Self-supervised pretrain on ImageNet-1K train. Evaluate via (i) end-to-end fine-tuning, (ii) linear probing, (iii) partial fine-tuning (tune last k blocks). Top-1 val accuracy, single 224 crop. Backbone ViT-B/L/H. Transfer: COCO detection/seg (Mask R-CNN + ViT-FPN), ADE20K (UperNet), iNat/Places classification, ImageNet robustness variants (IN-C/A/R/Sketch).

## Canonical code (facebookresearch/mae)
- `models_mae.py`: `MaskedAutoencoderViT`. Key methods: `patchify`/`unpatchify`, `random_masking` (argsort-of-noise shuffle, ids_restore = argsort(ids_shuffle), gather kept, build binary mask 0=keep/1=remove then gather by ids_restore), `forward_encoder` (patch_embed → +pos[1:] → mask → prepend cls+pos[:1] → blocks → norm), `forward_decoder` (decoder_embed → append mask_tokens → unshuffle via gather(ids_restore) → prepend cls → +decoder_pos → blocks → norm → linear pred p*p*3 → drop cls), `forward_loss` (patchify target, optional norm_pix, MSE per patch, mean over masked via (loss*mask).sum()/mask.sum()).
- Defaults: ViT-L enc 1024-d/24/16-heads; decoder 512-d/8 blocks/16 heads; mlp_ratio 4; LN eps 1e-6; fixed 2D sin-cos pos embed. mask_ratio default 0.75. norm_pix_loss optional.
- `engine_pretrain.py`: amp autocast, per-iteration cosine lr, accum_iter, loss = model(samples, mask_ratio).
