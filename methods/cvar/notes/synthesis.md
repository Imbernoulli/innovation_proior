## Convention to use in deliverables
Follow the **task spec + PyPortfolioOpt**: α = confidence level (e.g. 0.95), tail weight 1/(1-α). Loss L(w)=L(w,y) random; samples L_k, k=1..N each prob 1/N. F_α(w,ζ)=ζ + (1/(1-α))·E[(L(w)-ζ)⁺]. (NB: RU2000 calls confidence β, RU2002 calls it α; I use α=confidence to match the task and the code's role of ζ. I must not collide α with RU2002's ζ_α — I'll write ζ for VaR variable, not α.)

## The research question (pain)
Minimize the risk of large losses of a portfolio w over X. The industry-standard risk number is VaR_α = α-quantile of the loss = smallest ζ with P(L≤ζ)≥α. Two fatal flaws for *optimization*:
1. As a function of w, VaR is non-convex and even discontinuous / multi-extremal (Mauser-Rosen, McKay-Keefer) → can't optimize reliably, local≠global.
2. VaR is not subadditive (Artzner) → fails coherence; ignores how bad the tail is *beyond* the quantile (a thin spike and a fat catastrophe past ζ have identical VaR).

## Ancestors / baselines (elaborate, with the gap each leaves)
- **Markowitz mean-variance (1952).** Risk = variance/std of return, σ²=wᵀΣw. Convex QP, tractable. Gap: variance penalizes upside and downside symmetrically; only a coherent tail measure for elliptical/normal returns; real loss distributions have fat tails/discreteness (scenarios), where variance under-weights catastrophe. The *predecessor* risk-measure-in-an-optimization template.
- **VaR / RiskMetrics (1994-96).** VaR_α the regulatory standard (Basel). Parametric (normal) VaR ∝ σ → fine but just variance in disguise. Historical/MC simulation VaR for nonlinear instruments (options). Gap: as an *objective in w* it's non-convex, has multiple local minima, and is blind to tail severity beyond the quantile; not subadditive.
- **Artzner et al. coherent measures (1999).** Axiomatize what a risk measure *should* satisfy: T, S, PH, M. Show quantile-based VaR violates S. Propose "tail conditional expectation" / "worst conditional expectation" as coherent remedies, but these are characterized abstractly (generalized scenarios / sup over a set of probability measures) and are "impractical... can only be calculated in very narrow circumstances" — no general *optimization* recipe in w. Gap: a coherent measure exists in principle but there's no tractable way to minimize it over portfolios.
- **Mean shortfall / expected shortfall / mean excess loss** (Embrechts, insurance lit): E[L | L>VaR]. The right *concept* (averages the tail) but defined *through* VaR — so to minimize it you'd first need VaR(w), which is itself the ill-behaved object. Circular for optimization, and for discrete distributions CVaR⁺=E[L|L>VaR] is itself non-coherent (atom problem).

## The derivation (the heart of reasoning.md)
1. VaR bad as objective: quantile → non-convex in w, multi-extremal; blind beyond ζ. Construct the two-bond subadditivity counterexample analytically (i.i.d. bonds default w.p. p with loss L, choose α with p < 1-α < 1-(1-p)² i.e. between single & no-default tail mass... careful: want 1-α between p and 2p-p²). Single bond: P(loss≥L)=p. If 1-α<p then VaR of single bond at level α... Use the clean version: each bond loss is L w.p. p, 0 w.p. 1-p. Pick α=0.95, p=0.04. Single bond: P(L=loss)=0.04<0.05=1-α, so the 95% quantile of single-bond loss is 0 → VaR=0 (or face-value-ish small). Portfolio of two independent: P(at least one defaults)=1-(0.96)²=0.0784>0.05, so the 95% quantile jumps up to L → VaR(A+B)=L > 0+0 = VaR(A)+VaR(B). Subadditivity violated; "diversification looks like it *adds* risk." This is analytically derivable, not measured.
2. Want the tail-average φ = E[L|L≥VaR] (CVaR). But it's defined through VaR. Wall.
3. Auxiliary-variable trick. Define F_α(w,ζ)=ζ + (1/(1-α))E[(L(w)-ζ)⁺]. Claim: min over ζ of F equals CVaR, and the minimizing ζ is VaR. Motivate: (L-ζ)⁺ is the loss-exceedance over a candidate threshold ζ; (1/(1-α))E[(L-ζ)⁺] over-counts if ζ too low (you're averaging excess of a threshold below VaR over the full 1/(1-α) weight) and the +ζ rewards raising ζ; balance pins ζ at the quantile.
4. **Prove min_ζ F = CVaR.** F_α(w,·) convex (since t↦(t-ζ)⁺ convex in ζ, expectation preserves). Compute right derivative ∂⁺F/∂ζ. With Ψ(ζ)=P(L≤ζ): for ζ'>ζ, [(L-ζ')⁺-(L-ζ)⁺]/(ζ'-ζ) = -1 if L≥ζ', 0 if L≤ζ, in (-1,0) between. E[...]→ -(1-Ψ(ζ)) as ζ'↓ζ. So ∂⁺F/∂ζ = 1 + (1/(1-α))(Ψ(ζ)-1) = (Ψ(ζ)-α)/(1-α). Similarly ∂⁻F/∂ζ=(Ψ(ζ⁻)-α)/(1-α). Convex min where ∂⁻≤0≤∂⁺ ⟺ Ψ(ζ⁻)≤α≤Ψ(ζ) ⟺ ζ is a VaR (the α-quantile). At that ζ*=VaR, plug back: F(w,ζ*)=VaR + (1/(1-α))E[(L-VaR)⁺]. For continuous L, E[(L-VaR)⁺]=∫_{L≥VaR}(L-VaR)p = (1-α)(E[L|L≥VaR]-VaR), so F=VaR+(E[L|L≥VaR]-VaR)=E[L|L≥VaR]=CVaR. ∎ (For discrete: the atom-splitting weighted-average form, Prop 6, gives the same min value — CVaR is the "adjusted" tail mean.)
5. Joint convexity: F_α(w,ζ) jointly convex in (w,ζ) when L(w,y) convex in w (linear L=−Rᵀw is convex). So min over (w,ζ) is convex. And min_w CVaR = min_w min_ζ F = min_{w,ζ} F (Theorem 14): no need to handle the inner VaR separately — minimize jointly. Byproduct: ζ*=VaR (Cor 15).
6. Sample/scenario LP: replace E by empirical mean over N samples. F̃=ζ + (1/(N(1-α)))Σ_k (L_k(w)-ζ)⁺. Epigraph trick: introduce u_k, minimize ζ + (1/(N(1-α)))Σu_k s.t. u_k ≥ L_k(w)-ζ, u_k≥0. With L_k(w)=−R_kᵀw linear, all constraints linear → LP. Land on cvxpy code mirroring EfficientCVaR.
7. Coherence (payoff): F linear-in-w case ⟹ φ sublinear in loss ⟹ subadditive + PH; plus monotone + translation-equivariant ⟹ coherent (Cor 12). Fixes exactly VaR's flaw.

## Design-decision → why
- Why CVaR not VaR: convex+coherent+tail-aware vs non-convex+incoherent+tail-blind. CVaR≥VaR so low CVaR ⟹ low VaR (free bonus).
- Why the +ζ term & 1/(1-α) weight: they're exactly what makes the unconstrained min over ζ land on the quantile and the value equal the tail mean (derived, not chosen).
- Why epigraph u_k≥L_k-ζ, u_k≥0 instead of max(L_k-ζ,0): LP needs linear constraints; (·)⁺ is the pointwise max of two linear functions; at optimum u_k=(L_k-ζ)⁺ because the objective pushes each u_k down to its lower envelope. This is the standard convexification of a max in an epigraph.
- Why minimize jointly over (w,ζ) rather than ζ then w: joint convexity (Cor 11) makes it one convex program; inner min is always attained (Thm 14), so no nested loop.
- Why 1/N weights: empirical/scenario distribution, each sample prob 1/N (or general p_k).
- Sign convention in code: PyPortfolioOpt uses returns R (gains); loss=−R; their ζ var named `_alpha`; constraint R@w+ζ+u≥0 ⟺ u≥(−R@w)−ζ=loss−ζ.
