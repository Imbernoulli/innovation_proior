# Quanta — "Decades-Old Graph Problem Yields to Amateur Mathematician"
Source: https://www.quantamagazine.org/decades-old-graph-problem-yields-to-amateur-mathematician-20180417/ (retrieved this run)

## Motivation / entry point
De Grey (a biologist known for aging research) came to the problem unexpectedly. He found
graph theory through competitive Othello players who shared mathematical interests:
"Occasionally, when I need a rest from my real job, I'll think about math." Over Christmas
he turned to the chromatic-number problem.

## Approach
Began from an established foundation: "De Grey based his graph on a gadget called the Moser
spindle." This 7-vertex, 11-edge configuration had chromatic number four.

## Construction method
Through "a delicate process, and with minimal computer assistance," de Grey combined
multiple copies of the Moser spindle with additional point assemblies. His initial result
was a 20,425-vertex structure that couldn't be 4-colored. Recognizing the need for
simplification, he reduced this to 1,581 vertices and performed computer verification of
its non-four-colorability. ("it's not easy to see this just by looking — it takes a
computer search to verify that four colors really aren't enough.")

## Collaborative refinement
De Grey proposed the minimization challenge to the Polymath project. Collaborators
(Dustin Mixon, Boris Alexeev, Marijn Heule) reduced the vertex count to 826.
